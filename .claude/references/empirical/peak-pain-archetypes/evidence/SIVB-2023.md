## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = reversed)

FY2022 10-K Risk Factors verbatim: "increases in interest rates have caused, and may continue to cause, the value of our investment securities to decline." Investment Securities footnote: HTM **$91.3B carrying value vs $76.2B fair value = $15.1B unrealized loss** as of Dec 31, 2022 (vs ~$1.3B YE2021).

Fed 2022-2023 rate-hike cycle was the proximate cause of *both* legs: (1) collapsed VC-funding → deposit outflows forcing asset sales, and (2) crushed mark-to-market on long-duration HTM/AFS book → forced sales were capital-destroying. The zero-rate + tech-deposit-boom tailwind that drove SVB 2020-2021 ($102B → $189B deposits) **reversed** by Q4 2022. Same macro factor flipped net-positive → net-catastrophic — **reversed**, not "weakening."

Fed post-mortem (April 2023): "Silicon Valley Bank's failure is a textbook case of mismanagement… SVBFG management failed to manage the risks of its liabilities, which were overly concentrated in venture capital industry deposits, and the interest rate risks of its assets."

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = distressed)

FDIC pr23016 (Mar 10, 2023): "Silicon Valley Bank, Santa Clara, California, was closed today by the California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation, which appointed the Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) as receiver." Third-largest US bank failure ever, largest since 2008.

Run mechanics (Fed post-mortem + American Banker): on **March 9, 2023, ~$42 billion in deposits were withdrawn in a single day** (~25% of deposit base). Further ~$100B withdrawal requests queued for Mar 10 morning could not be funded. Cash went from "adequate" Wed Mar 8 morning to "negative cash balance" by Thu Mar 9 evening.

→ At Mar 8-10, liquid runway is effectively **zero days**. **distressed** is the only valid spec value (domain: `>24mo`, `12-24mo`, `<12mo`, `distressed`). FY22 10-K showed $13.8B cash + $26.1B AFS — but the AFS book was the asset *sold at $1.8B loss* Mar 8 to raise cash, and the $15.1B HTM unrealized loss meant HTM could not be liquidated without breaching capital. FY-close cash-on-paper is misleading; Mar-8 lens is correct.

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = yes; founder_insider_stake_direction = decreasing)

Greg Becker: CEO of SVB Financial Group from April 2011 through receivership Mar 10, 2023 (12-year tenure). Not literal founder (SVB founded 1983 by Biggerstaff/Medearis); per case-catalog convention Becker is treated as **founder-equivalent**.

→ **founder_in_place = "yes"** (spec ["yes","departed","replaced-by-competent"]).

**Form 4 (Feb 27, 2023):** Becker sold **12,451 shares for $3.6 million** — exercised options at $1.3M cost, sold same-day for $3.6M proceeds. **9 business days (11 calendar) before failure**, under a Rule 10b5-1 plan filed Jan 26, 2023 — the **plan itself filed only ~30 business days before failure**, inside the period when Q4 2022 unrealized HTM losses were already known internally. CFO Daniel Beck also sold $575K on Feb 27. CNBC: SVB executives sold ~$84M over prior 2 years.

→ **founder_insider_stake_direction = "decreasing"** (spec ["increasing","flat","decreasing","departed"]). Canonical insider-selling-at-peak-pain signal — documented Form-4 share-count delta in operator's beneficial ownership, executed inside the window where private HTM-portfolio info was material. Stronger and more dispositive than NVDA-2008's "flat" reading.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

SVB press release Mar 8, 2023: "SVB sold approximately $21 billion of securities, which will result in an after tax loss of approximately **$1.8 billion** in the first quarter of 2023." FY22 10-K: HTM at $91.3B amortized cost vs **$76.2B fair value = $15.1B unrealized loss** (vs ~$1.3B YE2021) — effective economic NIM was already negative once mark-to-market is recognized. Q4 2022 NIM had begun compressing as deposit costs repriced faster than the long-duration securities book.

→ **margin_trajectory = "deteriorating"** (spec ["improving","stable","deteriorating"]).

Deposit base: peak ~$189B Q1 2022 → $173B YE2022 (-8% over three quarters of VC-funding chill) → continued outflows through Q1 2023. NII was supported only by holding losses unrealized via HTM accounting; once AFS was sold Mar 8, Q1 2023 P&L was guaranteed deeply negative.

→ **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** (spec ["growing","flat","declining","pre-revenue"]).

## F. Operational shock specific to SIVB — the 36-hour run (idiosyncratic, not feature-mapped)

- **Wed Mar 8 after-market**: SVB announces $21B AFS sale at $1.8B realized loss + $2.25B capital raise; General Atlantic backstop $500M.
- **Thu Mar 9**: Stock opens -30%, **closes -60% from $267.83 → $106.04** on volume 38.7M shares (vs ~600K typical) per Polygon. **$42B deposits withdrawn intraday** — coordinated via Slack/Twitter among VC LPs.
- **Fri Mar 10 pre-market**: California DFPI closes bank; FDIC named receiver. Capital raise withdrawn. Equity → zero.
- **Sun Mar 12**: Treasury/Fed/FDIC invoke systemic-risk exception; all depositors made whole; BTFP facility announced.

36-hour event triggered by single asset-sale disclosure colliding with 94%-uninsured concentrated-VC deposit base.
