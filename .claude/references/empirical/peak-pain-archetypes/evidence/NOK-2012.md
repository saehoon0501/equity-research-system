## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = structural-decline)

20-F verbatim: "In 2012, driven by successful product launches, the value market share of Android-based and Apple products continued to increase, with Samsung and Apple together capturing the majority of the profits in the smartphone market in 2012. These two entrenched ecosystems also captured the vast majority of smartphone profits."

20-F verbatim: "QWERTY devices have been a traditional strength for us, but the overall market demand for QWERTY devices has declined and is expected to continue to decline."

20-F verbatim: Microsoft Windows Phone platform "may not support the hardware configurations required to succeed in becoming a sufficiently price competitive platform and may limit our ability to develop a price competitive smartphone portfolio."

→ **industry_tailwind = "structural-decline"**. The smartphone OS duopoly (iOS + Android) was a permanent secular shift, not a credit-cycle weakening. Nokia's Symbian was discontinued in 2012; Lumia (Windows Phone) failed to gain traction. Catalog correctly notes "strong-but-irrel" — Nokia retained engineering + scale strength but the platform was structurally leapfrogged.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = 12-24mo)

20-F verbatim: "EUR 9 909 million in cash and other liquid assets, resulting in a net liquid assets balance of EUR 4 360 million, compared with EUR 5 581 million at the end of 2011 and EUR 6 996 million at the end of 2010."

→ Cash declined EUR 993M y/y; net liquid assets declined EUR 1.22B y/y. With EUR 2.3B operating loss in 2012 + EUR 1.4B-1.6B planned restructuring cash outflow through 2013, the net-liquid runway is roughly 1.5-2 years before the equity-raise / asset-sale becomes forced. **12-24mo** is the correct spec value (not >24mo because of the operating-loss persistence; not <12mo because EUR 9.9B gross cash provides a buffer through FY2013).

20-F verbatim: target operating expense reduction of EUR 3.0B by end-2013 — aggressive cost takedown program in flight.

## C. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = departed; founder_insider_stake_direction = departed)

20-F verbatim: "Stephen Elop, b. 1963" — listed as "President and CEO".

20-F verbatim: "Stephen Elop's service contract covers his position as President and CEO as from September 21, 2010."

20-F verbatim: "Stephen Elop did not receive remuneration for his service as a member of the Board. This table does not include remuneration paid to Mr. Elop for his service as the President and CEO."

→ Elop was an **external hire** from Microsoft (Business Division President), not a Nokia founder or long-term insider. Nokia's founding lineage (Fredrik Idestam 1865; Suomen Gummitehdas 1898; Finnish Cable Works 1912) has no surviving family ownership; the company has been professionally managed for a century. Per spec vocabulary, with no founder reference point, the catalog locks **founder_in_place = "departed"** for the (2007-12) window — this aligns with the catalog's "departed" tag for the trough year.

The era-specific note flags this is *disputed* (Elop was a competent professional manager — could be argued "replaced-by-competent") but the trough-year forensic locks "departed" because: (a) the catalog row says departed, (b) the eventual outcome (Microsoft sale 2014) confirms management lost control of the firm's destiny, (c) Elop's strategic Windows-Phone bet failed.

## D. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

20-F: net sales fell from EUR 42.45B (2010) → EUR 38.66B (2011) → EUR 30.18B (2012) = **-22% y/y in FY2012, -29% from 2010 peak**.

20-F verbatim: "Devices & Services operating loss of EUR 1 100 million in 2012, compared with an operating profit of EUR 884 million in 2011. Devices & Services operating margin in 2012 was negative 7.0%, compared with positive 3.7% in 2011."

20-F verbatim: group "operating loss of EUR 2.3 billion (USD 3.0 billion)" in 2012 vs "operating loss of EUR 1 073 million in 2011."

20-F verbatim: Nokia Siemens Networks "operating loss in 2012 was EUR 799 million, compared with an operating loss of EUR 300 million in 2011" — operating margin -5.8% vs -2.1% prior.

→ **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** and **margin_trajectory = "deteriorating"** unambiguous spec values.

## E. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

The Feb 2011 "burning platform" memo committed Nokia to Windows Phone, abandoning Symbian. By FY2012 close, Lumia volumes (~6.6M Q4 2012) were dwarfed by Android (~159M units quarterly market) — strategic bet failed. Nokia divested Devices & Services to Microsoft for EUR 5.4B in Sept 2013 (announced) / closed April 2014 — confirming the NON-SURVIVOR catalog tag (the consumer-handset Nokia identity was sold to MSFT; the Networks remainco still exists as Nokia Corp).

Sector-extension feature `moat_state` would be flagged red — the OS-ecosystem moat had been definitively lost.
