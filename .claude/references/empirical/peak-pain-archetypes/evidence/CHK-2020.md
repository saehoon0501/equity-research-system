## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = structural-decline)

10-K verbatim (FY19): "natural gas prices ranged from a high of $6.15 per mmbtu to a low of $1.64 per mmbtu. As of February 19, 2020, the NYMEX WTI oil price was $53.29 per bbl and the NYMEX Henry Hub natural gas price was $1.99 per mmbtu."

→ Henry Hub $1.99/mmbtu vs CHK's hedge book floor of $1.80/mcf — minimal protection against further downside. Natural-gas glut from US shale oversupply structurally depressed prices through 2019; OPEC-Russia price war + COVID demand crash (Q1 2020) compounded.

For shale E&P names burdened by 2010-2014 vintage debt at sub-$3 gas, the shale-gas thesis is **structural-decline** — supply abundance + capital flight from sector. Distinct from cyclical "weakening" (which would imply mean-reversion).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = distressed)

Q3 2019 10-Q verbatim (Nov 2019, cited in WSJ/CNN): "If continued depressed prices persist, combined with the scheduled reductions in the leverage ratio covenant, our ability to comply with the leverage ratio covenant during the next 12 months will be adversely affected which raises substantial doubt about our ability to continue as a going concern."

10-K FY19 verbatim: "$8.916 billion in principal amount of debt outstanding (including $301 million of current maturities)."

The Q4 2019 10-K removed the going-concern language after refinancing actions (debt exchanges, covenant relief) — but $8.9B debt vs sub-$2/mmbtu gas + COVID Q1 2020 demand crash → distressed. Spec value **distressed** (single canonical from ["dependent on counterparty","distressed",...]).

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

WildHorse Resource Development acquired Feb 2019 ($381M cash + $1.4B assumed debt) — added oil-weighted Eagle Ford asset to dilute gas exposure but layered more debt at peak cycle. McClendon's pre-2013 build-out left $9B+ debt that successive CEOs (Lawler 2013-2021) could not fully service. June 28, 2020 Ch11 filing in S.D. Texas. Emerged Feb 2021 post-restructuring; later merged with Southwestern Energy (2024) → Expand Energy (2024 rebrand).

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = departed)

External record: Aubrey McClendon co-founded Chesapeake with Tom Ward in 1989. McClendon was forced out April 1, 2013, after disclosure of personal stakes in CHK wells used as $1.1B loan collateral (SEC investigation). Doug Lawler (ex-Anadarko) named CEO May 2013. McClendon died in single-vehicle collision March 2, 2016, the day after federal indictment for bid-rigging.

10-K verbatim Lawler bio: "Lawler served in multiple engineering and leadership positions at Anadarko Petroleum Corporation. His positions at Anadarko included Senior Vice President, International and Deepwater Operations… Lawler began his career with Kerr-McGee Corporation in 1988 and joined Anadarko following its acquisition of Kerr-McGee in 2006."

→ **founder_in_place = "departed"** (single canonical spec value from ["yes","departed","replaced-by-competent"]; founder McClendon left April 2013, died 2016 — he is NOT in place. Lawler is a replacement-CEO but he is not the founder, so "replaced-by-competent" only applies if we credit the operator-CEO logic; per sector note we use "departed").

Stake direction: Lawler open-market purchase ~$101k late 2019 (per Form 4) is a CEO buy, not a founder stake change. With founder gone, **founder_insider_stake_direction = "departed"** at the spec level.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

Q3 2019 results (per Billings Gazette / company release): "Chesapeake reported a third quarter net loss of $61 million" with "Revenue slipped to $2 billion from $2.4 billion over the same time" — -17% y/y revenue.

10-K FY19 verbatim: Sales of natural gas and NGLs dropped sharply on weighted-avg gas price falling from ~$2.76/mcf realized (FY18) to lower realized FY19. Hedge book bought-put floor $2.05/mcf with sold-put limit $1.80/mcf provided minimal protection.

→ revenue_trajectory **declining** (single canonical from ["growing","flat","declining","pre-revenue"]); margin_trajectory **deteriorating** (price-deck collapse hit pre-hedge realized prices in a high-fixed-cost E&P model).

## F. Operational shock specific to CHK — debt-trap legacy + COVID killshot

Pre-2013 McClendon era loaded balance sheet with $9B+ debt at peak cycle. Lawler executed continuous debt-paydown (asset sales, exchanges) but commodity collapse 2014-16 + 2019 gas crash + March 2020 OPEC-Russia + COVID demand crash combined to overwhelm. Ch11 filed June 28, 2020 (oil at ~$40, Henry Hub ~$1.50). Emerged Feb 2021.
