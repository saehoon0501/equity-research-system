## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = reversed)

10-K verbatim: "the average daily WTI oil price fell from $57.03/Bbl in 2019 to $39.40/Bbl in 2020 or 31 percent. We expect that the oil supply and demand balance and consequently oil prices in the near-term will continue to be influenced by [global demand recovery uncertainty and OPEC+ supply discipline]."

10-K verbatim: "If there is a worsening of the macroeconomic conditions and if such worsened condition is expected to or does persist for a prolonged period of time, Occidental's oil and gas properties may be subject to further testing for impairment, which… [resulted in $9.2 billion impairment]."

→ Triple shock: (1) March 6, 2020 Saudi-Russia OPEC+ split; (2) COVID lockdown demand crash April 2020 (WTI futures briefly negative); (3) high financial leverage from Anadarko ($10B preferred + ~$30B+ assumed debt). Tailwind **reversed** in spec terms — cyclical oil thesis inverted; not "structural-decline" because oil demand recovered 2021+.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = distressed)

10-K verbatim: "$8.2 billion of after-tax net proceeds from significant asset divestitures."

10-K verbatim: "$7.0 billion in senior unsecured notes (the Senior Notes Offerings) during 2020 to extend certain debt maturities in 2021-2023 to 2025-2031" — distress-driven liability-management.

10-K verbatim: "dividend rate is $0.04 per share" (quarterly) — slashed from prior $0.79/quarter ($3.16 annual) in March 2020 by ~95%.

10-K verbatim: "$2.6 billion from a range of $5.2 billion to $5.4 billion, a midpoint reduction of approximately 50%" (capital-spending cut) and "$1.5 billion of overhead savings and over $900 million in operating cost savings, of which a majority is expected to remain permanent."

→ Crash-protocol cuts to capex + opex + dividend = textbook "distressed" runway management. With $40B+ debt at FY20 close and WTI $39, OXY would have been Ch11-vulnerable absent (a) Anadarko divestiture cash + (b) Buffett $10B preferred (8% coupon). Spec value **distressed** (single canonical from spec domain).

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

Anadarko acquisition closed Aug 8, 2019 — Hollub outbid Chevron in dramatic 90-min trip to Omaha to secure Buffett $10B financing in exchange for 8% perpetual preferred + 80M warrants struck $62.50. Carl Icahn proxy fight followed. Permian + DJ Basin scale economics + low-carbon-ventures (DAC/CCS) repositioning anchored the recovery thesis post-trough.

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = replaced-by-competent; founder_insider_stake_direction = increasing)

10-K verbatim Hollub bio: "Vicki Hollub | President and Chief Executive Officer | 61."

OXY incorporated 1920 — no living founder; Hollub became CEO April 2016 succeeding Stephen Chazen (interim 2016 then Hollub permanent). Hollub stayed through the entire trough — first female CEO of a major US oil company, drove Anadarko bid, negotiated Buffett rescue.

→ **founder_in_place = "replaced-by-competent"** per sector-note instruction (no founder; Hollub is the operating-insider-CEO who stewarded through trough; not "yes" because she is not a founder; not "departed" because she stayed). Stake direction: Buffett/Berkshire **$10B preferred** April-August 2019 + warrants (effectively "Buffett rescue" rated as insider-aligned outside-capital). Hollub made Form-4 open-market buys post-trough including 14,191 shares March 2022. At FY20 close, Buffett's preferred is the dominant signal: outside insider committed at top of cycle and stayed; spec value **increasing** (single canonical from ["increasing","flat","decreasing","departed"]).

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "Net sales 17,809" (FY20, $M) vs ~$20.4B FY19 (full-year prior incl. partial Anadarko) — y/y decline despite full-year Anadarko volumes. Primary driver = realized oil-price collapse.

10-K verbatim: "lower realized oil prices in the oil and gas segment despite higher volumes as a result of a full year of production from properties added in the Acquisition and net gains on the three-way oil collars."

→ revenue_trajectory **declining** (single canonical spec value); margin_trajectory **deteriorating** with $9.2B+ impairment charges signaling negative operating leverage at the price-deck.

## F. Operational shock specific to OXY — Anadarko leverage + COVID killshot + Buffett lifeline

OXY acquired Anadarko at $76 oil ($55B deal closed Aug 2019). 7 months later WTI fell to negative on April 20, 2020. Hollub executed: dividend cut Mar 2020, capex cut 50%, $8.2B divestitures (Western Midstream, African assets), $7B debt-maturity extension via Senior Notes Offerings. Buffett $10B preferred (8% coupon, redeemable) + 80M warrants @ $62.50 stayed in place through trough. Stock dropped from peak ~$87 (April 2019) to trough ~$11 (Oct 2020), -87%. SURVIVOR outcome.
