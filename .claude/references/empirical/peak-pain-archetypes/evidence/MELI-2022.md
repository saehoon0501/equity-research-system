## A. Industry shock & operational stress (industry_tailwind = weakening)

The 2022 trough was driven by external macro shock: Fed rate-hike cycle (2022 +425bps), EM capital outflows, Argentine peso officially devalued 70%+ (parallel/blue-rate worse), Brazilian real volatility, regional inflation 50%+ in Argentina. MELI's operational franchise (LatAm e-commerce + fintech) continued to grow underlying — the multiple compressed from rate shock, not from operational decline.

10-K context: macro pressures ("inflation, currency devaluation, foreign exchange controls") flagged across LatAm operations. Tailwind weakening (cyclical) — NOT reversed (e-comm penetration in LatAm structurally still ~10-15%, far below US/EU); NOT structural-decline (post-COVID e-commerce stickiness held).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position (cash_runway = >24mo)

FY22 cash flow from operating activities = $2.94B (Macrotrends/Yahoo). Income from operations >$1B (first time crossing in full year). MELI was profitable, free-cash-flow generative, self-funding — runway constraint is irrelevant to a cash-generating business. Operating leverage from fintech (Mercado Pago) + 1P logistics (Mercado Envios) drove sustainable cash generation. Long-term debt manageable; ample committed liquidity; no covenant pressure.

→ `cash_runway = ">24mo"` unambiguous (profitable cash-generator).

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

FY22 milestones per Q4 2022 release: "processed more than $100 billion in payments, booked over $10 billion in net revenues, shipped more than 1 billion items through Mercado Envios, and surpassed $1 billion of income from operations." Mercado Pago TPV growth >70% FX-neutral; credit portfolio expansion. Q4 2022 net revenues $3.0B +56.5% y/y FX-neutral, operating income $349M (11.6% margin).

## D. Founder/insider behavior (founder_in_place = yes; founder_insider_stake_direction = flat)

Marcos Galperin co-founded Mercado Libre at Stanford GSB in 1999, served as CEO through end of FY22 (and beyond, until 2026 transition to Executive Chairman). 10-K identifies him as founder, CEO, Chairman.

Beneficial ownership: Galperin Trust held ~7-8% during FY22 (later filings note ~7% as of late-2024). The trust is irrevocable under New Zealand law (2012 estate planning structure). No documented Form-4 disposition during the trough window. → `founder_in_place = "yes"`; `founder_insider_stake_direction = "flat"`.

Catalog row "yes (Galperin)" — confirms founder-aligned through trough.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory (margin_trajectory = improving; revenue_trajectory = growing)

FY22 net revenues ~$10.54B vs FY21 $7.07B = +49% USD reported, +56% FX-neutral (Q4 ER). Gross profit ~$5.20B FY22 vs ~$3.00B FY21 (gross margin ~49% FY22 vs ~43% FY21 = +6pp). Operating income >$1.0B FY22 (~10% margin) vs $437M FY21 (~6%) = +4pp operating-margin expansion. First full year of operating profit >$1B.

→ `revenue_trajectory = "growing"`; `margin_trajectory = "improving"`. The trough was a stock-price/multiple event, NOT an operational deterioration. This makes MELI a peak-pain archetype where features at trough year are healthy despite -70% drawdown.

## F. Operational shock specific to MELI — EM macro (idiosyncratic)

The MELI 2022 drawdown was driven by:
1. Argentine peso devaluation 70%+ official (worse blue-rate); Argentina hyperinflation 95% YoY end-2022.
2. Fed rate-hike cycle compressing duration / EM tech multiples globally.
3. LatAm sovereign risk premia widening.
4. Sector rotation out of high-growth tech.

These were exogenous macro/multiple compression — operational metrics (revenue, margins, TPV, units) continued to expand. Catalog descriptor "contained" reflects survivor outcome with intact franchise.
