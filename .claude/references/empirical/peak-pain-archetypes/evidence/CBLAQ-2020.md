## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = structural-decline)

10-K verbatim Risk Factors: "Sustained adverse pressure on the results of department stores and other national retailers may have a similarly sustained adverse impact upon our own results. Certain department stores and other national retailers have experienced [pressure from internet and other forms of pressure]…"

10-K verbatim re: anchor cascade: "corporate merger or consolidation activity among department stores and other national retailers typically results in the closure of duplicate or geographically overlapping store locations. If a department store or large nationally recognized tenant were to close…"

CBL's portfolio is concentrated in **B/C-tier markets** (top-5 markets per 10-K: St. Louis MO, Chattanooga TN, Wausau WI, etc. — secondary metros, not gateway). Sales-per-sqft well below A-mall peers. Multi-year occupancy and rent-PSF decline pre-COVID. Sears 2018-19 bankruptcy + JCPenney May-2020 Ch11 + Bon-Ton 2018 liquidation each removed anchors triggering co-tenancy clauses. This is canonical **structural-decline** — the B-mall format itself is in secular run-off; COVID merely accelerated the timeline. NOT `weakening` (not cyclical) and NOT `reversed` (no thesis of recovery — the format is dying).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = distressed)

10-K verbatim: "any inability to obtain sufficient financing (including construction financing, permanent debt, unsecured notes issuances, lines of credit and term loans), or the inability to obtain such financing on commercially favorable terms, to fund repayment of maturing loans…"

10-K verbatim Risk Factors: "Our Properties may be subject to impairment charges, which could impact our compliance with certain debt covenants and could otherwise adversely affect our financial results." Mortgage-and-other-indebtedness disclosure shows large secured-property + unsecured-notes layers maturing 2021-2024.

10-K dividend-suspension reference (Risk Factors): "changes in our dividend policy (including, without limitation, our current susp[ension])…" CBL had suspended common-stock dividend Sept 2019 — pre-COVID — to preserve cash, indicating pre-COVID distress. Combined with subsequent Aug-18-2020 forbearance + Nov-1-2020 voluntary Ch11 petition, the FY2019 fiscal-close lens correctly classifies as **distressed** under spec vocabulary (going-concern not yet flagged in the 10-K itself but the trajectory and the ~9 months later Ch11 confirm).

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

CBL's Plan of Reorganization: pre-petition unsecured noteholders (~$1.4B) converted to ~89% of new equity; existing common+preferred wiped (~10% of new equity reserved for legacy holders subject to massive dilution; effectively $0). DIP financing $50M provided by ad-hoc bondholder group. Plan confirmed Aug 11, 2021; emerged Nov 1, 2021 with reorganized capital structure. Stephen Lebovitz remained CEO post-emergence — operationally retained but founder-family economic interest crushed.

This is the canonical NON-SURVIVOR-via-Ch11 profile: the operating entity survived but the equity did not.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim Selected Financial Data: "Total revenues $ 768,696 $ 858,557 $ 927,252 $ 1,028,257" (FY19 / FY18 / FY17 / FY16, in $thousands). Net loss FY19 $131.7M (vs $99.2M FY18) — 33% wider y/y.

→ Revenue -10.5% y/y FY19 vs FY18; -25% over 3 years from FY16 peak. This is a multi-year structural revenue compression — different signal-character from cyclical drops (NVDA, SPG). **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** unambiguous.

→ **margin_trajectory = "deteriorating"** — net loss widening, FFO compressing, impairment charges recurring across same-center NOI. The 10-K narrative on co-tenancy clauses + anchor closures + recurring impairments confirms the trajectory direction.

## F. Operational shock specific to CBLAQ — anchor cascade + COVID (idiosyncratic)

The Sears bankruptcy (Oct 2018), Bon-Ton liquidation (Apr 2018), JCPenney Ch11 (May 2020) collectively removed dozens of CBL anchors triggering co-tenancy rent-reduction clauses. 10-K verbatim re: co-tenancy: "The leases for a number of the tenants in our properties have co-tenancy clauses that allow those tenants to pay reduced [rent]." This is a structural-cascade pattern — a single anchor closure sets off a chain reaction of in-line tenant rent-reductions or terminations, driving NOI down without offsetting cost reductions. Combined with COVID-2020 mall closures, the path to Ch11 was deterministic by mid-2020.
