## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

10-K verbatim: "due to certain restrictive governmental orders placed on us, our domestic portfolio lost approximately 13,500 shopping days during the year. As of October 7, 2020, all of our domestic properties and certain of our retailer investments had reopened, but we do not have certainty that additional closures in the [future]…"

10-K verbatim Risk Factors: "The existence of competitive alternatives, accelerated by the impact of COVID-19, could have a material adverse effect on our ability to lease space and on the level of rents we can obtain." Plus: "[stay-at-home orders] may, particularly in certain market segments, accelerate the long-term penetration of pure online retail."

The shock is **cyclical-COVID**, not secular. The same 10-K acknowledges e-commerce pressure but Simon's portfolio is class-A productivity-tier (sales/sqft consistently top-decile). A-mall foot-traffic snapped back >90% in 2021; bankruptcy ratio of department-store anchors did NOT cascade to Simon's prime-mall NOI. This is canonical **weakening** (NOT `structural-decline` — that classification belongs to B/C-mall operators like CBL; A-mall fundamentals re-asserted post-COVID).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = 12-24mo)

10-K verbatim: "Our balance of cash and cash equivalents increased $342.2 million during 2020 to $1.0 billion as of December 31, 2020 as further discussed below. On December 31, 2020, we had an aggregate available borrowing capacity of approximately…"

10-K verbatim re: credit ratings: "We strive to maintain investment grade ratings at all times for various business reasons, including their effect on our ability to access attractive capital." Simon retained A-/A3 grade through COVID — accessed bond market in 2020 to extend liquidity.

Strategic actions: drew on revolver early in COVID (Mar-2020) to lock liquidity; cut common dividend ~38% (from $2.10 → $1.30 quarterly); preferred dividends maintained. Investment-grade access + ~$8B+ aggregate liquidity (cash + undrawn revolver) → spec value **12-24mo** is intentionally conservative (operator-instruction explicitly chose this not `>24mo`); reflects the COVID-overhang + dividend-cut + revolver-draw stress signals at FY2020 close even though absolute capacity was ample.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "Total revenue 4,607,503 5,755,189 5,645,288" (FY20 / FY19 / FY18, in $thousands).

→ Revenue -19.9% y/y FY20 vs FY19. Property-level NOI compressed from rent abatements + ~13,500 lost shopping-days + tenant-cotenancy clause activation. SG&A and depreciation are largely sticky for REITs, so the operating margin compression is mechanical.

→ **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** (-19.9% unambiguous).
→ **margin_trajectory = "deteriorating"** (FFO-per-share $11.13 FY20 vs $12.04 FY19; not a structural collapse but unambiguous deterioration). NOT `stable` (-19.9% revenue + dividend cut = clear deterioration signal).

This is the survivor-archetype profile: the SAME industry shock produces opposite outcomes (SPG SURVIVOR vs CBL NON-SURVIVOR) based on portfolio quality + balance-sheet quality + family control.
