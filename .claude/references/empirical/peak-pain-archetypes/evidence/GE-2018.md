## A. Industry shock & operational stress (industry_tailwind = weakening)

10-K verbatim: "ability to improve the operations and execution of our Power business, and the continued strength of our Aviation business" — this is the canonical bifurcation: Power gas-turbine market in structural-supply glut + renewable-energy displacement, while Aviation enjoyed peak engine-shipment cycle (1,100+ CFM LEAP engines shipped 2018, segment profit +20%). 10-K verbatim: "charge related to an oxidation issue within the HA and 9FB Stage 1 turbine blades, resulting in increased warranty and maintenance reserves." The mixed picture maps to **weakening** rather than reversed: the GE-conglomerate operating environment had clearly deteriorated, but the secular Aviation cycle was intact and ultimately drove the survivor outcome.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position (cash_runway = distressed)

10-K verbatim: "GE's primary sources of liquidity consist of cash and cash equivalents, free cash flows from our operating businesses, proceeds from announced dispositions, and short-term borrowing facilities."

10-K verbatim: "On October 30, 2018 we announced plans to reduce our quarterly dividend from $0.12 cents to $0.01 cent per share beginning with the dividend declared in December 2018, which was paid on January 25, 2019. This change will allow us to retain approximately $4 billion of cash per year compared to the prior payout level."

10-K verbatim: "GE Capital does not plan to issue any incremental GE Capital senior unsecured term debt until 2021."

GE Industrial cash flow from operations $2.3B in 2018 vs $11.0B in 2017 (primarily $6.0B Pension contributions in 2018); GE did not receive a common dividend distribution from GE Capital in 2018, "and it does not expect to receive such dividend distributions from GE Capital for the foreseeable future." Combined with $22B Power goodwill impairment, expanding SEC accounting investigation, and credit-rating downgrades — this was a balance-sheet-distress moment that required dividend cut + asset divestitures to fund operations. Maps to **distressed** under spec domain (consistent with case-brief: "distressed — $23.6B short-term debt vs $7B cash, but Aviation cash flow saved it").

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

Aviation Long-Term Service Agreements (LTSAs) — 10-K verbatim: "with multiple-year terms, to provide specified services for products in our Power, Renewable Energy Aviation, Oil & Gas and Transportation installed base — for example, monitoring, maintenance, service and spare parts." Aviation LTSAs generated multi-decade-visibility cash flow that bridged GE through 2019–22 Power workout; this is the survivor mechanism. Sector-extension `service-revenue_durability` territory; for universal-core extraction, use only the FY18 distress framing.

## D. Founder/insider behavior (founder_in_place = replaced-by-competent; founder_insider_stake_direction = increasing)

10-K verbatim: "three new directors, H. Lawrence Culp, Jr., Thomas W. Horton and Leslie F. Seidman" — board refresh in 2018, with Culp added April 2018 as director, then installed as CEO October 1, 2018, replacing John Flannery (who had himself replaced Jeff Immelt in 2017). 10-K signed "/s/ H. Lawrence Culp, Jr." — Culp is the trough-year operator.

Culp's prior track record at Danaher (CEO 2000–2014, ~$22B → $48B revenue, exemplary capital-allocation reputation) — qualifies as **replaced-by-competent** under spec domain (clear external upgrade with proven turnaround/operating credentials).

→ **founder_in_place = "replaced-by-competent"** (single canonical spec value).

Stake direction: Form 4 filing Nov 5, 2018 — Culp purchased 225,000 GE shares at average ~$9.73 = ~$2.19M open-market buy as new CEO (case-brief stipulates "$2.5M" — primary-source Form 4 filings register $2.2M; using primary-source figure). This is a discretionary, post-appointment, open-market purchase — unambiguous stake-build event.

→ **founder_insider_stake_direction = "increasing"** (single canonical spec value; documented Form 4 share-count delta).

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "Continuing earnings per share was $(2.43) primarily due to non-cash after-tax impairment charges of $22.4 billion recorded in the second half of 2018 related to goodwill in our Power" business.

10-K verbatim: "$22.0 billion of goodwill impairments in our Power business and a $0.1 billion goodwill impairment in our Renewable business in 2018 compared to a $1.2 billion charge for the impairment of Power" goodwill in 2017.

→ Power-segment margin and consolidated GE Industrial margins collapsed in 2018 from the impairment + warranty-reserve combination + slow-moving inventory charges. **Deteriorating** — single unambiguous spec value.

Revenue: GE Industrial revenue declined modestly y/y, with Power-segment organic revenue notably down; combined with the $1.7B held-for-sale loss program, revenue trajectory at the FY18 close is **declining**.

→ **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** (single canonical spec value).

## F. Operational specifics — Power impairment + SEC investigation

10-K verbatim notes the SEC investigation expanding in scope (Power + insurance reserves + revenue-recognition). $22B Power goodwill impairment + ongoing accounting-investigation overhang were the two structural pressures that forced the dividend cut + Culp appointment. Maps to `margin_trajectory = deteriorating` magnitude. The Aviation LTSA cash-flow durability is what separates GE-2018 SURVIVOR outcome from a hypothetical NON-SURVIVOR path.
