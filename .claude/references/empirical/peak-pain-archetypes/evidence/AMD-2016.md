## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

10-K Item 1A verbatim: "The PC market continues to experience a decline driven by, among other factors, the adoption of smaller form factors, increased competition and changes in replacement cycles."

10-K Item 1A verbatim: "Consumer demand has continued to shift from desktop PCs and notebooks to smaller form factor devices."

10-K Item 1A verbatim: "If the markets for our products grow more slowly than anticipated or if demand for our products in these markets does not materialize as we expect, our business could be materially adversely affected."

PC end-market in cyclical/transitional decline, not yet secular death (semis broadly intact; AMD's semi-custom console wins with Sony/Microsoft were a partial offset). Spec value **weakening** — not "structural-decline" because the broader silicon TAM (graphics, datacenter, console) was intact and AMD's Zen roadmap was a credible path back into x86 server.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = 12-24mo)

10-K verbatim: "Our cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities as of December 26, 2015 were $785 million compared to $1.0 billion as of December 27, 2014."

10-K verbatim: "Total debt as of December 26, 2015 was $2.26 billion, compared to $2.2 billion as [of December 27, 2014]."

10-K verbatim: "AMD's cash and cash equivalents and marketable securities balances, the savings from its restructuring plans and the secured revolving line of credit (Secured Revolving Line of Credit) will be sufficient to fund AMD's operations including capital expenditures over the next 12 months."

Cash used in operating activities in FY15 = $226M; FY15 net loss = $660M (includes non-cash charges). $785M cash vs $226M operating cash burn → ~3 years of runway at FY15 burn pace, but the high debt load ($2.26B) and absence of bank covenants headroom puts AMD in **12-24mo** comfort zone (matches catalog row "12-24mo"; the 10-K's explicit "sufficient over the next 12 months" language is the canonical anchor).

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

Zen architecture in development (taped out 2015, launched as Ryzen Mar 2017); Polaris GPU shipping mid-2016; semi-custom SoCs for Sony PS4/Microsoft Xbox One generating cash. The thesis recovery vector was R&D continuity through the trough.

10-K verbatim: "Our product roadmap includes a new x86 processor core codenamed Zen to help drive our re-entry into high-performance and server computing."

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = replaced-by-competent; founder_insider_stake_direction = flat)

AMD founder Jerry Sanders departed CEO role in 2002; Rory Read (CEO 2011–Oct 2014) was replaced by Dr. Lisa T. Su effective Oct 8, 2014 — twelve months ahead of the FY15 trough. AMD press release 2014-10-08: "AMD today announced that its Board of Directors has appointed Dr. Lisa Su as president and chief executive officer." Su (PhD EE MIT; ex-IBM SVP of Networking & Multimedia; ex-Freescale CTO) is the canonical "competent operator" replacement profile (Iacocca/Mulally archetype).

10-K signature page: "/s/ LISA T. SU President, Chief Executive Officer (Principal Executive Officer)".

→ **founder_in_place = "replaced-by-competent"** (single canonical value from spec domain ["yes","departed","replaced-by-competent"]).

Stake direction: no SEC Form-4 open-market purchase events documented in FY15. CEO equity comp issued via RSUs/options (10-K exhibits 10.78–10.80 — "SVP Option Agreement", "SVP RSU Agreement", "SVP PRSU Agreement"). Option grants are not stake-increases per the canary rule.

→ **founder_insider_stake_direction = "flat"** (single canonical value from spec domain ["increasing","flat","decreasing","departed"]).

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "Net revenue $3,991 [2015] $5,506 [2014] $5,299 [2013] $5,422 [2012] $6,568 [2011]." → FY15 revenue **-27.5% y/y**.

10-K verbatim: "Gross margin 1,080 [2015] 1,839 [2014] 1,978 [2013] … Gross margin percentage 27% 33% 37%."

10-K verbatim: "Gross margin, as a percentage of net revenue for 2015, was 27% compared to 33% in 2014."

→ Two consecutive years of GM compression (37% → 33% → 27%) plus a -27.5% revenue collapse. **Deteriorating** GM and **declining** revenue are both unambiguous spec values.

10-K verbatim: "Loss from continuing operations $(660) [2015] $(403) [2014] $(83) [2013]."

## F. Operational restructuring (idiosyncratic context)

10-K verbatim: "The 2015 Restructuring Plan provides for a workforce reduction of approximately 5% and includes organizational actions such as outsourcing certain IT services and application development."

10-K Item 1A verbatim: "AMD's ability to successfully complete its restructuring plan, its transformation initiatives and any future restructuring actions could adversely affect us."

Plus joint-venture spinout of back-end packaging/test (announced 2015 with Nantong Fujitsu) — AMD shedding asset-heavy operations to extend runway. Goodwill impairment charge of $233M FY15 reflected impairment of legacy operations.
