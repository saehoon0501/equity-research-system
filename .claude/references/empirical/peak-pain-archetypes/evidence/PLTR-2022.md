## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

The 2022 SaaS valuation reset was driven by Fed-rate normalization (zero-rate → ~4.25% by year-end), not demand collapse. PLTR's underlying revenue grew 24% — the equity drawdown was **multiple compression**, not operating deterioration. This is **weakening** (not "structural-decline"): government revenue stayed sticky (56% of FY2022 revenue), commercial growth moderated but did not reverse, and the secular AI/data-platform thesis re-asserted in 2023 (AIP launch). The case-brief tailwind reading "weakening" reflects: (a) commercial customer-concentration fears, (b) post-Ukraine geopolitical turbulence affecting EU sales, (c) compounding macro multiple compression.

NOT "reversed" — the AI-platform thesis became *stronger* in 2023, validating the FY2022 holding decision.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = >24mo)

10-K verbatim: "We had positive cash flow from operations for the year ended December 31, 2022 and had $2.6 billion in cash and cash equivalents available as of December 31, 2022. We believe that cash flows generated from operations, cash, cash equivalents, available funds and access to financing sources, including our credit facility, will be sufficient to meet our anticipated operating cash needs for at least the next twelve months."

10-K verbatim: "we had no outstanding debt balances and additional available and undrawn revolving and DDTL commitments of $950.0 million under our credit agreement to fund working capital."

→ **cash_runway = ">24mo"**. $2.6B cash + zero debt + positive operating cash flow + $950M undrawn credit = the strongest possible balance sheet posture. FCF-positive flip in 2022 means the burn-rate denominator is no longer the binding constraint.
