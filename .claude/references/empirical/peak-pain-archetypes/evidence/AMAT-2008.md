## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

10-K verbatim: "Credit markets, disruption in the financial markets, and global economic downturn experienced over the last several quarters contributed to significant slowdowns in the industries in which Applied operates, which slowdowns may persist or worsen if these economic conditions continue."

10-K verbatim: "Applied… anticipates that total net sales will increase more than 30 percent in fiscal 2010 as compared to fiscal 2009." (Forward outlook strong but FY09 itself was the trough.)

→ Semicap WFE spending is one of the most cyclical sub-industries in tech. The GFC freeze of fab capex was severe (-38% AMAT revenue) but cyclical. Spec value **weakening** (cyclical), not "structural-decline" (semis demand secularly intact) and not "intact" (the trough was real). Catalog row "strong" is the post-trough recovery framing — at the trough lens, weakening is correct per spec rule.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = >24mo)

10-K verbatim: "Cash, cash equivalents and investments decreased to $3.3 billion at October 25, 2009 from $3.5 billion at October 26, 2008, due primarily to a decrease in cash generated from operating activities."

10-K verbatim: "Applied has not undertaken any significant external financing activities for several years."

10-K verbatim: "Cash and cash equivalents $1,577 [FY09]." (Plus short-term and long-term investments to $3.3B total.)

→ AMAT entered the trough with a fortress balance sheet. Only $0.2B y/y cash decline despite a $305M net loss reflects modest working-capital release and disciplined capex. With no significant debt, runway is effectively unconstrained. **>24mo** unambiguous. Matches catalog row.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

AMAT held R&D investment relatively steady through trough; pursued solar/SunFab JV diversification (subsequently unwound). Continued share repurchases. Long-term semicap consolidation thesis intact (eventually played out via Lam, ASML duopoly + AMAT EPI/dep/etch leadership in 14nm+ ramps).

10-K verbatim: "Applied… continues to invest in research, development and engineering to develop the technology and products that will… maintain or improve our competitive position when the industry recovers."

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = departed; founder_insider_stake_direction = flat)

Original founder Michael A. McNeilly (1967) departed AMAT in the 1970s after leadership disputes. James C. Morgan (the transformative CEO 1976–2003, often called "the Father of Modern Applied") retired and Splinter joined.

10-K Officers section verbatim: "Splinter, age 59, was named President, Chief Executive Officer and a member of Applied's Board of Directors upon joining Applied in April 2003, and he has been Chairman of the Board of Directors since March 2009. Mr. Splinter worked for nearly 20 years at Intel Corporation (Intel), most recently as Executive Vice President and Director of the Sales and Marketing Group."

10-K signature page: "/s/ Michael R. Splinter, President, Chief Executive Officer (Principal Executive Officer), December 11, 2009."

→ Founder long departed; Splinter is a long-tenured competent operator (20-year Intel veteran, 6 years into AMAT CEO tenure at trough). Per spec rule, "founder_in_place=yes requires the founder remained CEO/Board through the trough" — fails. Not "replaced-by-competent" because Splinter's appointment (2003) preceded the trough by 6 years and was not a rescue dynamic; he was the steady-state operator, not a turnaround replacement. **departed** is the correct spec value.

→ **founder_in_place = "departed"** (single canonical value from spec domain).

Stake direction: no SEC Form-4 open-market share-purchase events by Splinter documented in FY09. Standard equity comp via RSU/option grants. Per canary rule, vesting is not "increasing."

→ **founder_insider_stake_direction = "flat"** (single canonical spec value). Matches catalog row.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "Net sales $5,014 [FY09] $8,129 [FY08] $9,735 [FY07]" (in thousands: $5,013,607 / $8,129,240 / $9,734,856). → FY09 revenue **-38% y/y** (and -48% vs FY07 peak).

10-K verbatim: "Gross margin as a percentage of net sales decreased to 28.5 percent in fiscal 2009 from 42.4 percent in fiscal 2008, compared to 46.1 percent in fiscal 2007. The decrease in the gross margin percentage in fiscal 2009 from fiscal 2008 was due to lower net sales, lower-margin product mix, and reduced factory absorption, offset in part by cost control initiatives."

10-K verbatim: "Net income (loss) $(305,327) [FY09] $960,746 [FY08] $1,710,196 [FY07]" (in thousands).

→ -1390bps GM compression in one year; revenue down -38%; swung from $961M net income to $305M net loss. Both **deteriorating** (margins) and **declining** (revenue) are unambiguous spec values.

## F. Operational restructuring (idiosyncratic context)

10-K verbatim: "Reduced its workforce by approximately 1,800 positions" (FY08 actions) plus "reduce its workforce as of October 25, 2009 by approximately 1,300 to 1,500 positions, or 10 to 12 percent, over a period of 18 months."

Combined ~3,100-3,300 position reductions across FY08-FY09 (out of ~14,000 starting headcount = ~22%). This was a deep but disciplined restructuring — preserved R&D and customer-engagement headcount.
