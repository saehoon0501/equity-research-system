## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

10-K Item 1A verbatim: "DRAM market, which experienced significant declines in pricing during 2015 and 2016."

10-K MD&A verbatim: "DRAM For the year ended 2016 2015 (percentage change from prior year) Net sales (30)% (7)% Average selling prices per gigabit (35)% (11)% Gigabits sold 7% 4% Cost per gigabit (17)% (12)%."

10-K Item 1A verbatim: "Memory industry is experiencing a period of consolidation as a result of these market conditions and other factors."

→ Classic DRAM cycle: ASP collapse -35% in FY16 (after -11% FY15), volumes still growing, costs falling but not fast enough. This is canonical **weakening** (cyclical), not structural-decline — DRAM/NAND demand was still secularly growing (mobile, datacenter, SSD adoption); only pricing was over-supply pressured.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = >24mo)

10-K verbatim: "Cash and equivalents and short-term investments $4,398 [FY16] $3,521 [FY15]" + "Long-term marketable investments 414 [FY16] 2,113 [FY15]." Total liquid assets ~$4.8B.

10-K verbatim: "Long-term debt 9,154 [FY16] 6,252 [FY15]." Significant debt load but well-laddered.

10-K verbatim: "Net cash provided by operating activities $3,168 [FY16] $5,208 [FY15] $5,699 [FY14]."

10-K verbatim: "Net increase (decrease) in cash and equivalents $1,853 [FY16]."

→ MU GENERATED $3.2B of operating cash flow in the trough year despite a $276M net loss (depreciation $2.98B and other non-cash items dominate). Cash *increased* $1.85B during the trough. **>24mo** is unambiguous — runway is effectively unconstrained. Catalog row "12-24mo" is contradicted by the actual cash-flow statement; spec rule "ignore catalog values that don't fit; pick closest in-domain spec value" → **>24mo**.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

Inotera consolidation (closed Dec 2016) — MU acquired remaining ~67% of Taiwan DRAM JV for $4B; this was strategic concentration, not desperation. 3D NAND ramp underway (1st-gen FloatingGate→2nd-gen). Durcan continued capex through trough ($5.8B FY16 vs $4.0B FY15).

10-K verbatim: "Memory industry is experiencing a period of consolidation as a result of these market conditions and other factors, and we may engage in discussions regarding potential acquisitions and similar opportunities arising out of these industry conditions."

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = departed; founder_insider_stake_direction = flat)

Original Micron founders (Joe & Ward Parkinson, Dennis Wilson, Doug Pitman; founded 1978 in Boise) all departed the company by mid-1990s. Steve Appleton, CEO since 1994, died in an experimental-aircraft crash Feb 3, 2012. D. Mark Durcan was named CEO immediately thereafter.

10-K Officers section verbatim: "D. Mark Durcan 55 Director and Chief Executive Officer" and "D. Mark Durcan joined us in June 1984 and has served in various positions since that time" and "Director and Chief Executive Officer in February 2012."

10-K signature page: "/s/ D. Mark Durcan Chief Executive Officer October 28, 2016."

→ Founder is not in place; long-tenured internal lieutenant operates the company. Per spec rule (founder_in_place=yes requires founder remained CEO/Board), this is **departed**. Not "replaced-by-competent" because there was no rescue dynamic — Durcan was the natural successor inside an ongoing operator regime, not an outside operator parachuted in to save a failing company.

→ **founder_in_place = "departed"** (single canonical value from spec domain ["yes","departed","replaced-by-competent"]).

Stake direction: no SEC Form-4 open-market purchase events documented in FY16 window. CEO equity comp issued via standard RSU/PRSU vehicles. Per canary rule, option/RSU vesting alone does not count as "increasing."

→ **founder_insider_stake_direction = "flat"** (single canonical value from spec domain ["increasing","flat","decreasing","departed"]). Matches catalog row.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "Net sales $12,399 [FY16] $16,192 [FY15] $16,358 [FY14] $9,073 [FY13] $8,234 [FY12]." → FY16 revenue **-23% y/y**.

10-K MD&A verbatim: "Net sales for 2016 decreased 23% as compared to 2015."

10-K verbatim: "Gross margin 2,505 20% 5,215 32% 5,437 33%."

10-K MD&A verbatim: "Our gross margin percentage declined to 20% for 2016 from 32% for 2015 primarily due to declines in the gross margin percentages for CNBU, MBU, and SBU, as decreases in average selling prices outpaced manufacturing cost reductions."

→ -1200bps GM compression in one year. Both **deteriorating** (margins) and **declining** (revenue) are unambiguous spec values.

10-K verbatim: "Net income (loss) attributable to Micron $(276) [FY16] $2,899 [FY15] $3,045 [FY14]."

## F. Operational restructuring (idiosyncratic context)

10-K verbatim: "Restructure and asset impairments 67 [FY16]." Modest restructuring (~$67M) compared to peer cycles. MU's response was capital-intensive consolidation (Inotera buy-in), not balance-sheet retrenchment.
