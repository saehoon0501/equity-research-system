## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

10-K Item 1A verbatim: "Global economic conditions have reduced demand for our products… consumers and businesses have postponed spending in response to tighter credit, negative financial news and/or declines in income or asset values, which have reduced the demand for our products."

10-K Item 1A verbatim: "The current financial turmoil affecting the banking system and financial markets… has resulted in a tightening in the credit markets, a low level of liquidity in many financial markets, and extreme volatility."

Press release 2009-02-10 (Jen-Hsun Huang): "The environment is clearly difficult and uncertain." Demand collapse + credit-market freeze = industry tailwind has weakened from FY08's intact state. NOT "structural-decline" (this is cyclical, not secular) and NOT "reversed" (the secular GPU/CUDA thesis remains; it's FY10+ where it re-asserts).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = >24mo)

10-K verbatim: "At January 25, 2009 and January 27, 2008, we had $1.26 billion and $1.81 billion, respectively, in cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities."

10-K verbatim: "We have never paid and do not expect to pay cash dividends for the foreseeable future."

No material long-term debt — only ~$25.6M capital-lease obligations. The $1.26B vs FY09 net loss run-rate (Q4 net loss $147.7M annualized = ~$590M) gives roughly 2 years of unaffected runway even at Q4-FY09 burn rate; sequential improvement reduces actual burn through FY10. Cash position therefore comfortably **>24mo**, not 12-24mo.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

CUDA SDK launched Feb 2007; Tesla HPC line shipped 2007. Through the trough NVIDIA continued investing — 10-K verbatim: "we announced a workforce reduction to allow for continued investment in strategic growth areas… we eliminated approximately 360 positions worldwide, or about 6.5% of our global workforce" with severance cost of just $8.0M.

This is sector-extension feature `moat_state` territory — for universal-core extraction, ignore the secular-GPU thesis; use only FY09 demand/credit framing.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "During the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2009, our gross margin declined to 29.4% as compared to 45.7% during the fourth quarter of fiscal year 2008 and decreased from 41.0% from the third quarter of fiscal year 2009."

→ Q4 FY09 GM 29.4% vs Q4 FY08 GM 45.7% = -16.3pp y/y collapse; sequential -11.6pp from Q3 FY09. Full-year FY09 GM ~34.3% vs FY08 ~45.6%. **Deteriorating** at FY09 close — single unambiguous spec value (["improving","stable","deteriorating"]).

10-K revenue: FY09 = $3.425B vs FY08 $4.1B = **-16% y/y**; Q4 FY09 = $481.1M vs Q4 FY08 = ~$1.2B = **-60% y/y**. Q4 GAAP net loss $147.7M / -$0.27 EPS.

→ **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** (single canonical spec value from ["growing","flat","declining","pre-revenue"]; -16% y/y full-year + -60% y/y Q4 unambiguously crosses the "declining" threshold).

Share count: outstanding 542.45M as of 2009-03-06; treasury rose ~29M shares y/y (anti-dilutive — net share count fell). Sector-extension `dilution_direction` not used in universal-core.

## F. Operational shock specific to NVDA — bumpgate (idiosyncratic, not feature-mapped)

10-K verbatim: "in July 2008, we recorded a $196.0 million charge against cost of revenue to cover anticipated customer warranty, repair, return, replacement and other associated costs arising from a weak die/packaging material set in certain versions of our previous generation MCP and GPU products used in notebook systems."

Compounded the macro shock; explains why the GM collapse was steeper than peer semis. Not directly mapped to universal-core features but provides context for `margin_trajectory = deteriorating` magnitude.
