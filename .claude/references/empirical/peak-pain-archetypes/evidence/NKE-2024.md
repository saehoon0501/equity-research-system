## A. Industry shock & operational stress (industry_tailwind = weakening)

Athletic footwear/apparel TAM remained intact globally, BUT category-level shifts hit Nike disproportionately: (1) Hoka + On gains in performance-running share; (2) New Balance + Adidas Samba/Gazelle resurgence in lifestyle; (3) YEEZY breakup left a lifestyle hole at the same time Nike's own Jordan/Dunk drops fatigued; (4) Greater China consumer caution + Anta/Li-Ning local-brand share gains; (5) Donahoe-era DTC pivot under-invested in wholesale relationships with Foot Locker/Dick's, costing distribution leverage.

→ `industry_tailwind = "weakening"` (cyclical + execution-driven), NOT structural-decline (athleisure category secularly intact). Catalog row "eroding-China" matches Greater China 4% reported FY24 growth obscuring underlying share loss to local brands.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position (cash_runway = >24mo)

Nike investment-grade balance sheet: long-term debt ~$8B; cash + short-term investments ~$10B+ at FY24 close; operating cash flow $7.4B+ FY24; net cash positive. Quarterly dividend; ~$5B+ buyback authorization. FCF/runway is not constrained — Nike is the canonical "blue-chip in cyclical air-pocket" archetype where universal-core balance sheet feature stays healthy through the multiple compression. → `cash_runway = ">24mo"` unambiguous.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

FY24 Q4 ER (June 27, 2024): revenue miss; FY25 guidance cut (revenues to decline mid-single-digit). Stock dropped -20% in a single session. Donahoe public statements: "We are taking our near-term challenges head-on, while making continued progress in the areas that matter most to NIKE's future — serving the athlete through performance innovation, moving at the pace of the consumer and growing the complete marketplace."

Sept 19, 2024: Nike announced Donahoe retirement + Elliott Hill (32-year veteran, retired 2020) returning as CEO effective Oct 14, 2024. Hill led Consumer & Marketplace before retirement; well-respected internally and by wholesale partners. Knight family + Mark Parker (Executive Chairman) backed transition.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory (margin_trajectory = improving; revenue_trajectory = flat)

FY24 (year ended May 31, 2024) per company press release:
- Revenue: $51.4B vs $51.2B FY23 = +0% reported, +1% currency-neutral
- NIKE Brand revenue: $49.3B, +1% reported and currency-neutral
- Gross margin: 44.6%, +110bps y/y "driven by strategic pricing actions and lower ocean freight rates, partially offset by higher product input costs and unfavorable foreign exchange rates"
- Greater China revenue: +4% reported FY24
- Net income: $5.7B, +12% y/y

→ `revenue_trajectory = "flat"` (literally 0% reported; +1% FX-neutral is closest to flat in spec domain {growing, flat, declining, pre-revenue}). `margin_trajectory = "improving"` (+110bps gross margin expansion).

This is the NKE archetype paradox: trough-year operating metrics actually IMPROVED (margin) or held FLAT (revenue), but FY25 guidance cut + Donahoe-era innovation deficit drove the multiple compression. Like MELI, the drawdown was largely a forward-expectations event, not a current-year operational deterioration.

## F. Operational shock specific to NKE — innovation deficit + DTC pivot

Donahoe-era 2020-2024 strategic missteps: (1) over-pivot to DTC at expense of wholesale relationships with Foot Locker, Dick's, Famous Footwear; (2) reorganization away from category-led structure to consumer-construct ("Mens/Womens/Kids") diluted product expertise; (3) reliance on Jordan/Dunk retro drops over performance innovation; (4) Greater China underinvestment in local design + product. By FY24 the wholesale ecosystem had repaired relationships with Adidas/Hoka/On; Foot Locker pivoted away from Nike-dependence. June 2024 ER + FY25 guide cut crystallized the multiple compression. Sept 2024 Hill appointment kicked off turnaround.
