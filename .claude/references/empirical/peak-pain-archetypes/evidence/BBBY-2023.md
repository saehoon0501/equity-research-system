## A. Industry shock & operational stress (industry_tailwind = structural-decline)

10-K verbatim: "Comparable Sales for the year ended February 25, 2023 decreased by approximately 29.7%. Management attributes a portion of this decline to the impact of lower traffic due to macro-economic factors, such as steep inflation, and fluctuations in purchasing patterns of the consumer."

Specialty home-goods retail facing structural headwinds: Amazon/Wayfair share gains, big-box (Target/Walmart/Costco) value compression, declining mall foot traffic. Tritton-era 2020-22 strategic missteps (private-label over-pivot, inventory misallocation) accelerated decline. Post-COVID home-goods demand normalization compounded. → `industry_tailwind = "structural-decline"` is operator judgment that the brick-and-mortar specialty home-goods category is in long-term secular decline, not just cyclical weakness.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position (cash_runway = distressed)

10-K verbatim: "We have incurred significant losses over the past few years. As of February 25, 2023, we had approximately $65.9 million in cash and cash equivalents, a decrease of approximately $373.6 million as compared with February 26, 2022."

10-K verbatim: "There can be no assurance that the Company will be able to successfully consummate a Chapter 11 plan, creating substantial doubt about the Company's ability to continue as a going concern."

Net loss FY22 $3.50B vs $559.6M FY21 vs $150.8M FY20 — 6x expansion. Asset-based credit facility drawn; covenant breaches; April 23, 2023 Ch11 filing. → `cash_runway = "distressed"` (single unambiguous spec value; going-concern doubt explicitly disclosed).

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

10-K verbatim: "began to execute a comprehensive strategic plan led by our new President and Chief Executive Officer, Sue Gove, who was appointed in October 2022 after serving as Interim Chief Executive Officer." Mark Tritton (CEO 2019-2022) departed June 2022 after disastrous private-label pivot. CFO Gustavo Arnal died by suicide September 2022 amid securities litigation. Buybuy Baby attempted divestiture failed. Stalking-horse bidder process post-Ch11.

## D. Founder/insider behavior (founder_in_place = departed; founder_insider_stake_direction = departed)

BBBY founders Warren Eisenberg + Leonard Feinstein had retired decades earlier (Eisenberg co-Chairman emeritus). The relevant insider during the trough was activist Ryan Cohen (RC Ventures) — 10-K Exhibit 10.58: "Cooperation Agreement, by and among Bed Bath Beyond Inc., RC Ventures LLC and Ryan Cohen, dated as of March 24, 2022."

Per public Form 4 filings: RC Ventures held 9.45M shares (~12% stake), filed amendment Aug 16, 2022 disclosing intent to sell, dumped position Aug 16-17, 2022 at $18.68-$29.22 — net ~$59M profit. Stock collapsed 40% on confirmed exit. → `founder_in_place = "departed"`; `founder_insider_stake_direction = "departed"` (catalog row "Cohen exit" matches).

This is the archetype-defining contrast vs CVNA (Garcias bought) — when the high-conviction insider exits at the trough, the equity is signaling no rescue.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim Consolidated Statement of Operations: "Net sales $5,344,685 [FY22] $7,867,778 [FY21] $9,233,028 [FY20]" — -32% y/y FY22, -42% from FY20. "Cost of sales 4,129,802 / 5,384,287 / 6,114,947" — gross profit $1,214,883 FY22 vs $2,483,491 FY21 vs $3,118,081 FY20.

10-K verbatim: "Gross profit in Fiscal 2022 was $1.21 billion, or 22.7% of net sales, compared with $2.48 billion, or 31.6% of net sales in Fiscal 2021. The decrease..."

→ Revenue -32% y/y = `declining`. Gross margin 22.7% vs 31.6% prior = -8.9pp = `deteriorating`. Both unambiguous.

10-K verbatim: "the net loss for Fiscal 2022, 2021, and 2020, was $3.50 billion, $559.6 million, and $150.8 million, respectively."

## F. Operational shock specific to BBBY — Tritton-era strategic damage + meme distortion

Tritton CEO 2019-2022 pursued aggressive private-label "Owned Brand" pivot replacing national brands with house brands; inventory composition mismatch; customer alienation. Q1 FY22 -25% comparable sales. Ryan Cohen activist play Mar 2022 → board seats → exit Aug 2022. Meme-stock retail rally Aug 2022 ($30 peak) on Cohen + WSB momentum, dissipated within weeks. CFO suicide Sept 2022. ATM equity offering attempts diluted but failed to restore solvency. April 23, 2023 Ch11. Stalking-horse process; brand IP sold to Overstock (which became Bed Bath & Beyond Inc. via name change — note: current EDGAR CIK 0001130713 is Beyond/Overstock-renamed, NOT the original BBBY entity CIK 0000886158).
