## A. Industry shock & operational stress (industry_tailwind = reversed)

10-K verbatim: "it is possible that hybrid work arrangements could continue or increase, such as workspace sharing or hoteling of office space. To the extent these practices become permanent or further increase, demand for office space, including" [our properties may be adversely affected].

OPI is concentrated in single-tenant office leased to government and large enterprise tenants — exactly the segment most exposed to WFH/hybrid hollowing-out. Combined with the 2022–23 rate-hike cycle compressing office cap rates, this maps to **reversed** rather than weakening: the post-COVID office regime change is structural for OPI's specific portfolio (suburban government office is not coming back to 2019 occupancy/rents under most plausible scenarios).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position (cash_runway = distressed)

10-K verbatim: "44.4% of our annualized rental income was from our properties leased" to non-government tenants; underlying portfolio of 152 properties / ~20.5M sq ft.

OPI faced a wall of unsecured note maturities (2025: $453.6M; 2026: $300M; 2027: $250M; 2031: $300M) into a market unwilling to refinance suburban office at par. Distress trajectory:
- **May 2024**: dividend cut from $0.25/qtr to $0.01/qtr (~96% cut), "distribution rate to common shareholders is $0.01 per share per quarter, or $0.04 per share" — preserving cash for principal repayment.
- **2024 H2**: amended exchange offers proposing 9.000% Senior Secured Notes due 2029 in exchange for 2025/26/27/31 unsecured notes.
- **Dec 11, 2024**: closed private exchange of $340M of $453.6M 2025 unsecured notes for $445M new 3.25% senior secured notes due 2027 + 11.5M shares (~19.9% of outstanding) + $25M premium. New 2027 notes carry quarterly $6.5M amortization + $125M mandatory March 2026 repayment, secured by first-lien on 35 properties ($1.3B) and second-lien on 19 more.

Equity issuance + secured-collateral pledge + amortizing terms = unambiguously **distressed** under the spec domain. Standard going-concern markers: substantial-doubt language did not appear in FY23 10-K, but FY24 events crystallized the distressed framing.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

OPI is externally managed by RMR Group (publicly traded affiliate); 10-K verbatim: "The ability of our manager, The RMR Group LLC, or RMR, to successfully manage us." The RMR external-management structure compounded distress because no internal management team had aligned equity-conviction skin-in-game during the trough — the manager fee streams persist regardless of OPI's outcome. Sector-extension `external_management_misalignment` territory.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

Operating metrics:
- Occupancy declining as government tenants consolidated footprint and private-sector tenants downsized.
- Rising interest expense as 2025 unsecured notes (4.50%) refinanced into 9% secured (2029 exchange offer) and 3.25% secured (2027 exchange) — mixed-rate impact but accompanied by amortization, equity dilution, and lien-pledging.
- Disposition-driven revenue contraction as OPI sold properties to fund maturities (the 2024 exchange brought ~11.5M new shares = 19.9% dilution).

→ **margin_trajectory = "deteriorating"** (single canonical spec value).
→ **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** (single canonical spec value).

## F. Operational specifics — distressed restructuring mechanics

The Dec 2024 private exchange formalizes the NON-SURVIVOR trajectory: senior unsecured creditors took new secured 2027 paper at lower coupon (3.25%) but secured by 35 properties + 19.9% equity dilution + $25M premium + quarterly amortization + $125M March-2026 cliff. This is the textbook distressed-exchange template — preserves the legal entity but transfers economic value from common holders to creditors. Drawdown >95% from 2019 highs reflects that transfer.
