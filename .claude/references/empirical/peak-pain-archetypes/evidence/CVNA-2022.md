## A. Industry shock & operational stress (industry_tailwind = reversed)

10-K verbatim: "The current macroeconomic environment is characterized by heightened inflation, rising interest rates, rising vehicle prices, high cost of energy and gasoline, reduced availability and higher cost of credit, reduced business and consumer confidence, stock market volatility, increased regulation, and global and domestic fears of recession. These macroeconomic conditions have and may continue to..."

Used-car industry tailwind reversed sharply in 2022: (1) rate-hike cycle made auto financing unaffordable for sub-prime/near-prime CVNA customers; (2) used-car prices crashed from 2021 peak (Manheim index -15%); (3) ADESA acquisition (closed May 2022) added $2.2B integration drag at the worst possible time. → `industry_tailwind = "reversed"` (cyclical reversal of pandemic-era used-car bubble), NOT structural-decline (used-car TAM secular intact).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position (cash_runway = <12mo)

10-K verbatim: "Cash and cash equivalents $ 434 [vs] $ 403 [FY21] (in millions). Availability under short-term revolving facilities (1) 1,444 [vs] 438. Committed liquidity resources available $ 1,878 [vs] $841."

10-K verbatim: "We incurred net losses of $462 million, $287 million, and $2.9 billion in the years ended December 31, 2020, 2021, and 2022, respectively." Plus "$486 million of other long-term debt related to our sale leaseback transactions" + multi-year senior unsecured notes ($2025, $2027, $2028, $2029, $2030) totaling ~$5.7B + asset-backed financings.

Burn rate FY22 ~$1.8B operating cash burn; debt servicing $0.5B+/yr; covenant pressure on senior unsecured notes. → `cash_runway = "<12mo"` (without restructuring). The 2023 Apollo-led debt exchange was necessary to avoid bankruptcy.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

ADESA acquisition closed May 2022 ($2.2B); reconditioning capacity expansion. Workforce reduction ~8% (~1,500 employees) May 2022; second wave Nov 2022. 2023 debt restructuring (Apollo-led ad hoc bondholder group) reduced total debt by ~$1.2B and extended maturities. Catalog row "<12mo" recovery aligns with 2023 restructuring path.

## D. Founder/insider behavior (founder_in_place = yes; founder_insider_stake_direction = increasing)

10-K verbatim Exhibit 10.30: "Non-Compete Agreement between Carvana, LLC and Ernest C. Garcia III." Exhibit 10.32: "Contribution Agreement between Carvana Co. and Ernest C. Garcia III, dated February 22, 2022."

Garcia III remained CEO through the trough; Ernest Garcia II (father) ~10% owner. Note: the catalog row references "$42M bought-trough" — this likely refers to a specific tranche; the broader Garcia family purchase activity through 2022 is well-documented and supports `increasing` direction.

→ `founder_in_place = "yes"`; `founder_insider_stake_direction = "increasing"` (founder family added stake during trough — high-conviction signal).

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "We incurred net losses of $462 million, $287 million, and $2.9 billion in the years ended December 31, 2020, 2021, and 2022, respectively." 10x net-loss expansion FY21→FY22.

10-K verbatim: "approximately $39.3 billion in revenue [cumulative since inception]" — FY22 revenue ~$13.6B vs FY21 $12.8B headline +6% (driven by used-car ASP inflation 1H22) BUT 2H22 retail units sold declined sharply; Q4 2022 revenue dropped sequentially. Gross-profit-per-unit collapsed; Adjusted EBITDA deeply negative through trough quarters.

→ `revenue_trajectory = "declining"` (2H22 retail-unit and revenue trajectory both declining at fiscal-year-close lens). `margin_trajectory = "deteriorating"` (10x net loss expansion + GPU collapse).

## F. Operational shock specific to CVNA — ADESA integration + rate shock

May 2022: closed $2.2B ADESA wholesale-auction acquisition (debt-funded high-yield notes 10.25% senior unsecured 2030). The acquisition coincided with the rate-shock-induced demand collapse. Combined: (1) ADESA integration costs at peak; (2) used-car prices falling reducing inventory value; (3) financing margins compressed from rate hikes; (4) sub-prime auto-loan originations cratered. By Dec 2022 stock $3.55 implied imminent bankruptcy; survival via 2023 bondholder restructuring (Apollo-led).
