## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

10-K verbatim: "Our total revenues declined from $847.4 million in the year ended December 31, 2007 to $721.6 million during the year ended December 31, 2008. Should the global economic [downturn]" persist… (truncated). Trend "continued and accelerated throughout 2008."

The shock is dual: macro consumer-spending pullback (2008-09 recession) PLUS idiosyncratic fad-cycle reversal (Crocs classic Beach/Cayman models fell from 62.2% of revenues in 2006 to 25% in 2008 as the trend matured). Footwear category itself remained intact post-crisis (consumer staples-adjacent), so this is **weakening**, not `structural-decline` (the Crocs brand is not structurally dead — McCarvel-era reversal proves cyclical-fad classification) and not `reversed` (no permanent inversion).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = distressed)

10-K verbatim: "Our auditors have expressed substantial doubt about our ability to continue as a going concern. The accompanying consolidated financial statements for the year ended December 31, 2008 were prepared under the assumption that we will continue to" operate as going concern.

10-K verbatim: "We incurred losses of $185.1 million in the year ended December 31, 2008 and experienced a decline in revenues from $847.4 million for the year ended December 31, 2007 to $721.6 million for the year ended December 31, 2008."

10-K verbatim: at Dec 31, 2008 had $51.6M cash and $22.4M outstanding under the Union Bank Revolving Credit Facility, "which currently has a maturity date of April 2, 2009." Going-concern + revolver maturing in <4 months + active negotiations for asset-backed replacement = canonical **distressed**, NOT `<12mo` (the spec's `<12mo` lacks the qualitative going-concern signal — distressed is the precise spec value here).

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

Restructuring in 2008: $76.3M inventory write-down + $23.9M goodwill impairment + $20.9M equipment/molds + $0.6M patents + transferred manufacturing from Brazil/Canada into US/Mexico. The sector-extension `moat_state` lens would mark Crocs brand-equity as damaged-but-recoverable; for universal-core extraction, ignore.

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = departed; founder_insider_stake_direction = departed)

10-K signature page Mar 17, 2009: signed by Ronald R. Snyder as President/CEO. Subsequent-events footnote verbatim: "the Company's board of directors appointed Mr. John Duerden to serve as the Company's president and Chief Executive Officer effective March 16, 2009." Snyder (CEO 2005-2009) was effectively forced out around fiscal close; founder Lyndon Hanson long-since marginalized to minority. Note: catalog row references "Marks-fired-Jan-09" — likely referring to George Boedecker / Lyndon Hanson / Scott Seamans original-founder branch's diminished operating role; the operating CEO at fiscal close was Snyder (non-founder), replaced by Duerden weeks later, then by McCarvel (2010, the "competent" replacement that drove the multi-bag recovery).

→ **founder_in_place = "departed"** (FY2008-close lens captures going-concern + Duerden announcement + Snyder exit). The eventual `replaced-by-competent` (McCarvel) value applies only to FY2010+ archetype matching.

→ **founder_insider_stake_direction = "departed"** (canonical pairing when `founder_in_place=departed`).

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "revenues decreased 14.8%, or $125.8 million, to $721.6 million, in the year ended December 31, 2008, from $847.4 million in the year ended December 31, 2007."

10-K verbatim: "selling, general and administrative expenses increased to 51.1% in the year ended December 31, 2008 from 30.7% for the [prior] year." Net loss $185.1M. Impairment charges $45.8M (vs $0 in 2007). Sept 30, 2008 triggering event for goodwill impairment evaluation.

→ **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** — single canonical spec value (-14.8% y/y unambiguous).
→ **margin_trajectory = "deteriorating"** — operating loss + 20pp SG&A blowout + first-ever impairment cycle.

## F. Operational shock specific to CROX — fad-cycle exhaustion (idiosyncratic)

Concentration risk verbatim: "Sales of our classic Beach and Cayman models accounted for 25%, 30%, and 62.2% of [footwear sales]" for 2008/2007/2006 — fad collapse. Inventory pre-built for fad-extension demand had to be liquidated at markdowns. Compounded macro cycle. Sector-extension only.
