## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = structural-decline)

10-K verbatim Item 1A: "intensifying competition from our competitors have had, and could continue to have, an adverse effect on our business." Sun's competitor list verbatim: "Hitachi Data Systems, Inc. (HDS), Fujitsu Technology Solutions, Inc., Microsoft Corporation (Microsoft), Oracle Corporation (Oracle) and Intel Corporation (Intel). We also compete with (i) systems manufacturers and resellers of systems based on microprocessors manufactured by Intel Corporation (Intel)…"

The shift is **secular, not cyclical**: customers were migrating from proprietary SPARC/Solaris UNIX servers to commodity x86 boxes running Linux/Windows. Sun's response (UltraSPARC T2 "commodity microprocessor", x64 systems, Java open-sourcing, Solaris 10 cross-platform support) was an explicit acknowledgment that the proprietary moat had eroded permanently. Combined with the Apr-2009 strategic-review-leading-to-Oracle-acquisition, this is the canonical **structural-decline** pattern (NOT `weakening` — the workstation/UNIX-server franchise did not reassert post-trough; Oracle absorbed the assets and within ~5 years had wound down most Sun product lines).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = 12-24mo)

10-K verbatim: "We ended the fiscal year 2009 with a cash and marketable debt securities balance of $3.1 billion."

10-K Selected Financial Data: Cash & marketable debt securities Jun-2009 $3,061M vs Jun-2008 $3,310M; long-term debt $1,249M (vs $1,265M FY08); net cash decrease $1,348M during FY09.

FY09 full-year net loss $2,234M (sum of quarterly: -$1,677M, -$209M, -$201M, -$147M). $3.1B / ~$1.4B annualized burn (excluding Q1 goodwill-impairment one-timer) ≈ ~2 years runway. **12-24mo** is the precise spec value (not `>24mo` — burn rate against trough-year operating cash use; not `<12mo` — sufficient buffer to negotiate Oracle deal at $9.50/share rather than fire-sale).

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

10-K Recent Events verbatim: "On April 19, 2009, we entered into a definitive agreement under which Oracle Corporation (Oracle) will acquire all of our common stock, through a merger, for $9.50 per share in cash and we will become a wholly owned subsidiary of Oracle." Sun stockholders adopted the merger Jul 16, 2009; DOJ approved Aug 20, 2009; deal closed Jan 2010. Java/MySQL/Solaris IP assets ultimately survived; the independent equity did not.

This is the canonical NON-SURVIVOR profile — equity ceased to exist as a separately-listed franchise. Sector-extension only.

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = departed; founder_insider_stake_direction = departed)

10-K verbatim Executive Officers list: "Jonathan I. Schwartz 43 Chief Executive Officer and President" — "Mr. Schwartz has served as President and Chief Executive Officer since April 2006."

McNealy (co-founder with Bechtolsheim/Khosla/Joy in 1982) signature page: "/s/ SCOTT G. McNEALY (Scott G. McNealy) Chairman of the Board of Directors August 28, 2008" — Chairman role only, NOT executive. McNealy's CEO tenure ended April 2006; founder operationally departed >3 years before fiscal close.

→ **founder_in_place = "departed"** — non-executive Chairman seat does not satisfy the spec's `yes` (which requires operating control). The eventual `replaced-by-competent` value would also fail (Schwartz did not save the franchise — Oracle acquisition was the resolution).

→ **founder_insider_stake_direction = "departed"** — canonical pairing.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K Selected Financial Data verbatim: "Total net revenues 11,449 13,880 13,873" (FY09 / FY08 / FY07) and "Gross margin 4,731 6,455 6,265".

→ Revenue -17.5% y/y. Gross-margin 41.3% FY09 vs 46.5% FY08 = -5.2pp compression. Q1 FY09 (Sept 2008 quarter) op-loss $1,650M (driven by ~$1.4B goodwill impairment). Full-year op-loss $2,236M.

→ **revenue_trajectory = "declining"** (single canonical spec value; -17.5% unambiguously crosses threshold).
→ **margin_trajectory = "deteriorating"** (GM compression + impairment + sustained operating losses across all four FY09 quarters).

## F. Operational shock specific to SUNW — Oracle takeover (idiosyncratic, not feature-mapped)

The 10-K explicitly notes the Oracle merger announcement weighing on independent operating performance: "The announcement and pendency of our agreement to be acquired by Oracle and intensifying competition from our competitors have had, and could continue to have, an adverse effect on our business."

This is the leapfrog-pattern outcome verbatim — the franchise did not survive in independent form; this case is therefore the canonical NON-SURVIVOR profile in the catalog.
