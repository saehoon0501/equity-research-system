## A. Industry shock & operational stress (industry_tailwind = intact)

10-K Item 1: "Netflix is the world's leading Internet subscription service for enjoying TV shows and movies. Our subscribers can instantly watch unlimited TV shows and movies streamed over the Internet to their TVs, computers and mobile devices."

The 2011 crisis was self-inflicted — July 2011 plan-separation raised effective price ~60% for hybrid subscribers, then September 2011 Qwikster announcement compounded. Streaming secular tailwind remained intact (subscribers continued to grow domestic streaming throughout); industry was not reversing. Catalog "rebound" descriptor confirms recovery context, but at the **trough fiscal year** (Dec 31, 2011), the streaming adoption curve was still secular-up — the issue was execution and pricing, not industry decline.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position (cash_runway = >24mo)

10-K verbatim: "Cash and cash equivalents $508,053 [thousand]" + short-term investments $289,758 = ~$798M liquid at FY11 close. 10-K verbatim: raised "$200 million worth of convertible notes in a private placement and $200 million worth of equity through a public offering" in November 2011. Long-term debt $200M (existing) + $200M new related-party (Technology Crossover Ventures convert). Combined ~$1.2B liquidity vs FY11 operating cash flow positive $317M = comfortable >24mo runway.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

July 2011 price split: "Prior to July 2011, in the U.S., our streaming and DVD-by-mail operations were combined under a single 'hybrid' plan. In July 2011, we separated the combined plans." Sept 2011 Qwikster spin announcement (later reversed in October 2011). Q3 2011 lost ~800K US subscribers — reversed direction by Q4 2011 with international expansion (Latin America Sept 2011, UK/Ireland Jan 2012).

## D. Founder/insider behavior (founder_in_place = yes; founder_insider_stake_direction = flat)

10-K verbatim: "we rely on the continued service of our senior management, including our Chief Executive Officer and co-founder Reed Hastings."

Hastings publicly apologized Sept 2011 ("I messed up. I owe you an explanation"), reversed Qwikster, stayed in seat. Beneficial ownership ~5% maintained — no Form-4 disposition documented in trough window. → `founder_in_place = "yes"`; `founder_insider_stake_direction = "flat"`.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = growing)

10-K Selected Financial Data verbatim: "Revenues $3,204,577 [FY11] $2,162,625 [FY10] $1,670,269 [FY09]" — FY11 +48% y/y. Gross profit $1,164,676 [FY11] vs $805,270 [FY10] vs $590,998 [FY09]. Headline GM ~36.3% FY11 ~steady, BUT contribution margin (streaming) deteriorated through 2H11 as content licensing costs ramped (Starz negotiations failed Sept 2011; new $1B+ Open Connect / content commitments). Q4 FY11 operating margin compressed sharply from content-cost ramp + international launch losses.

Subscriber metric: 24.4M total members FY11 close vs 20.0M FY10 = growing. Domestic streaming continued post-Q3 dip.

→ Revenue growing (+48%); margin trajectory deteriorating (Q4 contribution margin compression + FY12 guidance for sharp operating loss confirmed at FY11 close).

## F. Operational shock specific to NFLX — Qwikster/price-split (idiosyncratic)

July 12, 2011: Plan separation — DVD-only $7.99/mo + streaming-only $7.99/mo (vs combined $9.99). Effective ~60% increase for hybrid users. Sept 18, 2011: Hastings announces Qwikster DVD spinoff. Oct 10, 2011: Qwikster reversal. Q3 2011: ~800K net domestic subscriber loss. Stock peaked ~$298 July 2011 → trough ~$62-69 late Nov 2011 (-77 to -79%). Self-inflicted execution shock; demand and underlying secular adoption intact.
