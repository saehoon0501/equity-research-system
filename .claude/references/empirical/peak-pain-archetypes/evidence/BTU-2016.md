## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = structural-decline)

10-K verbatim: "natural gas is the most significant substitute to thermal coal for electricity generation in the U.S., and vice versa… The U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) reported in its February 2016 'Short Term Energy Outlook' that coal's share of U.S. electricity generation for all sectors was 33% in 2015, down from 39% in 2014. Electricity generation from coal [was] displaced by natural gas prices, which fell to an average price of $2.63 per mmBtu in 2015. The EIA expects full year average U.S. natural gas prices to remain in line with 2015 prices at an average of $2.64 per mmBTU."

→ Structural-decline (single canonical from spec domain ["intact","weakening","reversed","structural-decline"]). Distinct from cyclical "weakening" because: (a) coal's loss of share is unidirectional, not mean-reverting; (b) regulatory pressure (EPA PM/MATS/Clean Power Plan + ozone NAAQS Oct 2015) is ratchet-only; (c) financing markets had effectively closed for thermal coal by 2015. Australian metallurgical coal demand from China also fell.

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = distressed)

10-K verbatim: "substantial doubt about whether we will meet our obligations as they become due within one year after the date of issuance of this report. We have also elected to exercise the 30-day grace period with respect to a $21.1 million semi-annual interest payment due March 15, 2016 on the 6.50% Senior Notes due September 2020 and a $50.0 million semi-annual interest payment due March 15, 2016 on the 10.00% Senior Secured Second Lien [Notes]."

10-K verbatim: "$945 million under the 2013 Revolver, the maximum amount available, for general corporate purposes" — drew the entire facility February 2016 ahead of expected default.

10-K verbatim: "going concern basis and do not include any adjustments that might result from uncertainty about our ability to continue as a going concern, other than the reclassification of certain long-term debt and the related debt issuance costs to current liabilities… The report from our independent registered public accounting firm on our consolidated financial statements for the year [contained going-concern qualification]."

→ Auditor going-concern qualification + missed coupon + maxed revolver = textbook **distressed**. Single canonical spec value. Total long-term debt ~$6.3B; trough coal prices (Newcastle thermal $50/t, Powder River Basin $11/t) made debt service impossible.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature; archetype-matching only)

Peabody acquired MacArthur Coal (Australian met-coal) for $5.2B in 2011 at the top of the China commodity cycle. Met-coal prices collapsed 75% by 2015. Patriot Coal spinoff (2007) re-emerged as legacy-cost burden — Patriot filed Ch11 a second time May 2015; UMWA pension/healthcare claims fell partly back on Peabody. April 13, 2016 Ch11 filing in E.D. Missouri. Emerged April 2017 post-restructuring.

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = departed; founder_insider_stake_direction = departed)

External record: Peabody Energy traces to 1883 (Francis S. Peabody, Chicago coal-delivery business) — no living founder by FY15. Modern public-company structure dates to 2001 IPO. Boyce CEO 2006-2015; transitioned to Executive Chairman May 2015.

10-K verbatim Kellow bio: "Kellow has extensive experience in the global resource industry, where he has served in multiple executive, operational and financial roles in coal and other commodities in the United States, Australia and… Kellow served in a number of roles with BHP Billiton, the world's largest mining company, including senior appointments as President, Aluminum and Nickel (2012-2013), President, Stainless Steel Materials [...]"

→ **founder_in_place = "departed"** (single canonical spec value). Kellow is a competent operator-CEO but he is not a founder; spec definition of "replaced-by-competent" applies when an original founder is replaced by a competent operator — here the relevant lineage (1883 founder) is too distant. Boyce's transition from CEO to Executive Chairman during the trough year is best mapped to "departed" rather than "yes."

Stake direction: no documented founder open-market purchases. Spec value **"departed"** (single canonical from ["increasing","flat","decreasing","departed"]).

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

10-K verbatim: "Total revenues $5,609" (FY15, $M) vs "$6,792" (FY14) = **-17.4% y/y**.

10-K verbatim: "$1,183.0 million, primarily due to lower realized coal pricing and lower sales volumes driven by demand and production factors described in this risk factor" — asset impairment charge.

→ revenue_trajectory **declining** (single canonical spec value); margin_trajectory **deteriorating** with $1.18B impairment + multi-quarter EBITDA collapse. Net loss FY15 ~$2.0B (impairment-driven).

## F. Operational shock specific to BTU — China met-coal cycle inversion + US gas substitution + regulatory ratchet

Triple shock: (1) China steel-demand peak (2013-14) inverted, killing met-coal exports; (2) US natural gas at $2.63/mmBtu (2015) drove utilities to switch off thermal coal; (3) EPA MATS + Clean Power Plan (Aug 2015) + ozone NAAQS (Oct 2015) forced retirement of US coal capacity. Combined with $5.2B 2011 MacArthur acquisition leverage — Ch11 inevitable. Filed April 13, 2016. Emerged April 3, 2017 (post-restructuring).
