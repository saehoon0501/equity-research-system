## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

The 2008-09 GFC produced (1) U.S. light-vehicle SAAR collapse from ~16M units (mid-decade) to ~10.4M (2009 trough) — worst since early 1980s; (2) auto-loan and dealer floor-plan financing freeze tied to broader credit-market dislocation; (3) GM and Chrysler bankruptcies + TARP auto-bailout concurrent with Ford's recovery. This is `weakening` (single canonical spec value) — cyclical demand + credit shock, not `structural-decline` (the U.S. auto category survived and recovered). Not `reversed` (the secular ICE-vehicle thesis remained intact at the FY2008 lens; EV/structural disruption is a 2020s story).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = 12-24mo)

The pre-funding decision in November 2006 is the load-bearing event: under newly hired CEO Alan Mulally, Ford mortgaged virtually every hard and soft asset including the Blue Oval trademark to secure $23.6B in term loans + revolving credit (originally targeted $18B; oversubscribed to $23.6B) — at the time the world's largest industrial debt package. Mulally's stated rationale: "a cushion to protect for a recession or other unexpected event." This pre-funding was BEFORE the GFC, with no foresight of the specific shock — pure conservative liquidity hedging. By YE2008 Ford had ~$13.4B automotive gross cash + $24-25.5B total available liquidity despite $14.7B net loss + $21.2B negative automotive gross cash flow during 2008. Spec value `12-24mo` (single canonical value) — sufficient runway to avoid Ch11/TARP through the trough but materially stressed.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature)

Mulally's "One Ford" plan: divest Aston Martin (2007), Jaguar/Land Rover (2008 to Tata), Volvo (2010 to Geely); consolidate global platforms; restore product cadence. Avoidance of TARP is widely cited as the moat-preserving move — Ford emerged from the crisis with brand equity intact while GM and Chrysler bore the stigma of bankruptcy. This is sector-extension `moat_state` territory — for universal-core extraction, ignore the One-Ford thesis; use only FY2008 demand/credit/liquidity framing.

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = replaced-by-competent; founder_insider_stake_direction = flat)

Founder Henry Ford founded Ford Motor Company in 1903 (Detroit); died 1947. The Ford family retains Class B super-voting shares (~40% voting power despite ~2% economic stake, per the dual-class structure established at 1956 IPO). Bill Ford Jr. (great-grandson) served as CEO 2001-2006, then transitioned to Executive Chairman concurrent with hiring Alan Mulally (CEO Sep-2006, from Boeing Commercial Airplanes). Mulally executed the Nov-2006 pre-funding + One-Ford restructuring + the strategic decision to decline TARP. Spec values: `founder_in_place = replaced-by-competent` (Mulally, professionally hired, with founder-family aligned via Bill Ford Jr. on Board — single canonical value); `founder_insider_stake_direction = flat` (Class B structure maintained through trough; no documented Ford-family share liquidation; Bill Ford Jr.'s transition was a role change, not a stake change).

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = declining)

NBC News + S&P: 2008 net loss $14.7B (record) vs 2007 $2.7B net loss prior record. Total 2008 revenue $146B (-15.2% YoY, -$26B from 2007). Q4-2008 net loss $5.9B alone. 2008 negative automotive gross cash flow $21.2B. Cost-cutting: $4.4B savings 2008, projected $4B further 2009; 10K white-collar cuts + buyout offers to all 75K UAW employees. FY2008 trajectory unambiguously `deteriorating` (margin) and `declining` (revenue) — single canonical spec values. (Recovery: 2009 net income +$2.7B; revenue $118.3B = -14.3% — this is FY10-context and not used for FY2008 trough-lens features.)

## F. Operational shock specific to Ford — pre-funded liquidity bet (Nov-2006)

The defining idiosyncratic feature: Mulally's Nov-2006 decision to mortgage the Blue Oval and raise $23.6B BEFORE the shock arrived. Comparable-firm GM did not pre-fund and required TARP + Ch11. Chrysler-1981 is the closest historical analog (replaced-by-competent + product-pivot + survival) but Ford-2009 is the distinct "pre-funded balance sheet beats government rescue" pattern — the canonical SURVIVOR archetype for the GFC-nonfin sector when paired with a competent operator and aligned founder family.
