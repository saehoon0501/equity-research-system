## A. Industry shock & operational stress  (industry_tailwind = weakening)

GFC consumer-discretionary pullback hit the $4 latte directly — the "lipstick effect" worked against premium coffee in a way it did not for cheaper indulgences. McDonald's McCafe rollout (2008) added competitive pressure at the value tier exactly when SBUX traffic was softening. This is `weakening` (single canonical spec value) — cyclical consumer + competitive intensification, not `structural-decline` (the specialty-coffee category survived and grew post-GFC). Not `reversed` (the secular premiumization-of-coffee trend remained intact).

## B. Balance sheet & cash position  (cash_runway = >24mo)

Starbucks entered FY2008 with no liquidity crisis — modest long-term debt (~$550M), ~$10.4B revenue base, multi-year-positive operating cash flow even at trough. Restructuring charges expected $328-348M pre-tax with only ~$100M cash outflow (per fiscal-2009 disclosure) due to tax benefits + non-cash impairments. The trough was a profitability + same-store-sales problem, NOT a solvency problem — categorically different from Drexel-1990 / Chrysler-1981 (`distressed`) or Ford-2008 (`12-24mo` despite $24B liquidity, because of the $14.7B loss magnitude). SBUX cash runway = `>24mo` (single canonical spec value) — operating-cash-flow positive throughout the trough.

## C. Strategic context (NOT a universal-core feature)

Schultz's "Transformation Agenda" Mar-2008: closed 7,100 U.S. stores for 3.5 hours simultaneously Feb-26-2008 for barista espresso retraining (the iconic "Coffee Master" + "Pour Your Heart Into It" cultural reset). Closed 600 underperforming U.S. stores announced Jul-1-2008; another 300 announced 2009 with 6,700 layoffs. Streamlined SKU rationalization, eliminated breakfast sandwiches (briefly), accelerated VIA instant + Pike Place Roast launches. This is sector-extension `moat_state` territory; not used for universal-core extraction.

## D. Founder/insider behavior  (founder_in_place = replaced-by-competent; founder_insider_stake_direction = flat)

Howard Schultz is the de-facto founder of Starbucks-as-it-exists: original founders Jerry Baldwin / Zev Siegl / Gordon Bowker started a whole-bean retailer 1971; Schultz was hired as Director of Retail Operations 1982, raised funds for Il Giornale 1986, then acquired the Starbucks retail unit from the original founders for $3.8M in 1987. He was CEO until 2000, stepped to Chairman, then returned as CEO Jan-8-2008 after the Board ousted Jim Donald. Catalog framing: `replaced-by-competent` — Schultz IS the founder, but the catalog frames the trough event as the "replacement of an underperforming professional CEO with a competent founder-operator," which is the load-bearing axis (single canonical spec value from the spec domain). Stake direction: no documented Form-4 stake increase or decrease through trough — `flat` (single canonical spec value); Schultz's prior $1.5B stake was structurally maintained.

## E. Margin & revenue trajectory  (margin_trajectory = deteriorating; revenue_trajectory = growing)

Per CNN Money 2008-11-10 + Starbucks fiscal-2008 annual report: FY2008 net revenues $10.4B vs $9.4B FY2007 = +10% (revenue trajectory technically `growing` at FY2008 close — the spec value is mechanically determined by the y/y revenue delta, single canonical spec value, not the catalog narrative). FY2008 EPS $0.43 vs $0.87 = -51% — sharp earnings deterioration. Q4-FY08 U.S. comparable-store sales -8%; FY2008 global comp -3% / U.S. -5% / international +2%. Q3-FY08 (13 wks ended Jun-29-2008) restructuring charges $167.7M from store-asset impairments tied to 600-store-closure announcement; total expected pre-tax charges $328-348M across FY08+FY09. Margin trajectory `deteriorating` unambiguously. Important note: the catalog narrative anchors on the comparable-sales decline + earnings collapse, but the universal-core `revenue_trajectory` feature is mechanically the y/y total-revenue direction — `growing` here is the correct spec value despite the operational distress, which is exactly why margin_trajectory + comp-sales context is the load-bearing signal for this case rather than top-line direction.

## F. Operational shock specific to SBUX — Schultz return + cultural reset

The Jan-8-2008 CEO return — preceded by Schultz's leaked Feb-2007 "commoditization of the Starbucks experience" memo to Donald — is the canonical "founder-returns-to-rescue" event. The 600-store closure was the first time Starbucks publicly admitted overexpansion; the Feb-26-2008 simultaneous-store-close for retraining was a brand-equity reset that defined the "founder back at the helm" archetype. Direct precedent: Steve Jobs return to Apple 1997 (canonical case for the `replaced-by-competent` + `>24mo cash` + `weakening (not reversed)` SURVIVOR pattern).
