# L2 — Probabilistic Scenario Writing (3 / 5 / 10 year horizons)

Empirical-domain knowledge library, lane L2. Focus: how practitioners actually write multi-year scenarios, what makes a scenario falsifiable vs decorative, the empirical track record of multi-year forecasting, common failure modes, probabilistic branching techniques, and updating cadence.

## Section A — Curated sources

### Academic / track-record evidence (Tier 1)

- [How Well Do Economists Forecast Recessions? (IMF WP/18/39, An, Loungani, Jalles)](https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WP/Issues/2018/03/05/How-Well-Do-Economists-Forecast-Recessions-45672) — Systematic study finds neither private nor official forecasters reliably call recessions in advance; even within recession years, magnitude is dramatically underestimated until late. [Tier 1]
- [Philip E. Tetlock — Wikipedia summary of EPJ + IARPA tournament results](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_E._Tetlock) — Synthesizes the 284-expert / 28,000-forecast study showing experts barely beat chance on long-horizon political/economic questions; foxes outperform hedgehogs. [Tier 1]
- [The Good Judgment Project — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Good_Judgment_Project) — Documents the IARPA tournament where median GJP forecasts beat the control group by 60% in year one and ~80% in year two; superforecasters beat classified-access analysts by ~30%. [Tier 1]

## Section B — Distilled patterns

1. **At horizons of 5+ years, point forecasts of macro variables are barely better than chance.** Tetlock's 18-year study of 284 experts and 28,000 forecasts found accuracy "declined toward chance five years out"; on geopolitical/economic questions ten years out there is "no evidence forecasters can predict anything beyond the excruciatingly obvious" (Tetlock per [Wikipedia EPJ summary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_E._Tetlock); [Long Now talk](https://longnow.org/talks/02007-tetlock/)).

2. **Recession calls fail predictably: forecasters miss the onset and dramatically underestimate magnitude.** IMF analysis of private + official forecasters across recessions finds that, by April of a recession year, forecasts revise toward calling a downturn — but the predicted contraction is far milder than what occurs ([IMF WP/18/39](https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WP/Issues/2018/03/05/How-Well-Do-Economists-Forecast-Recessions-45672)).

3. **Macro-hedge-fund track records corroborate that point macro forecasting is not reliably profitable.** HFRI macro funds returned 2.8% annualized over 10 years through July 2022 vs 13.8% for the S&P 500; Marks uses this as direct evidence against forecast-driven strategies ([Oaktree, Illusion of Knowledge](https://www.oaktreecapital.com/insights/memo/the-illusion-of-knowledge)).

4. **Valuation-based 10-year forecasts have meaningful predictive power for asset-class returns even when point macro forecasts fail.** Hussman documents 0.9+ correlation between price/revenue and MarketCap/GVA vs subsequent 10-12yr S&P total returns ([Hussman, Aug 2022](https://www.hussmanfunds.com/comment/mc220815/)). Shiller-CAPE explained ~85% of variation in subsequent 10yr returns since 1975 (R² ~0.43 for 1926-2011, suggesting period-dependent reliability per [Advisor Perspectives](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/articles/2020/07/20/the-remarkable-accuracy-of-cape-as-a-predictor-of-returns-1) and [Wikipedia CAPE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclically_adjusted_price-to-earnings_ratio)). The signal is regime/level, not timing.

5. **Foxes (eclectic, multi-model, probabilistic) beat hedgehogs (one-grand-theory, high-conviction) — especially at long horizons.** Tetlock's signature finding is that the more theoretically committed an expert, the worse their long-horizon record, particularly within their own domain of expertise ([Long Now](https://longnow.org/talks/02007-tetlock/); [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_E._Tetlock)).

6. **Superforecasters use granular probabilities (60/40 vs 55/45), not verbal hedges.** Good Judgment's IARPA winners distinguished probability gradations beyond traditional 7-point verbal scales; this finer resolution is a measured component of their accuracy ([GJP Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Good_Judgment_Project); [Ten Commandments via FS.blog](https://fs.blog/ten-commandments-for-superforecasters/)).

7. **Incremental Bayesian updating outperforms big jumps; "small steps to accuracy" is empirically validated.** Tetlock's commandment: "Belief updating is to good forecasting as brushing and flossing are to good dental hygiene" — and the corollary, that superforecasters revise frequently in small increments rather than waiting for a single large signal ([Good Judgment Ten Commandments](https://goodjudgment.com/philip-tetlocks-10-commandments-of-superforecasting/)).

8. **Long-horizon forecasts systematically overreact, not underreact.** Empirical work on belief formation shows long-term expectations overreact to good news (extrapolation error), driving boom-bust dynamics; the intuition that long-horizon forecasts are "averaging out" is wrong ([Bordalo-Gennaioli-La Porta-Shleifer](https://scholar.harvard.edu/sites/scholar.harvard.edu/files/shleifer/files/bgls_june16.pdf); [NBER 24932](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w24932/w24932.pdf)).

9. **Scenarios are most useful when they change the writer's mental model — not when they predict the right numbers.** Shell's institutional view (Wilkinson/Kupers) is that scenarios that look "wrong" ex post still earn their cost if they break the assumption that the future resembles the present ([HBR 2013](https://hbr.org/2013/05/living-in-the-futures); [Wikipedia Scenario Planning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scenario_planning)).

10. **The 2-3 scenario count is empirically optimal; more dilutes commitment, fewer collapses to a forecast.** Shell's 50+ years of practice converged on 2-3 primary narratives drawn down from 7-9 mini-scenarios — this is the methodological default ([Wikipedia Scenario Planning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scenario_planning)).

11. **Falsifiable scenarios require pre-committed kill criteria written before market pressure.** Klein's premortem and Mauboussin's pre-investment premortem both rely on writing failure conditions ex ante; once price action begins, motivated reasoning makes objective evaluation nearly impossible ([HBR Klein 2007](https://hbr.org/2007/09/performing-a-project-premortem); [Acquirer's Multiple on Mauboussin](https://acquirersmultiple.com/2020/07/michael-mauboussin-perform-a-premortem-before-you-invest/)).

12. **Prospective hindsight ("imagine it failed — why?") increases identified failure modes by ~30%.** Mitchell-Russo-Pennington 1989 found that switching from "what could go wrong" to "it has already gone wrong" measurably increases reasons identified, the empirical foundation for premortems ([The Uncertainty Project](https://www.theuncertaintyproject.org/tools/pre-mortem); [Klein HBR](https://hbr.org/2007/09/performing-a-project-premortem)).

13. **Reference-class (outside view) forecasting beats inside view by curbing optimism bias.** Flyvbjerg's Edinburgh tram case: inside view forecast £320m, reference-class adjusted to £400m, actual £776m — outside view was meaningfully closer ([Wikipedia RCF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reference_class_forecasting)). Mauboussin advocates the same in equity work: ask "how often have similar setups produced this outcome?" ([Acquirer's Multiple Mauboussin](https://acquirersmultiple.com/2020/07/michael-mauboussin-perform-a-premortem-before-you-invest/)).

14. **Reflexive (path-dependent) frameworks dominate when narrative drives flows.** Soros's reflexivity says scenarios cannot be written as a one-shot probability tree because the act of believing the scenario changes the fundamentals it is built on; this is why bubble exits cannot be timed by valuation alone ([Soros transcript](https://www.opensocietyfoundations.org/uploads/9ae17912-2262-4646-8ffc-d01afc934c36/george-soros-general-theory-of-reflexivity-transcript.pdf); [Macro Ops](https://macro-ops.com/understanding-george-soross-theory-of-reflexivity-in-markets/)).

15. **Narrative tracking is a measurable input, not just rhetoric.** Shiller's Narrative Economics argues stories spread epidemiologically and observably drive consumption / investment / saving; scenario writers should track which narratives are gaining/losing share, not assume narratives are exogenous ([Narrative Economics](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691182292/narrative-economics)).

16. **Practitioner bubble-callers define success modestly: "out of the market when needed," not "called the top."** Grantham explicitly: "sooner or later there will come a time when an investor is pleased to have been out of the market" — the falsifiable claim is reversion to trend, not date precision ([Waiting for the Last Dance](https://www.gmo.com/americas/research-library/waiting-for-the-last-dance/)).

17. **The 3P test (possible / plausible / probable) prunes scenario sets.** Damodaran's discipline: many narratives are possible, fewer plausible, and only a few probable; force every scenario through this filter and convert to value drivers, otherwise it is decorative ([Narrative and Numbers](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/narrative-and-numbers/9780231180481/)).

18. **The decomposition Long-Term Return = Fundamental Growth + Valuation Re-rating + Dividend Yield is a useful scenario-writing scaffold for 5-10yr horizons.** Hussman's identity forces the writer to specify which lever drives the return — and any scenario that requires all three to compound favorably should be down-weighted ([Hussman, Estimating Long-Term Return](https://www.hussmanfunds.com/html/longterm.htm); [Hussman, Aug 2022](https://www.hussmanfunds.com/comment/mc220815/)).

19. **Confidence intervals from human forecasters are systematically too narrow; correction factor of 1.5-2× is empirically motivated.** Across calibration studies, nominal 99% intervals contain truth ~65% of the time; experts often *worsen* calibration because their narratives are more internally coherent than the world ([Wikipedia EPJ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_E._Tetlock) on expert overconfidence).

20. **Scenarios should be written in the second-level form: "range × probability × consensus delta," not as point forecasts.** Marks's framing — what is the range of outcomes, what probabilities do I assign, and how does the current price comport with consensus — is the operational form of the academic literature on probabilistic forecasting ([Oaktree, I Beg to Differ](https://www.oaktreecapital.com/insights/memo/i-beg-to-differ)).

## Section C — Open questions / disagreements

- **Mean-reversion (GMO/Hussman/Grantham) vs reflexivity (Soros) vs "we just don't know" (Marks).** GMO's 7-year forecasts and Hussman's models assume valuation multiples and margins revert to historical norms within a definable window (typically 7-15 years). Soros explicitly denies that markets are mean-reverting in any clean sense — they are path-dependent and the act of forecasting changes the path. Marks, more agnostic, argues both views overclaim: we cannot know the macro future; we can only know where we sit in the cycle. There is no resolution; the three frameworks generate different scenario sets for the same starting state. Sources: [GMO 7-Year Forecast](https://www.gmo.com/americas/research-library/gmo-7-year-asset-class-forecast-1q-2025_gmo7yearassetclassforecast/); [Hussman Aug 2022](https://www.hussmanfunds.com/comment/mc220815/); [Soros transcript](https://www.opensocietyfoundations.org/uploads/9ae17912-2262-4646-8ffc-d01afc934c36/george-soros-general-theory-of-reflexivity-transcript.pdf); [Oaktree Illusion of Knowledge](https://www.oaktreecapital.com/insights/memo/the-illusion-of-knowledge).

- **Are valuation signals like CAPE truly stable forecasters, or have they been overfit?** Wikipedia's CAPE entry summarizes Siegel's critique that 1990s accounting changes inflated CAPE readings; Vanguard finds R² of 0.43 for 1926-2011 vs 0.85+ since 1975 — implying structure-dependence. Shiller and Hussman defend the relationship; Siegel and parts of Vanguard's research argue the multiple is regime-conditional. Sources: [Wikipedia CAPE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclically_adjusted_price-to-earnings_ratio); [Advisor Perspectives](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/articles/2020/07/20/the-remarkable-accuracy-of-cape-as-a-predictor-of-returns-1).

- **Underreaction vs overreaction in expectations.** Classical "post-earnings-drift" literature shows investors *underreact* to fresh information; Bordalo-Gennaioli-La Porta-Shleifer and the broader Coibion-Gorodnichenko literature show long-horizon expectations *overreact*. Both can be true simultaneously: short horizon underreact, long horizon overreact. Source: [BGLS](https://scholar.harvard.edu/sites/scholar.harvard.edu/files/shleifer/files/bgls_june16.pdf); [NBER 24932](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w24932/w24932.pdf).

- **Does "strong opinions weakly held" actually work?** Saffo's heuristic is widely adopted in tech/macro; Tetlock-style probabilistic thinking suggests the framework collapses opinion strength and probability granularity — a 60/40 forecast is structurally different from a "strong opinion." Practitioners disagree on whether the heuristic helps or hurts calibration. (Search-level finding; representative critique cited in earlier search results.)

- **Quantitative scenario probabilities — useful or fake precision?** Damodaran's 3P / probability-weighted DCF, Mauboussin's expected-value framework, and Bridgewater's "believability-weighted" models all assign explicit probabilities. Marks and Taleb argue these probabilities are mostly noise — the right approach is to invest only when scenarios converge, not to compute a single weighted value. The practitioner literature does not resolve this. Sources: [Narrative and Numbers](https://cup.columbia.edu/book/narrative-and-numbers/9780231180481/); [Oaktree Illusion of Knowledge](https://www.oaktreecapital.com/insights/memo/the-illusion-of-knowledge).
