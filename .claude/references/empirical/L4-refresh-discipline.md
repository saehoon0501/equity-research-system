# L4 — View-Refresh Discipline

**Lane:** How practitioners balance "shift the view when warranted" against "stay disciplined to the thesis." Anchor-drift defense, trigger-based re-evaluation, pre-mortem cadence, and the empirical case on hold-loser-too-long vs cut-winner-too-early.

## Section A — Curated sources

### Academic / primary research (Tier 1)

- [Are Investors Reluctant to Realize Their Losses? — Odean (1998, J. Finance)](https://faculty.haas.berkeley.edu/odean/papers%20current%20versions/areinvestorsreluctant.pdf) — Seminal disposition-effect study: 10k brokerage accounts 1987–1993, gains realized ~60% more often than losses (PGR 14.8% vs PLR 9.8% ex-Dec). [Tier 1]
- [Disposition Effect — Wikipedia summary of empirical findings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disposition_effect) — Synthesizes Shefrin-Statman (1985) origin, Odean numbers, Seru/Shumway/Stoffman (2010) 3.2–5.7% return cost, Feng-Seasholes (2005) 67% lower disposition effect among sophisticated investors. [Tier 1]
- [Trading Is Hazardous to Your Wealth — Barber & Odean (2000, J. Finance)](https://faculty.haas.berkeley.edu/odean/papers%20current%20versions/individual_investor_performance_final.pdf) — 66,465 households 1991–96: most-active traders earn 11.4% vs 17.9% market; stocks they sell outperform stocks they buy. The empirical case against constant view-shifting. [Tier 1]
- [Back to the Future: Temporal Perspective in Explanation of Events — Mitchell, Russo & Pennington (1989, J. Behavioral Decision Making)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/bdm.3960020103) — Foundational "prospective hindsight" study: imagining outcome already occurred increases ability to identify correct reasons by ~30%. The empirical basis for pre-mortems. [Tier 1]
- [Performing a Project Premortem — Klein (2007, Harvard Business Review)](https://hbr.org/2007/09/performing-a-project-premortem) — Klein's HBR formalization. Pre-mortem can roughly double the number of risks identified vs traditional brainstorming; 20–30 min protocol. [Tier 1]
- [Incremental Belief Updaters Are Better Forecasters — Mellers et al. (Wharton/OBHDP)](https://faculty.wharton.upenn.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/1-s2.0-S0749597819300949-main.pdf) — From Good Judgment Project data: forecasters who make frequent small updates (e.g., 0.40 → 0.35) outperform those making rare large updates; one SD smaller update magnitude → 0.12 lower (better) standardized Brier score. [Tier 1]
- [Expert Political Judgment — Tetlock (Princeton, 2005)](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178288/expert-political-judgment) — 18-year study, 284 experts, 28k forecasts: hedgehogs (one big idea) underperform foxes (multi-framework, comfortable with "however"). Foundational anti-anchor evidence. [Tier 1]
- [The Ostrich Effect: Selective Attention to Information — Galai & Sade (2006), summarized by CMU](https://www.cmu.edu/news/stories/archives/2015/december/ostrich-effect.html) — Investors check portfolios more often during gains than losses; the avoidance of bad news is empirically measurable and stable across individuals — directly relevant to "anchor drift" because unobserved positions don't trigger re-evaluation. [Tier 1]
- [Sunk-Cost Fallacy in VC Staging (J. Banking & Finance, 2024)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0929119924000518) — 30,602 follow-on investment decisions 2009–19: prior capital committed and prior monitoring intensity both raise probability of continued investment, controlling for fundamentals. Cleanly identifies sunk-cost-driven anchor drift. [Tier 1]
- [Sunk-Cost Effects on Acquisitions — Guenzel/Wharton](https://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/article/how-sunk-costs-affect-firms-investment-decisions/) — Acquisition sunk-cost attachment lowers subsequent divestiture rates by 8–9%; the firm-level mirror of investor disposition effect. [Tier 1]
- [Noise: A Flaw in Human Judgment — Kahneman, Sibony & Sunstein (2021), summary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noise:_A_Flaw_in_Human_Judgment) — Independent-aggregation as decision hygiene; level/pattern/occasion noise typology directly applicable to repeated thesis re-checks. [Tier 1]

## Section B — Distilled patterns

1. **Disposition effect is large and well-replicated; the cost is real, not just psychological.** Odean (1998) measured ~50% asymmetry in realization rates and a ~3.4%/yr penalty on the held-loser vs sold-winner pair (sources: [Odean 1998](https://faculty.haas.berkeley.edu/odean/papers%20current%20versions/areinvestorsreluctant.pdf), [Disposition Effect Wiki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disposition_effect)). For the disposition spread specifically across markets, Seru/Shumway/Stoffman estimate 3.2–5.7% annual return cost (source: [Disposition Effect Wiki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disposition_effect)). Treat disposition risk as a quantified factor, not a soft-skill issue.

2. **Sophistication and experience reduce, but do not eliminate, the disposition effect.** Feng & Seasholes find experienced investors are 67% less prone — but still prone (source: [Disposition Effect Wiki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Disposition_effect)). Implication: design the process to be robust to a residual ~1/3 of the bias even in experienced operators.

3. **High turnover empirically destroys returns; "shift the view often" is not free.** Barber & Odean find the most-active retail quintile underperforms the market by ~6.5%/yr; the stocks they sell outperform the stocks they buy (source: [Barber & Odean 2000](https://faculty.haas.berkeley.edu/odean/papers%20current%20versions/individual_investor_performance_final.pdf)). The empirical default should be "raise the bar to act," not "raise the bar to hold."

4. **Pre-commit invalidation/kill criteria at entry, in writing.** Annie Duke's framework — explicit "state + date" conditions ("if X is or isn't true by date Y, exit") — works because it externalizes the exit decision when no emotion is present (source: [Behavioral Scientist — Duke](https://behavioralscientist.org/annie-duke-quit-mental-models-to-help-you-cut-your-losses/), [Cowen Partners summary](https://cowenpartners.com/annie-duke-quit-book-power-of-quitting/)). Without an ex-ante rule, sunk-cost dynamics dominate the in-position decision.

5. **Pre-mortems double the count of identified risks vs unstructured brainstorming, in 20–30 minutes.** Klein (2007) HBR result, building on Mitchell-Russo-Pennington (1989), which found a ~30% gain in correctly identifying outcome causes when imagining the outcome has already occurred (sources: [Klein HBR 2007](https://hbr.org/2007/09/performing-a-project-premortem), [Mitchell et al. 1989](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/bdm.3960020103)). Frequency-wise, the literature does not show a strong dose-response for monthly vs quarterly vs annual; what's established is that ad-hoc has the lowest yield because the worst time to do a pre-mortem is during a drawdown.

6. **Frequent small belief updates outperform rare large updates.** Mellers et al. (Good Judgment Project) — incremental updaters dominate; one SD reduction in update size correlates with a 0.12 better Brier score (source: [Wharton/OBHDP](https://faculty.wharton.upenn.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/1-s2.0-S0749597819300949-main.pdf)). Translation for investors: refresh probabilities by single-digit percentage points on every catalyst, rather than waiting for a large narrative shift to flip the position. This is the empirical anti-anchor technique.

7. **Foxes beat hedgehogs at long-horizon forecasting; multi-framework reasoning beats one-grand-theory thesis.** Tetlock's 18-year, 284-expert study: confident, story-driven hedgehogs underperform "however"-laden foxes (source: [Expert Political Judgment](https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691178288/expert-political-judgment)). For thesis discipline, this argues against "the elegant five-bullet thesis" and for explicit competing-hypotheses tracking.

8. **The ostrich effect is a measurable threat to refresh discipline, not a personality trait.** Investors verifiably reduce portfolio-checks during drawdowns (source: [CMU/Galai-Sade](https://www.cmu.edu/news/stories/archives/2015/december/ostrich-effect.html)). The refresh process must be calendar-triggered, not curiosity-triggered, or it self-disables exactly when needed.

9. **Sunk-cost effects are visible in disciplined institutional capital, not just retail.** VC staging study (30,602 decisions) shows prior capital committed and prior monitoring intensity each independently raise the probability of follow-on funding, controlling for fundamentals (source: [J. Banking & Finance 2024](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0929119924000518)). M&A divestiture rates fall 8–9% from prior-acquisition sunk-cost attachment (source: [Wharton/Guenzel](https://knowledge.wharton.upenn.edu/article/how-sunk-costs-affect-firms-investment-decisions/)). Sophisticated processes still need explicit kill-criteria; sophistication alone doesn't fix it.

10. **Thesis drift is not "thesis update" — distinguish them explicitly.** MicroCapClub's argument: when the original thesis is falsified, the lesson isn't that you have a new thesis; it's that your model produced a wrong prediction and you should exit, not re-underwrite (source: [MicroCapClub](https://microcapclub.com/the-perils-of-thesis-drift/)). Operational rule: a thesis change that arrives _after_ a stock has fallen and _conveniently rationalizes holding_ should be rejected by default.

11. **Process-vs-outcome separation is a precondition for refresh discipline.** Mauboussin: short-term outcomes do not distinguish good process from bad; an investor who refreshes views in response to short-term P&L is updating on noise (source: [Mauboussin/CFA Institute](https://blogs.cfainstitute.org/investor/2012/07/25/decision-making-for-investors-under-uncertainty/)). The refresh trigger should be _new information about the world_, not _new information about the price_.

12. **Decision journals improve calibration over time.** Recording ex-ante reasoning prevents hindsight rewriting and is associated with measurable accuracy improvement (one cited 2024 study: 19% better forecasting accuracy among managers using decision journals) (source: [Farnam Street](https://fs.blog/decision-journal/)). The journal lets you separate "I was wrong about A but right about B" from "I was wrong overall."

13. **Checklists work because they interrupt overconfidence at predictable moments.** Pabrai's ~60-item checklist (modeled on Gawande's surgical-checklist evidence base) catches the recurring failure modes — especially leverage, management quality, and balance-sheet items — that get glossed over under conviction (sources: [Pabrai checklist](https://www.oldschoolvalue.com/investment-tools/mohnish-pabrai-checklist-investor/), [Gawande](https://www.valueinvestingworld.com/2009/09/checklist-by-atul-gawande.html)). For refresh discipline, a re-evaluation checklist should mirror entry-checklist items, not free-form "how do I feel about this name now."

14. **Inversion is structurally different from devil's-advocacy and steelmanning, and worth doing in addition.** Munger's inversion: ask "what would have to be true for this to be wrong" before "is this still right" — the former is harder to confirmation-bias around (source: [25iq/Munger](https://25iq.com/2015/09/12/a-dozen-things-ive-learned-from-charlie-munger-about-inversion-including-the-importance-of-being-consistently-not-stupid-2/)). Steelmanning the bear case (rather than weakly attacking it) is the second-best substitute (source: [LinqAlpha/AWS](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/machine-learning/how-linqalpha-assesses-investment-theses-using-devils-advocate-on-amazon-bedrock/)).

15. **Catalyst calendars convert "discretionary refresh" into "scheduled refresh."** Buy-side practice in event-driven mandates: every position has an explicit list of upcoming events that will produce evidence, with pre-registered "what would shift the view" notes (source: [Bloomberg/biotech catalyst calendar](https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/insights/markets/bi-biotech-pharma-catalyst-calendar-solution-for-tracking-milestone-timelines/)). This neutralizes ostrich-effect avoidance because the refresh trigger is exogenous.

16. **The Marks/Druckenmiller polarity is real and unresolved by data; it may be a regime-conditional choice.** Marks/Klarman: hold through volatility, capitulation in drawdowns is the central enemy (sources: [Marks "Illusion"](https://www.oaktreecapital.com/insights/memo/the-illusion-of-knowledge), [Klarman/Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/notes/margin-of-safety-by-seth-klarman/)). Druckenmiller/Soros: the moment the buy-reason has changed, exit, even at a loss, even if the stock could recover (sources: [Morgan Stanley/Druckenmiller](https://www.morganstanley.com/insights/videos/hard-lessons/duquesne-stan-druckenmiller-iliana-bouzali), [Soros](https://www.goodreads.com/author/quotes/76751.George_Soros)). Both positions have track-record support; the empirical literature does not pick a winner. See Section C.

17. **Buffett's "20-punch-card" is a thought experiment about scarcity of acting, not about scarcity of refreshing.** The framework is rhetorical — even Buffett does not literally limit himself to 20 — but the pattern it instantiates is real: raise the bar to act, including to exit (source: [Bronte Capital](http://brontecapital.blogspot.com/2016/09/comments-on-investment-philosophy-part.html)). A high refresh-frequency on _evidence_ is consistent with a low action-frequency on _trades_.

18. **Independent-aggregation reduces noise in repeated thesis assessments.** Kahneman/Sibony/Sunstein recommend collecting view-refreshes from team members independently before discussing, to avoid social-influence cascade (source: [Noise wiki summary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noise:_A_Flaw_in_Human_Judgment)). Operationally: each analyst writes their own quarterly re-underwrite before a group meeting, not after.

19. **The Ackman/Valeant case shows: anchor-drift escalates with public commitment.** Ackman publicly defended Valeant for ~18 months after the October 2015 SIRF/Philidor reporting; he held a board seat and had testified before Congress; the disconfirming-evidence accumulation (Khmelnitsky 2014, Chanos shorting, Hempton blog posts, Citron Oct 2015) was visible *months before* he exited the board in mid-March 2016 and *years* before the final equity-position exit in March 2017 (sources: [Globe and Mail](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/report-on-business/rob-magazine/inside-story-valeant-pharmaceuticals-fall/article34432530/), [SIRF Boyd](https://smartvestsecurities.com/sirf-data), [CNBC March 2017](https://www.cnbc.com/2017/03/29/bill-ackman-tells-investors-he-has-learned-from-his-valeant-mistake.html), [Fortune March 2017](https://fortune.com/2017/03/29/valeant-pharmaceuticals-stock-bill-ackman-pershing-square-hedge-fund-letter/)). The lesson: public-commitment and board-affiliation are independently powerful anchor-drift accelerators that warrant explicit countervailing process, not just "I'll keep an open mind."

20. **The Buffett 2020 airlines exit is the clean counter-example to "Buffett never sells."** At the May 2020 BRK AGM, Buffett articulated explicit invalidation: *the world had changed for airlines* such that even at 70-80% pre-COVID passenger recovery, the industry would carry "far too many planes," and management had not erred — the business had (sources: [Motley Fool 2020 AGM recap](https://www.fool.com/investing/2020/05/02/what-musgrave-buffett-said-at-berkshires-2020-shareh.aspx), [CNBC live updates](https://www.cnbc.com/2020/05/02/warren-buffett-berkshire-hathaway-annual-meeting-live-updates.html), [Fortune "reversing course again"](https://fortune.com/2020/05/02/warren-buffett-berkshire-airline-industry/)). The teaching point: the hold-through school still maintains an exit-trigger; the exit trigger is *demonstrated thesis-fact change*, not price-action change. Buffett did not sell because airlines were down; he sold because the demand-curve forecast had been overtaken by an event that broke his earnings model.

21. **Einhorn/GMCR shows: a thesis can be analytically right and operationally wrong.** Einhorn's 2011 short identified accounting and competitive-moat concerns at GMCR and listed explicit invalidation criteria (attachment rate >2 cups/brewer/day, FCF turn-positive, ROIC sustaining >16.3%); the patent expiry expected in 2012 didn't trigger competitor entry as predicted because management re-engineered the system to preserve lock-in (sources: [MarketFolly Einhorn presentation summary](https://www.marketfolly.com/2011/10/david-einhorn-short-green-mountain.html), [Quartz K-Cups recap](https://qz.com/292444/how-k-cups-defeated-a-legendary-hedge-fund-manager)). The discipline lesson: invalidation criteria must include *management response* as a degree-of-freedom, not just structural inevitability. Re-shorting in 2015 then taking the JAB-acquisition hit (+72% on news) is the canonical "thesis re-entry without re-establishing conviction structure" failure mode (source: [CNBC 2015 Einhorn/GMCR](https://www.cnbc.com/2015/12/07/greenlights-david-einhorn-gets-roasted-on-keurig-green-mountain-deal.html)).

22. **Ackman/Herbalife shows the inverse failure mode: 5-year thesis hold despite no confirming evidence.** Opened December 2012 at ~$1B short; FTC settled with Herbalife for $200M in July 2016 (a regulatory partial-win that *did not* validate the "pyramid scheme to zero" thesis); stock rallied 95% over the holding period; final exit was in February 2018 driven by price path — i.e., capital cost — not by thesis update (source: [CNBC Feb 2018 Ackman exit](https://www.cnbc.com/2018/02/28/ackman-exits-bet-against-herbalife.html)). Together with Valeant, this gives Ackman two canonical refresh-discipline failures of opposite sign: late-exit on a long where evidence accumulated against, and prolonged-hold on a short where evidence didn't accumulate for. Both are explicable as *anchor* problems: in Valeant, anchored to public commitment; in Herbalife, anchored to public conviction (the slide deck, the documentary, the Icahn feud).

23. **Pod-shop drawdown limits operationalize "pre-registered kill criteria" at firm scale.** Multi-strategy shops enforce explicit position/pod-level drawdown thresholds — typical structure at Millennium-style firms is 5% drawdown → halve allocated capital, 7.5% → terminate the pod (sources: [Wall Street Oasis multi-manager risk discussion](https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/forum/hedge-fund/multi-manager-risk-limits), [Young & Calculated multi-manager primer](https://youngandcalculated.substack.com/p/how-multi-manager-hedge-funds-actually)). At Citadel/Point72 the thresholds are individually negotiated but exist as enforceable contracts (source: [Quartr Citadel profile](https://quartr.com/insights/company-research/citadel-relentless-optimization-at-global-scale)). This is Annie Duke's "state+date kill criteria" implemented as enterprise risk infrastructure rather than personal discipline — and the cost is high turnover relative to discretionary single-manager funds. Choose the architecture appropriate to the strategy: pod-shop discipline outsources the kill-decision to a pre-committed rule, single-manager discipline requires the operator to internalize it.

24. **Bridgewater's "Daily Observations" is the operational forcing-function for daily refresh.** A formal written market-commentary discipline produced every business day for 50+ years (source: [Bridgewater 50 years](https://www.bridgewater.com/50-years-of-the-bridgewater-daily-observations)), reinforced by recorded-meeting and radical-transparency norms (source: [CNBC/Dalio](https://www.cnbc.com/2018/06/21/bridgewater-associates-ray-dalio-how-to-have-productive-meetings.html)). The mechanism: forcing daily written articulation prevents quiet anchor-drift because views must be re-stated, and the team-level radical-transparency norm prevents seniority-cascade in repeated refresh cycles. This is the institutional analog of personal decision-journaling.

25. **Druckenmiller's 4:30 AM chart-review and macro-regime probability is a personal-scale daily refresh ritual.** Per practitioner accounts (sources: [Antoine Buteau lessons](https://www.antoinebuteau.com/lessons-from-stanley-druckenmiller/), [Mr. Zepczynski summary](http://mrzepczynski.blogspot.com/2021/06/stanley-druckenmiller-on-exiting.html)): start of every day reviews 200+ charts of currencies, rates, commodities, and major equity groups; updates the "where is liquidity going" probability before any trading decision. The combination — daily refresh of macro-regime probability + the "exit when buy-reason changes" rule — is what keeps Druckenmiller's discretionary process robust against anchor-drift over decades.

26. **Sentiment is not one signal; market-level pessimism mean-reverts but firm-specific adverse language carries real fundamental content.** The "More Than Words" paper (2008) finds firm-specific negative-word fraction in news predicts lower future earnings and brief stock-price under-reaction (source: [Columbia/Tetlock-Saar-Tsechansky-Macskassy preprint](http://www.columbia.edu/~pt2238/papers/TSM_More_Than_Words_08_06.pdf)); the Fed's 2016 follow-up using the LM dictionary on Reuters news independently confirms negative-word fraction predicts cross-sectional returns over weeks-to-months (source: [Federal Reserve Board working paper](https://www.federalreserve.gov/econresdata/feds/2016/files/2016048pap.pdf)). Operational implication: market-level sentiment indices should be treated as price-action noise (do not refresh thesis on them); firm-level adverse news language should be treated as a real signal (do refresh on it). This is the empirical basis for distinguishing the two kinds of "new information."

27. **Social-media flow is a new and distinct anchor-drift contaminant.** Reddit/r-WSB-style discussion intensity has stronger predictive content for *abrupt* volatility than Twitter (source: [ScienceDirect r/wallstreetbets paper](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1057521924006537)); ~58% of Reddit-aware institutional investors (Brunswick 2023) reported decisions influenced by the platform; CFA-Institute identifies algorithm-curated feeds as confirmation-bias amplifiers that institutional shops must actively filter (source: [CFA Institute "Tweets to Trades"](https://rpc.cfainstitute.org/blogs/enterprising-investor/2024/from-tweets-to-trades-the-hidden-risks-of-social-media-in-investing)). Refresh-discipline implication: an explicit "what social-media exposure influenced this re-evaluation, and is that exposure aligned with my prior?" is a reasonable addition to the re-underwrite checklist.

28. **Catalyst-calendar discipline is the cleanest existence-proof of "scheduled refresh."** Biotech specialist funds and event-driven shops construct calendars of PDUFA dates, AdCom meetings, primary-completion dates, antitrust-clearance milestones, and deal-close windows (sources: [BiopharmaWatch FDA Calendar](https://www.biopharmawatch.com/fda-calendar), [BPIQ catalyst database](https://app.bpiq.com/), [AnalystPrep merger-arb notes](https://analystprep.com/study-notes/cfa-level-2/event-driven-strategies-merger-arbitrage/)). For each catalyst the position has explicit pre-registered "if outcome A, X / if outcome B, Y" notes. This pattern generalizes outside biotech/M&A: any equity has macro-data prints, earnings dates, regulatory windows, and product-launch dates that can be calendarized in the same way. The marginal cost of building a calendar is small; the benefit is that refresh becomes exogenously timed, defeating the ostrich effect.

## Section C — Open questions / disagreements

### C.1 — Cut-fast vs hold-through: the central unresolved schism

| Position | Core claim | Practitioner anchor | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cut-fast school** | The moment the buy-reason has changed, exit. Holding past invalidation is the costliest mistake. | Druckenmiller, Soros, Schwager's Market Wizards composite | Thesis-broken signal is informative; survivorship in drawdowns dominates compounding |
| **Hold-through school** | Most "thesis breaks" are noise; capitulation at the bottom is the costliest mistake; volatility is not loss. | Buffett, Marks, Klarman, Munger | Variance is not error; market sentiment dominates short-term price signal; the disposition-effect literature actually flips for already-cheap mispriced assets |

The empirical case is genuinely two-sided:
- Cut-fast finds support in the disposition-effect literature ([Odean 1998](https://faculty.haas.berkeley.edu/odean/papers%20current%20versions/areinvestorsreluctant.pdf)) and momentum/PEAD literature suggesting investors under-react to disconfirming information.
- Hold-through finds support in the value/mean-reversion literature, in Barber-Odean's finding that retail high-turnover destroys returns, and in Buffett's >60-year compound record on a hold-forever default.

What's likely going on: the two schools may be implicitly conditional on _what kind of new information_ has arrived. Cut-fast is right when the new evidence updates the central probability of the thesis (factual contradiction). Hold-through is right when the new evidence is price action only (noise/sentiment). No source I retrieved explicitly tests this conditional hypothesis with clean data.

### C.2 — Pre-mortem cadence

The literature establishes that pre-mortems are effective ([Klein HBR 2007](https://hbr.org/2007/09/performing-a-project-premortem), [Mitchell et al. 1989](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/bdm.3960020103)) but does **not** establish a dose-response for cadence. Quarterly is most-cited as practitioner default; ad-hoc-on-stress is most-criticized (because the worst time to do a pre-mortem is when you most need it — you're already committed). Annual is plausibly underused. This is an open question.

### C.3 — Thesis update vs thesis drift

MicroCapClub-style hardliners argue any post-entry "expanded" thesis is contamination and should trigger exit. Mauboussin/Mellers-style probabilistic updaters argue continuous incremental updates _are_ the discipline. These two views disagree on _whether_ to allow the original thesis to evolve at all. They probably both disagree with the strawman "set thesis at entry, never revisit until catastrophic break."

### C.4 — Group vs individual refresh

[Noise (Kahneman et al.)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noise:_A_Flaw_in_Human_Judgment) recommends independent aggregation. Practitioner shops vary: some run group-debate first (fast convergence, vulnerable to seniority-cascade), others require written individual re-underwrites first (slow, expensive, captures more signal). No clean empirical comparison was found in the searches.

### C.5 — Whether decision journals durably improve calibration

The 19% accuracy figure cited at [FS.blog](https://fs.blog/decision-journal/) is suggestive but the underlying study isn't well-tracked in the literature I retrieved. The mechanism (preventing hindsight rewriting) is well-established; the magnitude of the effect on professional investors specifically is not.

### C.6 — Ackman vs Druckenmiller on capitulation timing — a within-school disagreement

Ackman and Druckenmiller are both "active manager, concentrated portfolio, public profile" archetypes — and their refresh-timing styles are nearly opposite. Druckenmiller's stated rule: exit the moment the buy-reason has changed, even immediately, without waiting for narrative confirmation (source: [Morgan Stanley/Druckenmiller](https://www.morganstanley.com/insights/videos/hard-lessons/duquesne-stan-druckenmiller-iliana-bouzali)). Ackman's revealed practice in Valeant and Herbalife: maintain conviction publicly through years of disconfirming evidence, exit only when capital cost becomes prohibitive (sources: [Globe and Mail Valeant timeline](https://www.theglobeandmail.com/report-on-business/rob-magazine/inside-story-valeant-pharmaceuticals-fall/article34432530/), [CNBC Herbalife exit Feb 2018](https://www.cnbc.com/2018/02/28/ackman-exits-bet-against-herbalife.html)). The stated rationales differ: activist concentration explicitly *requires* public commitment; Druckenmiller-style discretionary macro deliberately *avoids* public commitment for exactly this reason. The disagreement is not over the underlying logic of "exit when wrong" but over how to manage the *commitment-cost* of being a public activist who has to defend positions to LPs and boards. The question this raises: does the activist style structurally require slower refresh-cadence as the price of the strategy, or has Ackman just been slow in execution where a more disciplined activist would have moved faster? No source clearly answers this.

### C.7 — Is refresh-discipline a function of strategy-type or operator-personality?

Pod-shop quants run 5%/7.5% drawdown rules enforced algorithmically (sources: [WSO multi-manager limits](https://www.wallstreetoasis.com/forum/hedge-fund/multi-manager-risk-limits), [Young & Calculated multi-manager](https://youngandcalculated.substack.com/p/how-multi-manager-hedge-funds-actually)). Bridgewater runs daily-written-views protocol (source: [Bridgewater 50 years](https://www.bridgewater.com/50-years-of-the-bridgewater-daily-observations)). Buffett runs annual-letter-and-board-meeting protocol. Klarman holds cash >50% as the default discipline. Druckenmiller does the chart-review-and-exit personal protocol. All of these are robust track-records. The empirical literature does not adjudicate whether it's the *frequency* of refresh or the *match between refresh-cadence and strategy half-life* that matters. A reasonable operating hypothesis: the refresh-cadence should be approximately the half-life of the alpha source — daily for liquidity/macro, monthly for catalyst-driven, quarterly for fundamental, annual for compounder/long-duration value — but no source explicitly tests this.
