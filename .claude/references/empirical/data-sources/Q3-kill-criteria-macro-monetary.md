# Post-2020 macro / monetary kill criteria — empirical evidence

**Research question:** What specific empirical signals (variable + threshold + deadline) have invalidated commonly-held macro / monetary scenarios in 2020-2026, and which canonical pre-2020 signals were *discredited* by post-COVID episodes?

**Method:** Scan Fed working papers, BIS bulletins, NBER, FEDS Notes, FRBNY Liberty Street, OFR, Brookings, plus practitioner commentary (Pozsar, Sahm, Pimco). Each entry triangulates source, episode, and threshold actually fired (or didn't).

**Quality bar:** ≥15 sources, ≥150 lines. Every URL fetched.

## Section B — Kill criteria catalog

Format: `[ID] variable | comparator | threshold | deadline | episode where it fired (or didn't) | source`

### Fed-policy regime kill criteria

**B1 — Sahm rule**
- Variable: 3M-MA unemployment rate minus trailing-12M low
- Comparator: ≥
- Threshold: +0.50pp
- Deadline: monthly BLS print
- Fired: July 2024 (UR 4.3%, +0.53pp). Did NOT produce textbook recession in subsequent 9 months. False positive in this cycle (immigration-supply shock per Sahm herself).
- Source: CRS IN12410; FRED SAHMREALTIME

**B2 — Engstrom-Sharpe NTFS**
- Variable: 6-quarter-fwd Treasury yield minus 3M T-bill
- Comparator: <
- Threshold: 0 bp (inversion)
- Deadline: rolling daily; persistent for ≥3M
- Fired: Late 2022 through 2023; recession probability rose materially in their model. Did NOT produce NBER recession. Discredited rule of thumb that NTFS strictly dominates 10y-3m.
- Source: Engstrom-Sharpe 2018 FEDS; FRB Chicago 2022

**B3 — 10y-3m yield curve inversion**
- Variable: 10y T-yield minus 3M T-bill
- Comparator: <
- Threshold: 0 bp for ≥3M consecutive
- Deadline: 6-18M lead time historically
- Fired: October 2022 — March 2024 (longest inversion since 1980). Did NOT produce recession within historical window. Major canonical signal that BROKE post-COVID.
- Source: FRB Chicago Letter 404; FED FEDS Note "Don't Fear the Yield Curve, Reprise"

**B4 — Fed reserves/GDP ample threshold**
- Variable: Bank reserves / nominal GDP
- Comparator: <
- Threshold: 8-10% (Logan/NY Fed working estimate); some estimates as low as 7% (scarcity onset)
- Deadline: rolling H.4.1 weekly
- Fired: Sep 2019 (~7% reserves/GDP, $1.4T) → SOFR spike to 5.25%, intraday 10%
- Source: Logan 2026 Dallas Fed; Richmond Fed EB 23-07; OFR 2023

**B5 — SOFR-IORB / repo dispersion**
- Variable: SOFR minus IORB (or 99th-percentile of repo distribution minus median)
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: +5-10bp persistent (vs. typical near-zero)
- Deadline: 3-5 business days persistence flags structural stress
- Fired: Sep 16-17, 2019 (SOFR jumped 282bp in one day to 5.25%); did NOT fire materially in 2023 banking stress (BTFP backstopped reserves).
- Source: OFR 2023; FRBNY Staff Report 918

**B6 — RRP balance trajectory**
- Variable: Overnight RRP outstanding
- Comparator: rate-of-change
- Threshold: drainage from peak >$2T in <12M = liquidity-regime tightening; <$50B = "next stress falls on reserves"
- Deadline: weekly
- Fired: Drained from $2.4T peak (late 2022) to ~$10-20B (late 2025)
- Source: Richmond Fed EB 21-43; Kansas City Fed Economic Bulletin

### Inflation-regime kill criteria

**B7 — Trimmed-mean PCE persistence**
- Variable: Dallas Fed 12M trimmed-mean PCE
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 3.0% sustained ≥6M (vs. 2% target)
- Deadline: monthly PCE print
- Fired: Persistently above 3% from late 2021 through 2023; "transitory" thesis broke when trimmed-mean (which excludes outliers) confirmed broad-based pressure.
- Source: Dallas Fed PCE page; ClevelandFed WP 23-06

**B8 — Cleveland Fed median CPI**
- Variable: Median CPI YoY
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 4% sustained ≥3M = persistent regime; >5% = entrenched
- Deadline: monthly CPI release
- Fired: Hit 7.2% in late 2022, persistent above 4% through mid-2023. Confirmed broad inflation, killed transitory thesis.
- Source: ClevelandFed Median CPI page; FRED MEDCPIM158SFRBCLE

**B9 — NY Fed Multivariate Core Trend (MCT)**
- Variable: Sectoral persistent component of PCE inflation
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: persistent component >3% with services-ex-housing breadth >50%
- Deadline: monthly
- Fired: Mid-2022 services breakdown of MCT showed services component drove persistence even as goods normalized — used by Powell in 2022 speeches.
- Source: NY Fed MCT page

**B10 — Atlanta Fed Wage Growth Tracker (WGT)**
- Variable: Median 12M wage growth, matched CPS
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 4.0% = above pre-COVID baseline; 5.0%+ = wage-price spiral risk; 3.5% = consistent with 2% inflation
- Deadline: monthly
- Fired: Peaked 6.7% Jul-2021; 6.1% Dec-2022. By 2024 had decelerated to ~4%. Cooled without recession (Phillips-curve flattening / disinflation-ex-recession).
- Source: Atlanta Fed Wage Growth Tracker

**B11 — UMich 5-10y inflation expectations**
- Variable: U-Mich median 5-10y inflation expectation
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 3.5% (top of 20y range = anchoring concern); 4.0%+ = unanchoring
- Deadline: monthly preliminary + final
- Fired: April 2026 reading hit 4.4% (highest since June 1991); IQR widened to 9.6pp (largest since 1979). Strong unanchoring signal.
- Source: U-Mich; Richmond Fed Macro Minute 2025

**B12 — NY Fed SCE 3-year inflation expectations**
- Variable: Median 3y-ahead inflation expectation
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 3.0% sustained = mild de-anchoring; 4.0%+ = serious
- Deadline: monthly
- Fired: 3y SCE has hovered near 3% in 2025-26 ("anchored at 3%, not 2%"); softer signal than U-Mich.
- Source: FRBNY SCE

**B13 — 5y5y forward breakeven**
- Variable: 5y forward 5y inflation breakeven (TIPS-derived)
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 2.5% = mild de-anchoring; >3% = unanchoring
- Deadline: daily
- Fired: Stayed remarkably anchored 2021-2024 (around 2.0-2.5%) even as spot CPI ran 9%. Long-end credibility held.
- Source: FRED T5YIFR; J.P. Morgan Treasury Inflation report

### Credit-regime kill criteria

**B14 — HY-OAS 500bp threshold**
- Variable: ICE BofA US HY OAS (BAMLH0A0HYM2)
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 500bp = soft regime change; 800bp = recession-pricing; 1000bp+ = crisis
- Deadline: rolling 5-day average
- Fired: Mar 23, 2020 (1087bp, 6 weeks from 360bp at YE-2019); briefly 600bp+ in Oct 2022 then mean-reverted; 2023 SVB stress maxed ~520bp.
- Source: LCD Comps; CFA Institute Enterprising Investor; FRED BAMLH0A0HYM2

**B15 — Excess Bond Premium (Gilchrist-Zakrajšek)**
- Variable: EBP (residual after stripping default-risk from BBB-AAA spread)
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 0 (long-run mean); +1σ ≈ +50bp = stress; +2σ flags recession
- Deadline: monthly
- Fired: COVID March 2020 (large +EBP spike); 2022-23 episodes saw EBP elevated but not at recession-prob >50% trigger. EBP gave more accurate "no-recession" signal than yield curve in 2022-23.
- Source: GZ 2012; FEDS 2016 update; FRED Series

**B16 — CDX HY 5y trigger levels**
- Variable: CDX HY 5y spread
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 500bp = early caution; 700bp = stress; 1000bp+ = crisis
- Deadline: daily
- Fired: 800bp+ March 2020 COVID; 600bp 2022 cycle; ~500bp 2023 banking.
- Source: ICE / Markit data via brokers

### Bank-stress kill criteria

**B17 — Uninsured deposit ratio**
- Variable: Uninsured deposits / total deposits
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 50% = elevated risk; >85% = SVB-class fragility
- Deadline: quarterly call reports
- Fired: SVB 86.4% uninsured (Dec 2022); First Republic ~68%; Signature ~90%. Predicted bank-run risk where it manifested.
- Source: FDIC OIG; SVB Material Loss Review

**B18 — Held-to-maturity unrealized loss / Tier-1 capital**
- Variable: HTM unrealized loss / CET1
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 50% = vulnerable; 100% = solvency-impaired if forced to mark
- Deadline: quarterly
- Fired: SVB had HTM losses exceeding tangible common equity by Q4 2022 — the leading indicator missed by markets pre-SVB.
- Source: FDIC OIG First Republic MLR; SVB Material Loss Review (Federal Reserve)

**B19 — One-day deposit outflow rate**
- Variable: % of deposits outflowing in 24-48h
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 20% in 48h = uncontainable run
- Deadline: real-time
- Fired: SVB lost $42B (~25% of deposits) in 24h on Mar 9, 2023 — first social-media-accelerated bank run; First Republic lost 40% of deposits in March 2023.
- Source: FDIC; CNBC; First Republic earnings

### Dollar / FX-regime kill criteria

**B20 — DXY trend break levels**
- Variable: DXY index
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 105 = strong-dollar regime threshold; 110+ = stress regime; 100 = neutral / breaking down
- Deadline: weekly close
- Fired: DXY hit 114.78 Sep 27, 2022 (20-year high), reflected Fed-BoJ-ECB policy gap. 100 served as triple-test floor in 2024.
- Source: TradingView DXY; Ferrante Capital

**B21 — JPY level vs. BoJ intervention thresholds**
- Variable: USD/JPY
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: 150 = MoF rhetorical intervention; 155-160 = actual intervention; collapse >145 = forced unwind
- Deadline: intraday
- Fired: Aug 5, 2024 — JPY rallied from 161 to 142 in 3 weeks after BoJ 25bp hike; Nikkei -12.4% (worst since 1987); S&P -3% in single session.
- Source: BIS Bulletin 90; Wellington

**B22 — Pozsar Bretton Woods III triggers**
- Variable: foreign FX-reserve composition (gold % of total reserves; commodity-linked reserves)
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: gold reserves at central banks moving above ~20% of total reserves = systemic shift
- Deadline: monthly IMF COFER + WGC data
- Fired: Central bank gold buying hit record levels 2022-2024 post-Russia sanctions; gold breakout above $2400 (2024) and $2700 (2025); discrete regime shift.
- Source: Pozsar; OMFIF; IMF COFER

### EM / international kill criteria

**B23 — Sudden-stop indicator (Calvo)**
- Variable: capital inflows/GDP relative to trailing avg
- Comparator: <
- Threshold: 2σ negative shock combined with EMBI+ spread breakout >+200bp in 30d
- Deadline: monthly
- Fired: Repeatedly in 2022 (Sri Lanka, Pakistan, Egypt, Argentina); "GDP growth falling ~4pp YoY in first 4 quarters" canonical post-stop signature.
- Source: Calvo 1998; Eichengreen 2016

**B24 — VIX/MOVE regime divergence**
- Variable: MOVE index level + VIX-MOVE correlation
- Comparator: >
- Threshold: MOVE >150 = stress; MOVE rising 4w before VIX = bond-led regime shift
- Deadline: daily
- Fired: MOVE hit 170 in March 2020; ~200 in March 2023 SVB; MOVE led VIX both episodes.
- Source: Charles Schwab MOVE explainer; Convex

## Section C — Hard vs soft criteria classification

### Hard criteria (single firing invalidates scenario)
- **B5 — SOFR > IORB by 5-10bp persistent for >5 days** (proves reserves are scarce)
- **B19 — One-day deposit outflow >20% in 48h** (terminal bank run)
- **B14 — HY-OAS >1000bp** (definitionally crisis pricing; never fires without macro stress)
- **B21 — USD/JPY collapses >10 figures in 3 weeks** (carry-trade unwind in motion)
- **B17/B18 — uninsured-deposit ratio >85% AND HTM-loss/CET1 >100%** (joint trigger; if both fire, bank fails barring intervention)
- **B11 — UMich 5-10y >4.0%** (50-year-high de-anchoring, hard signal Fed historically responds to)

### Soft criteria (cumulative firing erodes probability)
- **B1 — Sahm rule trip** (post-2024 episode showed soft, not hard — false-positive risk)
- **B2/B3 — yield-curve inversion** (post-2022 showed soft, not hard — failed without confirmation)
- **B7/B8 — trimmed-mean / median CPI >3%** (soft; needs ≥6M persistence + breadth)
- **B10 — WGT >5%** (soft; cooled without recession)
- **B15 — EBP elevated +1σ** (soft; needs to push recession-prob >50%)
- **B20 — DXY >110** (soft; reflects relative policy not absolute regime)
- **B22 — gold reserve % at central banks** (soft; multi-quarter regime indicator)
- **B12 — NYFed SCE 3y >3%** (soft; less reactive than UMich)
- **B23 — sudden-stop indicators** (soft; basket signal)

## Section D — Episodes where canonical criteria FAILED post-2020

### D.1 — 2022-2023 yield-curve inversion without recession
The longest 10y-3m inversion since 1980 (Oct-2022 → Mar-2024) failed to produce an NBER recession within historical lead-time windows. Engstrom-Sharpe 2022 FRB-Chicago piece flagged ahead of time that NTFS pointed to LOW recession risk while 10y-3m screamed inversion. Mechanism: locked-in low mortgage rates + corporate term-out at 2020-21 lows → rate-sensitivity of household & corporate balance sheets temporarily severed. Source: FRB Chicago Letter 404; ClevelandFed yield-curve indicator; OMFIF 2022. **Implication:** treat 10y-3m / 10y-2y inversion as soft, not hard, post-2020. Confirm with EBP, NTFS, labor (Sahm).

### D.2 — March 2020 Treasury-market dysfunction not predicted by canonical signals
Treasuries SOLD OFF with risk assets in mid-March 2020 — the inverse of the canonical safe-haven flight-to-quality. No pre-2020 stress indicator captured the basis-trade unwind / dealer-balance-sheet capacity constraint. Required an entirely new family of signals (dealer inventory, basis spreads, MBS dollar-roll dislocations). Source: FSB Holistic Review; FRBNY SR 1010; OFR. **Implication:** add basis-trade health and dealer-capacity indicators to S0 dimensions.

### D.3 — August 2024 yen carry-trade unwind suddenness
Carry-trade unwinds in canonical models were modeled as gradual; Aug 2024 saw 11% USD/JPY move and 12.4% Nikkei single-day crash from a 25bp BoJ surprise + soft US jobs print. BIS Bulletin 90 documents that VIX hit 65, S&P -3% intraday, with no warning from canonical risk indicators in the 1-week-prior window. Source: BIS Bulletin 90. **Implication:** carry-trade unwinds need real-time intraday signals (FX vol/skew, JPY-funded ETF basket flows), not weekly-monthly indicators.

### D.4 — Sahm rule false positive (July 2024)
Sahm rule triggered (0.53pp) but recession did not follow. Sahm-acknowledged immigration supply-shock confounded the standard interpretation. Source: CRS IN12410; CNBC. **Implication:** soft, not hard. Confirm with claims, hires, payrolls.

### D.5 — "Transitory" inflation thesis broke despite anchored long-end
Trimmed-mean PCE and median CPI persistence above 3% from mid-2021 through 2023 disconfirmed the "transitory" framing despite 5y5y forwards staying near 2.5%. Source: Dallas Fed; ClevelandFed WP 23-06. **Implication:** breadth measures (trimmed-mean, median) lead long-end markets in detecting persistence.

### D.6 — Goyal-Welch-Zafirov 2024: >50% of post-2008 predictors fail OOS
Updated equity-premium prediction audit showed more than 1/3 of new predictors lose in-sample significance, and half of those that retain it have poor OOS. Practitioner takeaway: any "novel" macro indicator published 2010-2020 should be treated as guilty-until-proven-innocent. Source: RFS 2024.

### D.7 — Bank-stress canonical signals (loan-loss provisions, NPL ratios) failed in 2023
SVB and Signature failed with healthy NPL ratios but lethal duration mismatch + uninsured deposit concentration. Forced new kill criteria around HTM-loss/CET1 and uninsured-deposit-ratio. Source: FDIC OIG First Republic MLR.

## Section E — Recommended kill-criteria templates for our 6 S0 dimensions

### S0-D1 — Economic cycle / recession risk
**Hard**
- Sahm rule + claims confirmation: 3M-MA UR rises 0.5pp AND initial claims 4w-MA >300k for 6 consecutive weeks
- Excess Bond Premium >+1σ (>~50bp residual) AND HY-OAS >700bp simultaneously
- ISM Manufacturing <45 for 3 consecutive months AND ISM Services <50

**Soft (cumulative)**
- 10y-3m inversion (now soft post-2022)
- Sahm rule isolated (post-2024 false positive)
- Conference Board LEI -10% YoY
- Atlanta Fed GDPNow Q-tracking <0%

### S0-D2 — Credit / financial stress
**Hard**
- HY-OAS >1000bp
- CDX HY 5y >800bp for >5 trading days
- One-day SOFR-IORB spread >+10bp persistent
- Treasury basis (cash-CTD vs futures) blowing out >200bp

**Soft**
- HY-OAS >500bp (canonical regime line)
- EBP +1σ
- MOVE index >150
- Loan officer survey tightening >20pp net

### S0-D3 — Volatility regime
**Hard**
- VIX >40 sustained ≥10 days
- MOVE >175 sustained ≥10 days (+ MOVE-led signaling per March 2023 pattern)
- Realized 21d S&P vol >30%

**Soft**
- VIX/MOVE divergence (MOVE rising w/o VIX = bond-led regime change)
- Term structure of VIX inverted (front>back)
- Skew index >150

### S0-D4 — Dollar / international regime
**Hard**
- DXY breakout >110 sustained 4 weeks
- USD/JPY collapse >10 figures in 3 weeks (carry-unwind kill)
- JPY/CNH joint moves correlated >0.7 (capital-flight signal)
- EM hard-currency spread (EMBI+) widens >+200bp in 30 days

**Soft**
- DXY trend persistence above 100/105
- Central bank gold buying >300t/qtr
- China FX-reserve drawdown >$50B/month

### S0-D5 — Inflation regime
**Hard**
- UMich 5-10y >4.0% (de-anchored)
- Trimmed-mean PCE >3% AND median CPI >4% sustained 6 consecutive months (joint persistence + breadth)
- WGT >5% AND ECI >5% (wage-price spiral)
- 5y5y forward breakeven >3.0%

**Soft**
- UMich 5-10y in 3.0-3.5% range (anchoring strain)
- Trimmed-mean PCE 2.5-3% (not yet persistent regime)
- NY Fed SCE 3y >3% (anchored at 3% not 2%)
- WGT 4-5% (above pre-COVID, not crisis)

### S0-D6 — Monetary policy / liquidity regime
**Hard**
- Bank reserves/GDP <8% (scarcity onset; Logan threshold)
- ON RRP <$50B (cushion exhausted, next stress on reserves)
- Fed funds rate cuts >100bp in single quarter (forced-pivot kill criterion for hawkish scenarios)
- Treasury-market basis blowout requiring Fed intervention

**Soft**
- RRP draining at >$200B/month pace
- Term-premium (ACM) breakout >+50bp on no-news (term-premium repricing)
- Money-market fund AUM shifting >$500B in 30 days
