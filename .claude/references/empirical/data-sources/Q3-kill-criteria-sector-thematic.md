# Post-2020 sector / thematic kill criteria — empirical catalog

> **Scope.** Concrete, observable conditions whose breach has invalidated (or would invalidate) sector / thematic scenarios in the 2020–2026 window. Focus areas: AI capex cycle, energy transition + traditional energy, regional banking 2023, post-COVID tech reset, semiconductor cycle, China / EM thematic plays, biotech regulatory regime.
>
> **Methodology.** Pulled empirical episodes (2022 SaaS reset, March 2023 SVB / regional bank run, 2022–23 memory glut, 2024 China property liquidations, 2024 office CMBS spike) and reverse-engineered the leading indicators that flagged invalidation. Each criterion is tier-labeled (Tier 1 = official / regulator data, Tier 2 = sell-side / academic, Tier 3 = blog / aggregator).
>
> **Calibration date.** April 27, 2026.

## Section C — Cross-sector failure-mode patterns

Three recurring patterns across the 2022 SaaS reset / 2023 regional banks / 2024 China property / 2024 office CRE / 2025 AI capex inflection signals:

### C.1 "Discount-rate / multiple compression precedes fundamental break"
- 2022 SaaSacre: BVP index −50% in calendar '22 BEFORE most underlying ARR growth deceleration showed up in print results (median NRR didn't crack 100% until 2023).
- 2023 regional banks: HTM mark-to-market losses (rate-driven) showed up Q3 '22; SVB run March '23.
- 2025 AI: FCF coverage starts deteriorating ('24 → '25 Mag-4 FCF $237B → $200B) before any single hyperscaler has cut capex guide.
- **Generalizable signal:** when liability-side / discount-rate dynamics deteriorate, *the asset-side print typically lags 2–4 quarters*. Treat funding-cost / multiple compression as a leading indicator, not a contemporaneous one.

### C.2 "Concentration risk + funding-source homogeneity"
- SVB: 86% uninsured + concentrated VC depositor base + concentrated rate-duration on asset side.
- NVDA: 53% of DC revenue from 3 customers, who are simultaneously building competing ASICs.
- Office CRE: concentrated bank exposure (CRE concentration ratios >300%) combined with concentrated tenant industry mix in major metros.
- **Generalizable signal:** any thematic where a *single counterparty cluster* (depositor base / customer set / lender pool) >50% concentrated is structurally fragile to a coordinated reversal.

### C.3 "Buildout > deployment" gap (the Cisco-2000 echo)
- AI capex 2025: $40B/yr depreciation on infra coming online vs $15–20B/yr realized revenue at current usage rates (CNBC Feb 2026).
- Telecom 1999–2002: ~$1T fiber buildout vs negligible incremental enterprise revenue — debt forced collapse.
- 2022 SaaS: COVID-pulled-forward demand met capacity overbuild; magic-number / Rule-of-40 collapsed in unison.
- **Generalizable signal:** when *physical / capacity buildout outpaces realized revenue by ≥2×* for ≥4 quarters, expect either (a) discontinuous valuation reset or (b) extended multiple compression as growth fills in. Cisco took 25 years to fill its 27× P/S — the analog isn't always collapse; it's compression during a long fundamental fill.

### C.4 "Hard-data lag the price action"
- DRAM inventory: 31w glut in early 2023 only became visible in supplier disclosures *after* memory equity drawdowns in 2022.
- Office CMBS DQ: hit 11% in Dec 2024 — but office REIT prices had drawn down from 2021 highs ~50% before that.
- China property TSF: still printing ~8% even after Evergrande / Country Garden defaults visible.
- **Generalizable signal:** equity prices typically lead Tier-1 official data by 6–12 months. Kill criteria should be *price + private-data triangulated*, not Tier-1-only.

## Section E — Recommended kill-criteria templates for thematic name discovery (P3) + deep dive (P4)

### E.1 P3 (thematic / name-discovery) template
For any sector / thematic scenario, require:
1. **Two falsifiers**: at least one **hard** (binary, single-print) and at least one **soft** (regime / multi-quarter trend).
2. **Deadline pattern**: each falsifier specifies its observation cadence (quarterly earnings / monthly print / per-readout / live).
3. **Source mapping**: every falsifier maps to a Tier-1 (regulator / official) data source where possible; Tier-2/3 only if Tier-1 unavailable.
4. **Lead/lag classification**: explicitly note whether the criterion leads, contemporaneously confirms, or lags fundamental break (cf. Section C.4).
5. **Concentration check**: for any thematic with single-counterparty-cluster >50%, require a dedicated concentration-reversal falsifier (Section C.2).

#### Sample P3 falsifier-set, AI capex thematic (NVDA-anchored):
- **Hard 1 (lagging confirm):** NVDA GAAP GM drops ≥300 bps for 2 consecutive quarters from >73% baseline.
- **Hard 2 (leading):** Hyperscaler aggregate FCF coverage of capex <1.0× for two consecutive quarters.
- **Soft 1:** AI pilot-to-production conversion stuck <10% in successive MIT NANDA reports.
- **Soft 2:** Power-bottleneck signal — Dominion/AEP/PJM cancelled or deferred ≥20% of announced 2025–27 MW.
- **Concentration falsifier:** NVDA top-3 customers >55% of DC revenue AND any one customer's custom-ASIC reaches >5% inference share (per industry tracker).

### E.2 P4 (deep dive / per-name) template
For each held / candidate name, embed:
1. **Idiosyncratic kill (per-name):** at least one company-specific binary (e.g., a Phase III readout, a single covenant, a single-customer churn).
2. **Sector kill (inheritance):** thematic falsifiers from P3 inherited.
3. **Macro kill (regime):** at least one D1–D5 regime falsifier (e.g., periphery EM CDS >300 bps if EM-exposed; office CMBS DQ >10% if regional-bank exposed).
4. **Lead/lag stack:** all criteria stacked with their lead/lag tag for refresh-discipline routing.

#### Per-sector P4 starter checklist (drop-in)
- **Regional bank name:** uninsured-dep% >70%; CRE concentration ratio >300%; HTM mark-to-market loss / Tier-1 ratio; single-day outflow >5%; community-bank NIM <3.20% (sector regime); office CMBS DQ >10% (sector regime).
- **AI / semi name:** customer concentration top-3 share; gross margin trajectory (Q/Q); inventory days; foundry utilization (sector); hyperscaler capex Y/Y (sector); enterprise AI-ROI (regime).
- **Energy E&P name:** WTI vs corporate breakeven spread; OPEC+ discipline state; demand-peak signal; ROIC trajectory.
- **SaaS name:** NRR trend; Rule-of-40; magic number; ARR Y/Y deceleration vs FCF margin; BVP index forward EV/Revenue (regime).
- **Biotech name:** Phase II/III readout dates as per-name kill anchors; FDA novel-approval rate (regime); IRA selected-drug list inclusion risk.
- **China / EM name:** TSF growth (regime); offshore-bond defaults in linked sector; periphery EM CDS; portfolio outflows (regime).

### E.3 Sectors where empirical literature is thin (caution)
Two sectors where empirical 2020–2026 episodes are insufficient to confidently pose hard / threshold criteria:
1. **AI ROI / enterprise deployment** — only one full survey cycle (MIT NANDA 2025) is publicly available; "95% no measurable ROI" is one data point and may be measurement-method-dependent (sales/marketing pilot bias). Use this as a *trend-watch* (require improvement vs 2025), not a hard threshold.
2. **Biotech regulatory-regime (RFK / IRA)** — IRA price-negotiation has bedded in but the second-round selected-drug list and any post-2026 enforcement variance is too thin to set numerical haircut thresholds. Use binary regime-shift flags (e.g., "selected-drug-list inclusion of held name" → kill) rather than numerical price-haircut thresholds at this stage.

A third area is borderline:
3. **AI capex peak / Cisco-2000 analog** — abundant qualitative analogy literature, but no post-2020 *resolution* episode (NVDA hasn't yet had its multiple-compression event). Hard thresholds in B.1 are ex-ante, not ex-post calibrated. Update annually as 2026–28 episodes resolve.
