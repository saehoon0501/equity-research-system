**Research question:** What specific kill-criteria language have named PMs used in their 2020-2026 letters / interviews / public statements? What were the explicit "if X happens, this thesis is wrong" formulations?

**Method:** Primary-source extraction from Pershing Square shareholder letters, Greenlight quarterly letters, Oaktree memos, Berkshire AGM transcripts, CNBC / Bloomberg / Real Vision interview archives, plus the Morgan Stanley *Hard Lessons* podcast (Feb 2026). Verbatim quotes preferred. Each entry triangulates source URL + episode + outcome.

**Quality bar:** ≥15 sources, ≥150 lines. Practitioner-by-practitioner with verbatim quotes where surfaced.

**Calibration date.** April 27, 2026.

**Scope distinction.** Sister files `Q3-kill-criteria-macro-monetary.md` and `Q3-kill-criteria-sector-thematic.md` cover *empirical / regulatory* signal thresholds (Sahm, NTFS, FDIC QBP, etc.). This file covers *practitioner-articulated* kill criteria — the specific phrasing investors used, whether the trigger fired, and what they did about it.

## Section C — Cross-practitioner patterns

### C.1 — Common structural elements

Looking across Druckenmiller / Tepper / Ackman / Einhorn / Marks / Loeb / Klarman, the kill-criteria architecture converges on five repeated elements:

1. **Time-horizon-conditioned re-evaluation, not stop-loss-as-rule.** Druckenmiller: *"three-week"* default revision cadence; Marks: 18-36 month "regime" horizon; Klarman: catalyst-realization horizon. None of the elite practitioners use price-stop-loss as the *primary* kill criterion. Price action is *diagnostic* (Druckenmiller: *"if the stock's not going anywhere, makes me go back and check the thesis over and over"*) but not auto-trigger.

2. **Predictability degradation as the core formal kill.** Ackman's Netflix exit is the clearest articulation: *"dispersion of outcomes has widened to a sufficiently large extent."* This is operationalized as: when the *posterior variance* on a thesis grows beyond a portfolio-construction threshold, exit — even if expected value remains positive.

3. **"Original thesis no longer holds" language as canonical activist exit.** Loeb's CoStar: *"We no longer believe that our original thesis holds true today and have disposed of our position in its entirety."* This is the activist analog of Buffett's *"world changed for airlines."* Both are *thesis-environment* kills, not *price* kills.

4. **Macro-overlay can override single-name thesis.** Ackman covered the Treasury short in Oct 2023 *"because of concern about the economy"* despite the position being profitable. Tepper trimmed China in Q2 2025 on trade-war risk. This is documented as a valid exit path: even if the original thesis is intact, regime-shift can make the *risk-adjusted* hold unattractive.

5. **Post-mistake doctrine encoded in subsequent letters.** Pershing Square's 2022 Netflix exit explicitly references Valeant: *"One of our learnings from past mistakes is to act promptly when we discover new information."* This is the critical institutional feature — kill-criteria evolve from documented prior failures.

### C.2 — Where practitioners diverge

- **Marks** publishes *binary* invalidation conditions ("Fed funds back to 0 or 1% would invalidate sea change"). This is unusual.
- **Druckenmiller** explicitly disclaims rigid triggers: *"I tend to change my mind every three weeks."* The criterion is the *process*, not a number.
- **Buffett** uses *qualitative dispersion* language ("future much less clear") rather than quantitative thresholds.
- **Ackman** post-Valeant uses *"high degree of predictability"* as a portfolio-construction binary — when violated, exit, regardless of expected value.
- **Klarman** uses *catalyst realization* (event-driven) rather than time-horizon (regime-driven) framing.

## Section E — Failure modes when kill-criteria didn't fire (or fired but were ignored)

### E.1 — Cases where kill criteria SHOULD have fired but practitioners delayed

**Ackman / Valeant Pharmaceuticals (2015–2017)** — *retrospective case, pre-period but heavily referenced in 2020-2026 letters.*
- Pre-period thesis-break: accounting fraud allegations (Citron Oct 2015), management fraud disclosures (March 2016), but Pershing Square doubled down before final exit at large loss in March 2017.
- The 2022 Netflix letter is the explicit mea culpa: *"One of our learnings from past mistakes is to act promptly when we discover new information about an investment that is inconsistent with our original thesis."* The Valeant lesson directly powered the Netflix exit speed.
- **Lesson:** kill-criteria are *organizational* — they need pre-commitment. Without pre-commitment, sunk-cost fallacy / public-thesis-defense delays exits.

**Buffett / IBM (2011–2018)** — kill criterion was *moat-degradation* (cloud disrupting on-prem systems) but Berkshire held for ~6 years past that signal.
- Buffett later acknowledged: *"I had a different view of IBM 6 or 7 years ago."*
- **Lesson:** quality-investor framework can suppress the dispersion-widening kill criterion when the brand-moat narrative remains intact. Tech disruption can erode moats faster than quality-investor frameworks anticipate.

**Buffett / Airlines (2016–2020)** — entry thesis in 2016 was post-consolidation pricing-power durability. The COVID shock fired the criterion in March 2020, but exit at ~50-70% loss raised questions whether the *original* dispersion analysis was already too narrow.
- Buffett documented this as *"probability-weighted decision when we bought ... I made a mistake in evaluating"* — owning the pre-existing analytical error.
- **Lesson:** even when a kill criterion fires *cleanly*, the underlying problem is often pre-existing under-weighting of tail risks. Post-mortem must distinguish "thesis broke" from "I miscalibrated tail risk."

**Druckenmiller / NVDA early exit (2024)** — *opposite failure mode.* Kill criterion fired *too early* on emotional / position-management grounds rather than thesis grounds.
- *Hard Lessons* Feb 2026: *"I couldn't stand success. It had gone from $150 to $800. I was long-term in it. I couldn't deal with it, and I sold it."*
- **Lesson:** psychological-discomfort is NOT a valid kill criterion. Druckenmiller documented this as a regret.

### E.2 — Cases where kill criteria fired but were ignored

**Ackman / Lower-Lower 2024 thesis** — Fed-cut path proved shallower than priced; the implicit "rates fall fast" component of the thesis weakened, but Pershing Square did not announce a formal kill criterion. Letters re-framed positioning rather than acknowledging thesis revision.
- **Lesson:** macro-overlay theses are easier to silently adjust than to publicly invalidate. The discipline gap is a known failure mode — Marks explicitly warns against it in *I Beg to Differ.*

**Tepper / Dec 2022 "leaning short"** — call did not fire (S&P +24% in 2023). Tepper did not publicly retract; subsequent positions did not match the bearish call.
- **Lesson:** public bearish *commentary* without portfolio-action commitment is not a kill criterion at all — it is opinion. Distinguishing the two is a critical practitioner-discipline gap.

**Druckenmiller / 2023 short bonds** — yields rose ~80bps from Aug → Oct (favorable), but Druckenmiller "got nervous about something breaking" and built a counter-position (long 2-year) without fully covering the long-bond short. The criterion-revision was real but partial; full kill was not articulated until later interviews.
- **Lesson:** position layering can function as *de facto* kill-criterion ("if X breaks, Y leg compensates") without a clean public articulation. This is the macro-trader analog of Klarman's catalyst layering.

### E.3 — The Ackman / Valeant cautionary archetype

The Valeant case is the single most-cited counter-example in post-2020 letters:
- **Original thesis:** specialty-pharma roll-up with attractive ROIC.
- **Kill criteria that should have fired:** Citron short report (Oct 21, 2015) flagging Philidor; CEO Pearson resignation (March 2016); SEC investigation; revenue-restatement.
- **What Pershing did:** doubled down in early 2016, public CNBC defense, then exited near low in March 2017 at ~$300M loss.
- **Net lesson encoded:** Pershing's 2022 Netflix exit at a $400M loss in 84 days is *deliberately* a fast exit (vs. 17-month Valeant delay). The 2022 letter explicitly references this as the learning.
- **Cross-practitioner reading:** Loeb's *"original thesis no longer holds"* CoStar 2026 language; Buffett's *"I'd made a mistake in evaluating"* airlines 2020 language; Klarman's *"catalysts have turned holdings into cash"* — all are post-Valeant-era articulations of the same pre-commitment principle.

### E.4 — The "no-explicit-criterion" failure mode (Tepper, post-Druckenmiller-NVDA)

A recurring failure pattern: public-figure PMs make directional calls in interviews without specifying invalidation conditions. When the call is wrong, there is no explicit retraction event because there was no explicit criterion. This is the *anti-pattern* the system kill-criteria framework should explicitly avoid.

Tepper's Dec 2022 "leaning short" and Sept 2024 "buy everything in China" are textbook examples. Both calls were partially or fully wrong; neither generated a clean public retraction with documented rationale.

## Section F — Synthesis: 5 most concrete kill-criteria templates extracted

For direct adoption into the system's kill-criteria framework:

1. **Predictability-degradation (Ackman / Buffett pattern):** *"If the dispersion of forward outcomes widens such that we lose confidence in predicting the variable to within [X%] over [Y horizon], exit."* — Operationalizes Ackman Netflix exit and Buffett airlines exit. Most general-purpose template.

2. **Binary macro threshold (Marks pattern):** *"This thesis is invalid if [macro variable] crosses [explicit threshold] for [N consecutive periods]."* — Operationalizes Marks's Sea Change ("Fed funds back to 0–1%") and Druckenmiller's Sohn 2022 ("inflation above 5% with Fed funds below CPI"). Cleanest binary kill.

3. **Time-horizon revision-review (Druckenmiller pattern):** *"Re-evaluate full thesis every [3 weeks / 1 quarter]. If price action contradicts thesis on stated time horizon, force re-investigation."* — Druckenmiller's *"if it goes against my belief over the given time frame"* and *"three-week revision"*. Process-kill rather than threshold-kill.

4. **Catalyst-realization / fair-value (Klarman / Loeb pattern):** *"Exit when (a) catalyst realizes value, OR (b) catalyst delays beyond [X period], OR (c) original thesis no longer holds."* — Klarman catalyst framework + Loeb CoStar exit. Best for event-driven and activist positions.

5. **Macro-overlay risk-asymmetry override (Ackman 2023 TLT cover pattern):** *"Even if single-name thesis intact, if macro regime shifts such that risk-adjusted asymmetry deteriorates beyond [threshold], exit."* — Ackman's Oct 2023 *"too much risk in the world"* cover. Necessary supplement to other criteria for portfolio-level discipline.
