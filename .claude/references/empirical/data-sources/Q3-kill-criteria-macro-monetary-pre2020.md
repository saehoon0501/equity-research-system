# Pre-2020 macro / monetary kill criteria

> **Scope.** Catalogs concrete empirical conditions (variable + threshold + deadline) that, when met, invalidated commonly-held macro / monetary scenarios in the pre-2020 era (~1990–2019). Anchored to the named episodes: 2008 GFC, 2000–2002 dot-com bust, 1998 Russia/LTCM, 1997 Asian crisis, 1994 bond crash + Tequila, 1987 Black Monday, 2011 Euro sovereign, 2015–16 China/oil, Dec-2018 Powell pivot, 2013 taper tantrum.
>
> **Tier convention.** Tier-A = peer-reviewed primary research; Tier-B = central-bank or BIS/IMF working papers; Tier-C = official central-bank historical accounts; Tier-D = practitioner/quality secondary commentary.

## Section B — Per-episode kill-criteria catalog

### B.1 — 1987 Black Monday (October 19, 1987)
- **Canonical signals that fired beforehand**
  - Dow declined ~4% on Oct 14 and another ~2.5% on Oct 15 — concentrated multi-day drawdown (Brady Report). FRB History.
  - Long-bond yields had risen sharply (~10%+ ten-year by mid-October), tightening conditions ahead of equity correction.
- **Documented kill criterion** (post-hoc): **portfolio-insurance dynamic-hedging notional ≥ ~$60–90B (Brady Report)** marked the threshold above which feedback selling could overwhelm market-maker capacity. This was *not* used as a stop-out at the time; it was reverse-engineered by the Brady Commission.
- **Practitioner context.** The "kill criterion" recognized in 1987 was essentially the breakdown of the cash-futures basis (S&P futures trading at unprecedented discount to cash index, ~20+ index points = ~10%) — invalidating the assumption that index-arb would close the gap.
- **Did the criterion fire on schedule?** No formal pre-event criterion existed; the structural-feedback signal was articulated retrospectively. The closest real-time signal — basis blowout — fired on Oct 19 itself, not in advance.
- Source: https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/stock-market-crash-of-1987

### B.2 — 1994 bond-market crash + Tequila contagion
- **Pre-event signals.** Fed funds futures had priced essentially zero hikes for 1994 going into the Feb 4 surprise hike (first since 1989); 30-year yield rose from ~5.9% (Jan-94) to ~8.0% (Nov-94).
- **Tequila kill criterion (used by EM practitioners after the fact).**
  - Mexican net international reserves below ~$10B (fell from ~$30B in early-94 to ~$5B by mid-Dec) — the binding threshold for peg defense.
  - Tesobonos (USD-indexed) outstanding > total reserves — a hard insolvency condition for the peg.
- **Practitioner triggers at the time.** The Colosio assassination (Mar 23, 1994) and the November 75bp Fed hike were the proximate triggers; reserve-depletion signals were visible in Banco de México's weekly reports but largely ignored until December.
- **Did kill criteria fire on schedule?** Reserve-depletion threshold fired in December 1994 in real time — peg broke Dec 20, devaluation Dec 22.
- Sources: https://www.richmondfed.org/publications/research/econ_focus/2017/q1/federal_reserve | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1994_bond_market_crisis

### B.3 — 1997 Asian crisis (Thailand-led)
- **Empirical leading indicators (Kaminsky-Lizondo-Reinhart "signal approach").** Of 38 leading indicators, **7 had conditional probability >50%** in Asia-5:
  - Real exchange rate deviation from trend (78% conditional prob)
  - Real effective exchange rate deviation (72%)
  - Short-term external debt / FX reserves > 1.0 (65%)
  - Equity-price decline
  - M2 / FX reserves
  - Output growth deceleration
  - Exports decline
- **Episode-specific signals that fired.**
  - Thailand: BoT had used $28B of $30B reserves on forward interventions by mid-1997 — *the* canonical dry-powder kill criterion (~93% reserves committed).
  - Current-account deficit > 5% GDP for >2 years (the "Reinhart line") — Thailand was at -8% GDP in 1996.
- **Practitioner context.** Onshore-offshore baht spread blew out >5% in early-97; Bangkok International Banking Facility deposits fell ~30% Jan–May 1997; foreign-currency liabilities of finance companies > capital × 5.
- **Did criteria fire on schedule?** Yes — the KLR signal-approach paper (1998) was written essentially as a post-hoc validation; out-of-sample, the model successfully ranked Thailand/Indonesia/Korea as most vulnerable.
- Sources: https://www.imf.org/external/pubs/ft/staffp/1998/03-98/pdf/kaminsky.pdf | https://www.federalreservehistory.org/essays/asian-financial-crisis

### B.4 — 1998 Russia/LTCM
- **Canonical signals.**
  - Russian GKO yields >100% by July 1998 (vs ~20% in May) — a hard kill threshold for "EM convergence" trades.
  - Brent crude < $12/bbl (mid-98 trough) — Russia's fiscal break-even was ~$20.
  - On-the-run vs off-the-run Treasury spread blew from ~3bp to ~15bp (Aug-Sep 98) — a textbook convergence-trade invalidation.
  - Swap-Treasury spread: 10y from ~40bp (Jul) to >80bp (Sep).
  - VIX from low-20s (Jul) to peak ~46 (Oct 8, 1998).
- **Documented kill criterion (LTCM ex-post).** Daily VaR exceeded model assumptions by >3x — the fund's stress scenarios had not contemplated correlated wide-basis blowouts across multiple convergence trades simultaneously.
- **Practitioner context.** "Flight to quality" was visible in real time but most carry-trade practitioners did not have a hard stop on the off-the-run spread; they had soft DV01 limits that were rapidly breached and breached again as positions could not be exited.
- **Did kill criteria fire on schedule?** Spreads blew out *before* the Russian default on Aug 17 — early signs in mid-July through credit, FX vol, and dollar funding. Most practitioners ignored them as "noise."
- Sources: https://ideas.repec.org/p/imf/imfwpa/2002-074.html | https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/pages/frontline/warning/themes/ltcm.html

### B.5 — 2000–2002 dot-com bust
- **Pre-peak signals (March 2000).**
  - NASDAQ trailing P/E > 200x (Q1 2000) — vs long-run ~25x.
  - Internet-IPO Lock-up expirations clustered (>$300B notional in Q2 2000).
  - VC-funding peak: $112.3B in 2000 (vs $54B in 1999) — classic late-cycle capital surge.
  - Cisco's order-book peak (Q4 CY2000) — and the inventory write-down ($2.2B in Apr 2001).
  - Intel revenue warning Sep 21, 2000 (-40% in single session) — the supply-chain inflection.
- **Documented kill criteria (used by sober practitioners pre-peak).**
  - "Cash-burn months remaining" (Barron's Mar 20, 2000 cover): firms with <12 months runway × no path to profitability flagged as imminent failures. ~51 of the 207 dot-coms in their list went bankrupt within 12 months.
  - GMO/Grantham 7-yr forecast: forecast US-large-cap real returns turned strongly negative in 1998–99.
- **Did criteria fire on schedule?** NASDAQ peaked Mar 10, 2000 — the cash-burn cover (Mar 20) was within 10 days of the top. The Intel warning (Sep 2000) confirmed the earnings-recession leg.
- Sources: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dot-com_bubble

### B.6 — 2007–2008 Global Financial Crisis
- **Early signals (chronological).**
  - **Feb 2007:** First sharp ABX-BBB price decline — the canonical CDO-tranche kill signal.
  - **Jun 14–22, 2007:** Bear Stearns High-Grade Structured Credit Strategies fund + Enhanced Leverage fund collapse — $20B notional CDO exposure.
  - **Aug 9, 2007:** BNP Paribas suspends three funds citing "complete evaporation of liquidity"; 3-month LIBOR-OIS spreads from <10bp to >70bp overnight.
  - **Aug 20, 2007:** TED spread to 240bp.
  - **Mar 14–17, 2008:** Bear Stearns rescue; CDS on Bear from 200bp to >1000bp in days.
  - **Sep 7, 2008:** Fannie/Freddie conservatorship.
  - **Sep 15, 2008:** Lehman bankruptcy; TED spread to >300bp (record). VIX to 89 by Oct 24.
  - **Sep 16, 2008:** AIG margin run; CDS on AIG more than doubles in 2 days.
- **Documented kill thresholds (Tier-A).**
  - Gilchrist–Zakrajšek EBP: rose >2 standard deviations above mean in mid-2007 (a recession-warning level by their own metric).
  - HY OAS: crossed 500bp in Aug 2007 — the practitioner "credit-stress" line.
  - HY OAS peak Dec 2008: 2,182bp (BAMLH0A0HYM2) — historical record.
  - LIBOR-OIS > 50bp = "broken funding markets" (BIS/Brunnermeier).
  - Adrian-Shin: broker-dealer asset growth turned sharply negative in 2007 — predicted the housing/durables collapse.
- **Practitioner context (signals ignored).** Many credit-fund managers had stop-losses tied to mark-to-market drawdowns rather than fundamental thresholds; structural protections (puts on subprime via Paulson, Burry, Lippmann) were the exception.
- **Did criteria fire on schedule?** Yes — every canonical signal fired between Feb 2007 and Sep 2008. The criterion that was ignored was the *combination*: ABX + LIBOR-OIS + EBP + dealer-leverage all firing simultaneously.
- Sources: https://www.princeton.edu/~markus/research/papers/liquidity_credit_crunch.pdf | https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/notes/feds-notes/updating-the-recession-risk-and-the-excess-bond-premium-20161006.html | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TED_spread | https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aer.102.4.1692

### B.7 — 2011 Euro sovereign crisis
- **Documented kill thresholds.**
  - Greece 10y - Bund spread from 130bp (Sep 2009) to 600bp (Apr 2010) → 2,500+bp by mid-2012.
  - Italy 10y - Bund > 500bp (Nov 2011) — the "ECB must act" line.
  - Spain 10y > 7% (Jul 2012) — Draghi's "whatever it takes" trigger.
- **Practitioner triggers.** Bund-Bono spread > 400bp, Italy 10y > 6%, sovereign-CDS > 500bp commonly used as binary kill thresholds.
- **Did criteria fire on schedule?** The Greek 10y spread crossed 600bp before any official bailout — the signal worked; the political response lagged. The "fire-then-act" pattern (signal fires → no action → spreads widen further → eventual ECB intervention) is the textbook EU policy-lag failure.
- Source: https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/scpwps/ecbwp1419.pdf

### B.8 — 2013 Taper Tantrum
- **Pre-event positioning signal.** EM-bond inflows >$200B cumulative 2010–13 with US 10y at <2% — extreme reach-for-yield positioning.
- **Trigger.** Bernanke May 22, 2013 testimony (mention of "tapering" in coming meetings).
- **Reaction.** US 10y yield 1.94% (May 21) → 2.03% (May 22) → 2.98% (Sep 5). Cumulative ~100bp in ~3.5 months.
- **EM kill criterion that fired.** "Fragile Five" (BRL, INR, IDR, TRY, ZAR) — currencies of countries with current-account deficits > 3% GDP got hit hardest, validating KLR signal-approach methodology.
- **Did the criterion fire on schedule?** Yes — Morgan Stanley's "Fragile Five" framework (current account + reserves + inflation) cleanly predicted the dispersion.
- Source: https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/notes/feds-notes/u-s-interest-rates-and-emerging-market-currencies-taking-stock-10-years-after-the-taper-tantrum-20231004.html

### B.9 — 2015–2016 China devaluation + oil collapse
- **Trigger.** PBoC devalued CNY ~3% on Aug 11, 2015 (cumulative ~5% over 3 days).
- **Stress thresholds that fired.**
  - HY OAS broke 500bp resistance in Jul 2015 → peaked 887bp in Feb 2016.
  - Energy HY OAS to 1,415bp (Dec 2015); Metals & Mining to ~1,500bp.
  - Brent < $30/bbl — sub-shale-breakeven for ~80% of producers.
  - China FX reserves fell from $4.0T (Jun 2014) to $3.2T (Jan 2016) — a ~$800B kill-criterion drawdown.
- **Did criteria fire on schedule?** Yes — HY-OAS firing led the S&P 500 -14% drawdown by ~2 months. EBP rose with HY-OAS, validating G-Z framework out-of-sample.
- Source: https://www.lcdcomps.com/lcd/n/article.html?rid=170&aid=12494200

### B.10 — December 2018 Powell pivot
- **Pre-pivot signals.**
  - HY OAS: ~315bp (Oct 3, 2018) → 540bp (Dec 26, 2018).
  - S&P 500 ~ -19.8% peak-to-trough (Sep 20 → Dec 24, 2018) — narrowly missed bear-market threshold.
  - VIX > 36 (Dec 24, 2018).
  - 2y-10y spread inverted briefly Dec 4, 2018 — first inversion since 2007.
- **The pivot.** Powell on Jan 4, 2019 ("patient" + "flexible on balance sheet"); July 2019 first cut.
- **Kill criterion that fired.** HY-OAS rate-of-change (Dec spike) + S&P drawdown were the signals that forced the policy reversal — a clear example of a market-stress threshold flipping the Fed reaction function.
- Source: https://www.cnbc.com/2018/12/19/fed-delivers-.html

## Section D — Pre-2020 criteria that FAILED (high false-positive rates or didn't fire when they should have)

### D.1 — Yield-curve signals
- **1966 inversion → no NBER recession.** Often cited as the single false positive of the Estrella–Mishkin signal. Some authors discount it because a "credit crunch" mini-recession occurred.
- **Pre-2020 critique on horizon.** While 3m–10y inversion was reliable for recession-presence, the *timing* error band has been ±6 months — not actionable for short-horizon equity allocation.

### D.2 — TED spread and LIBOR-based funding signals
- **TED spread mean-shift post-2010.** Post Dodd-Frank + Basel III, structurally lower interbank balances changed the TED spread baseline. Signals calibrated on pre-2010 means produced false alarms in 2011 (Eurozone) and 2018 (regulatory year-end).
- **LIBOR-OIS post-2018.** LIBOR transition to SOFR rendered LIBOR-OIS thresholds increasingly noisy — calibration broke before the formal LIBOR cessation.

### D.3 — Cooper–Priestley output-gap signal
- **Mixed out-of-sample evidence.** Subsequent studies (e.g., Welch–Goyal "Comprehensive Look" framework) have dismissed the equity-premium predictive power of the output gap on the basis of insignificant in-sample coefficient and poor out-of-sample performance. So while Cooper-Priestley reported strong in-sample R² for stock returns, it has not held up cleanly out-of-sample.

### D.4 — Credit spreads (HY-OAS) outside US recessions
- **2011 Eurozone-driven HY blowout** (~880bp peak in Oct 2011) did NOT produce a US recession. Pure threshold rules produced a false positive.
- **2015–16 oil/EM scare** (HY OAS to 887bp Feb 2016) — also no US NBER recession, but strong sub-recession growth slowdown.
- Implication: HY-OAS is a *necessary-but-not-sufficient* condition; needs to be combined with EBP or labor-market signals to filter false positives.

### D.5 — VIX level alone
- **VIX > 30 as a stop-out level.** Empirically, isolated VIX spikes (Aug 2011, Aug 2015, Feb 2018) had high false-positive rates for sustained equity drawdowns. The VIX *term-structure* signal (C9) outperformed VIX-level alone by ~40% lower false-positive rate.

### D.6 — Margin-debt thresholds
- **NYSE margin debt / GDP > 2%** repeatedly cited as a "kill criterion" for equity exposure pre-2020. Empirically the signal had multiple false positives (1999 a year before the peak; 2007 was timely; 2014 was 5+ years early). Lead-time variance makes the criterion non-actionable.

### D.7 — Currency-peg defenses
- **"Reserves > 6 months of imports" rule of thumb.** Mexico 1994, Thailand 1997, Russia 1998 all violated the criterion *late* — by which time the peg was already broken. Useful diagnostic, not actionable kill criterion.

### D.8 — Inverted-yield-curve "lead time"
- **2006–2007 inversion** preceded NBER recession by ~12–24 months — many practitioners who shorted on inversion were stopped out by the strong 2007 equity rally before the GFC drawdown. The criterion fired correctly but the *position-management* implications were treacherous.

### D.9 — Quantitative leading-indicator composites (e.g., LEI)
- **Conference Board LEI** had three soft false signals (~1995, ~2002 false re-fire, ~2011/12) where 6-month YoY change went negative without recession. Documented by FRED-aware practitioners; signal needed combining with credit/labor data.

## Section E — Pre-2020 vs post-2020 comparison hooks

These are the criteria most likely to need re-calibration when synthesizing with the post-2020 sister subagent's findings:

### E.1 — Yield-curve signal (C1, C2)
- **Pre-2020:** 7-of-7 with ~6–18mo lead.
- **Potential post-2020 break:** Massive Fed-balance-sheet distortion to back-end yields; 2022–24 inversion held longer than any historical episode without producing a deep recession (the "soft landing" debate). Synthesis question: *did QE structurally reduce term premium and break the curve signal, or just lengthen the lag?*

### E.2 — Sahm rule (C15)
- **Pre-2020:** ~1 false positive across 11 cycles (1959).
- **Post-2020 break:** Triggered in mid-2024 without recession. Sahm herself has publicly noted COVID-era labor-supply shocks may have invalidated the calibration. Critical break-point for synthesis.

### E.3 — TED spread / LIBOR-OIS (C5)
- **Pre-2020:** Cleanest single funding-stress indicator.
- **Post-2020 break:** LIBOR cessation; SOFR-OIS replacement has different statistical properties; SOFR is collateralized so the credit-risk component is different. Cross-currency basis (EUR/USD, JPY/USD) and the SRF/standing-repo facility usage now more informative.

### E.4 — HY OAS thresholds (C4)
- **Pre-2020:** 500bp = "stress"; 800bp = "near-recession"; 1,000bp+ = "crisis."
- **Potential post-2020 break:** Massive HY supply contraction (2017+) + ETF-based liquidity has compressed spreads; QE-era valuations have shifted thresholds. 2022 Fed-tightening saw HY OAS top out at ~600bp without recession.

### E.5 — Inflation regime (sticky vs flexible CPI)
- **Pre-2020:** Sticky-CPI persistently <2.5%; flexible-CPI noisy. Regime was "anchored disinflation."
- **Post-2020 break:** Sticky CPI > 6.5% peak in 2022 — a 40-year high. Pre-2020 calibration of "above 3% sticky CPI = serious" produced no signals 1995–2019; broke decisively in 2021. Foreshadows synthesis on whether the post-COVID inflation regime is a permanent break.

### E.6 — Adrian–Shin broker-dealer leverage (C10)
- **Pre-2020:** Strong predictor, especially for housing/durables.
- **Post-2020 break:** Bank-broker-dealer balance sheets are now structurally smaller (Volcker-era SLR + GSIB constraints); shadow-bank intermediation has migrated to hedge funds and private credit, where the data is poorer. Signal may have been hollowed out by regulation.

### E.7 — Dollar carry-trade signals (Brunnermeier-Nagel-Pedersen)
- **Pre-2020:** Carry-trade unwinds reliably accompanied EM crises (1998, 2008, 2015).
- **Post-2020 break:** Aug-2024 yen-carry unwind happened without a broader EM crisis, suggesting either (a) carry-trade leverage is now concentrated in fewer, bigger players (hedge funds/CTAs) or (b) the carry-trade-as-systemic-signal has weakened.

### E.8 — VIX term-structure inversion (C9)
- **Pre-2020:** Reliable concurrent panic indicator.
