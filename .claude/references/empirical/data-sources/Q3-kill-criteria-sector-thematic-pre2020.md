## Section B — Per-episode kill-criteria catalog

### B.1 Dot-com / TMT bust 2000–2002

**Setup (what was the bull thesis):** Internet adoption would drive infinite growth in networking, optical, server, B2C reach. Cisco was "the picks-and-shovels" pure play; "this time is different" multiples justified by future TAM.

**Specific signals that fired (and that practitioners cited):**

| Signal | Threshold / level | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Cisco P/E** | >200x at March 2000 peak | Harding Loevner |
| **Cisco EV/Sales** | ~31x at peak | Harding Loevner |
| **Median tech PS at peak** | ~17x; >18x identified ex post as "avoid" | maximizations.com analysis |
| **Telecom-equipment debt build** | >$500bn 1996–2001 capex financed by debt | MOI Global / Princeton (Starr) |
| **Capacity-utilization on long-haul fiber** | Fell to ~2–5% lit fraction post-bust | Princeton (Starr) |
| **Cisco inventory build (Q4 FY00 → Q1 FY01)** | Inventory grew much faster than revenue (writedown of ~$2.2bn announced Apr-2001) | Harding Loevner / TheStreet retrospective |
| **CAPE (S&P 500)** | Peaked at ~44 in March 2000 — historic record | Wikipedia/Shiller |
| **Iridium debt service / cash burn** | $40m/month interest on $5bn debt; Ch. 11 within 9 months of launch | Smithsonian/Iridium Museum |
| **Globalstar** | Filed Ch. 11 with $570m assets vs $3.3bn liabilities | Wikipedia |

**Practitioner kill-trigger heuristics (pre-2020, with this episode in mind):**
- "Stock at >18x sales is in the kill zone" — folklore that hardened after Sun Microsystems' McNealy 2002 quote: at 10x revenues, "to give you a 10-year payback I have to pay you 100% of revenues for 10 straight years in dividends... assumes I have zero costs of goods... no taxes..."
- Customer-concentration tripwire: when Cisco's top-10 telco customers represented >35% of order book and those telcos' capex guides turned negative simultaneously, the shift was unfalsifiable.
- Revenue mix shift to vendor financing: when >15% of new bookings depended on Cisco-/Lucent-financed buyers (rather than cash), the equity case died once the financing turned.

### B.2 Financials collapse 2007–2009

**Setup:** Housing-boom-financed bank earnings; structured-credit volume; "we have hedged this." Key thesis was diversification of risk via securitization.

**Signals that fired:**

| Signal | Threshold / level | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Investment-bank gross leverage** | 26–33x (2007 10-Ks); Lehman 31x; Bear ~33x | NBER (Ball) / Stanford Lehman docs |
| **ABX 2006-1 BBB- spread** | Started widening Nov-2006; >1,000 bp by Mar-2007 (vs ~150 bp at issue) | BIS WP 279 |
| **Subprime stated-income share (1Q–4Q 2007 originations)** | ~32% — peak "liar loan" share | Cleveland Fed |
| **Subprime origination volume** | $600bn (2006), 20% of originations vs 5% in 1994 | Cleveland Fed |
| **First-year delinquency, 2006/2007 vintages** | ~20% (vs ~10% for 2001–2005 vintages) | Cleveland Fed |
| **ABCP outstanding** | $1.18T (Aug-07) → $745B (Aug-08); >$350B run in last 5mo of 2007 | Federal Reserve |
| **Bear Stearns liquidity pool** | $18.1bn (Mar-10-08) → $2bn (Mar-13-08) — collapse in 3 days | FCIC final report |
| **Case-Shiller composite-20 YoY** | First negative print 2007; -18% YoY by late 2008 | Wikipedia / Brookings |
| **Lennar cancellation rate** | 33% (Q4 07), declining to 26% (Q1 08); industry peak ~47% | Calculated Risk |
| **Pulte cancellation rate** | 47% (Q4 08) | Calculated Risk |

**Practitioner-cited "fire" sequence:**
1. **Mortgage broker behavior change** (early 2006 — origination quality decay; broker-channel loans 50%+ delinquent in 12mo)
2. **ABX BBB- widening** (Q4 2006 → Q1 2007)
3. **Specific-name CDS spreads** (Bear hedge funds June 2007; Bear parent CDS Mar-2008)
4. **ABCP run** (Aug 2007)
5. **Money-fund break-the-buck** (Reserve Primary, Sep-2008)

### B.3 European banks 2010–2012

**Signals that fired:**

| Signal | Level | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Greek bank deposit outflows** | -€72bn (~30% of deposits) since 2010 | World Socialist Web Site / WB |
| **Spanish bank deposit run-fear (May 2012)** | €50bn (~2.9% of deposits) at risk | WSWS |

## Section C — Sector-by-sector validated pre-2020 kill criteria (consolidated)

### C.1 Tech / TMT
- EV/Sales >20x at scale ($10bn+ revenue) → multiple-compression scenario must be sized; >30x → scenario invalidated absent step-change rev growth >50%.
- Inventory growth >2× revenue growth for >2 quarters → demand-pull-in / channel-stuffing tripwire.
- Customer concentration: top-10 customers >35% AND those customers' capex guides turning negative within 3 months → invalidate equity case.
- Vendor financing >10% of bookings → reverse the leverage when financing tightens.

### C.2 Financials
- Bank gross leverage >25x (post-Basel I) → tail-risk regime; >30x → kill-zone.
- Sovereign holdings >100% of core capital → bank cannot be underwritten independently.
- Deposit outflows >5% within 30 days → solvency-presumption flips.
- Repo haircuts widening >2pp on Tier-1 collateral → liquidity-crisis confirmation.
- Loan-loss reserves /NPL ratio falling below 100% in expansion → forward credit-loss bomb.

### C.3 Energy / shale
- Sustained Brent below industry P50 break-even (e.g., $45 in 2015–16) for 6+ months → equity case for high-cost producers expires.
- HY energy default rate accelerating quarter-over-quarter from 2% baseline → contagion.
- OPEC public statement of market-share defense → priced regime shift; do not underwrite cuts.
- Shale rig count -50% from peak → 9–12 month production-decline lag confirms thesis.

### C.4 Retail
- SSS YoY <-3% for 4+ consecutive quarters + debt/EBITDA >5x → 3-yr bankruptcy probability materially elevated.
- Vendor-credit insurance withdrawal → 6-month liquidation window.
- Gross margin contraction >300 bp YoY without volume offset → strategic-failure signal.
- Online penetration in category passes ~25–30% (books, music, electronics first; apparel later) → traditional incumbents kill-zone.

### C.5 Biotech / pharma
- Phase III LoA: anchor scenarios on the Wong/BIO base rate (31–58%), not management guidance.
- Single-Phase-III asset-concentration (>50% of NPV in one program) AND no clear MoA validation in Ph2 → specific kill-trigger if an interim looks weak.
- Class-wide failure precedent (Alzheimer's, sepsis, ALS) → require >2× base-rate-PoS argument to fund.

### C.6 REITs / property
- P/NAV discount: <-15% historically marked stress lows (1999, 2009, 2013) — also the prior to anchor "buy zone" (mean-revert from -17%).
- Cap rate vs 10Y Treasury spread <100 bp → property over-valued; vulnerable.
- Rent-growth deceleration >300 bp YoY in primary market (multifamily/office/retail) → underwriting reset.

### C.7 Commodities / cyclicals / EM
- Producer FCF negative at spot prices for 4+ quarters → capex/dividend cuts incoming → equity case requires re-rating.
- China steel-demand growth <2% (vs 8–10% historical) → end of supercycle confirmed (2015 inflection).
- Mining capex /revenue >15% with falling spot price → write-down cycle imminent.

## Section D — Pre-2020 patterns that DIDN'T survive post-2020

### D.1 "EV/Sales >30 = kill-zone" — broken (or temporarily suspended) for AI-capex names
- Cisco's lesson: peak EV/Sales 31x → -88% drawdown in 24 months despite revenue continuing to grow.
- Post-2020 equivalent (NVDA, hyperscaler ecosystem) traded at similar multiples but also delivered 2-3x sustained revenue acceleration. The criterion-as-stated did not invalidate post-2020 names within the same window; multiple compression in NVDA-class names has not occurred (yet).
- **Open question / asymmetry:** Cisco's revenue topped at ~$22bn (FY01) before flat-lining. NVDA's revenue trajectory has continued to accelerate. Whether the rule "EV/Sales >30 ⇒ multiple compression in 12–24mo absent rev-growth step-change >50%" holds depends on the rev-growth step-change clause continuing to be satisfied. Once growth decelerates below 30% YoY at scale, the pre-2020 rule should fire.

### D.2 "Bank deposit run = kill" — re-set by 2023 SVB regional-bank crisis
- Pre-2020 deposit-run heuristics centered on retail flight measured in days/weeks (Northern Rock 2007, Greek banks 2010–12).
- 2023 SVB demonstrated digital-era runs unfold in hours — $42bn (~25% of deposits) attempted withdrawal in single day.
- **Implication:** the ~5%/30-day threshold from pre-2020 is too slow; modern threshold compressed by ~30x.

### D.3 "OPEC cut = price floor" — broken in 2020 Saudi-Russia price war
- 2014–16 episode taught practitioners that OPEC cuts come eventually after market-share campaigns.
- April 2020 negative-price WTI episode broke the "spot can't go below cash break-even" assumption baked into many shale-credit models.

### D.4 "Online penetration <25% means traditional retail safe" — accelerated post-2020
- Pre-2020 pattern: e-com inflection happened category-by-category; apparel/grocery were "safe" until ~2017–18.
- Post-2020 pulled forward 3–5 years of digital adoption in 6 months → multiple categories crossed thresholds simultaneously.
- Pre-2020 "category-by-category sequential disruption" forecasting framework no longer maps cleanly.

### D.5 "Phase III base rate = anchor" — NOT broken (still valid)
- The Wong/BIO base-rate framework (31–58% Phase III LoA) remains the empirical anchor post-2020.
- Approval-acceleration mechanisms (breakthrough designation, accelerated approval) have shifted timing, not raw efficacy probability.

### D.6 "CAPE >25 ⇒ low forward returns" — strained post-2020
- Pre-2020 Shiller framework: CAPE 44 (2000), 27 (2007), 32 (2018) all preceded weak forward returns.
- Post-2020 CAPE has spent extended time >30 with positive forward returns over 1–3y windows (rate environment, AI capex, fiscal); whether the long-run mean-reversion still holds at the 10y horizon is open.

### D.7 "Subprime stated-income share >30% = kill" — became unique-to-housing
- Pre-2020 the heuristic was nominally applicable to other consumer-credit verticals; post-2020 (auto, BNPL) the mechanics differ enough that the same threshold doesn't directly transfer.

## Section E — Cross-episode common patterns

Synthesis across 2000 / 2008 / 2014–16 / 2018–19 episodes. These are the pre-2020 patterns that fired most reliably:

### E.1 Capex-cycle peak signals (the five-pillar framework)
Across Cisco-2000, telecom-2001, energy-2014, mining-2014–15, and homebuilders-2006:
1. **Capex /revenue ratio >15%** (vs sub-industry historical median ~8–10%)
2. **Debt-financed share of capex >50%**
3. **Industry-wide order-book turning down while capex still rising** (i.e., 2-quarter divergence)
4. **Sector capacity-utilization >85% for 6+ quarters before peak**
5. **First-mover marginal producer announces project pause or cancellation**

When 4 of 5 fired simultaneously, sector-wide top within 6 months was the empirical pattern.

### E.2 Leverage / debt-service patterns
- **Industry-wide median net-debt/EBITDA >4x** at sector peak → distress in first downturn.
- **Interest coverage <3x** at sector peak → bankruptcy during downturn.
- **Maturity wall concentration** (>30% of sector debt maturing within 12 months in a stressed window) → forced refinancing → equity wipeout.

### E.3 Funding-market signals lead equity by 3–9 months
Across 2007–08 (ABCP, ABX, CDS), 2014–16 (HY energy spreads), 2018 (HYG/CCC):
- Funding-spread widening of 200+ bp from cycle-low → equity peak within 6 months.
- ABX-equivalent (sector-specific structured-credit benchmark) widening from issue spread by 5x → defining lead indicator.

### E.4 Customer / counterparty concentration
- **Top-10 customer concentration >35%** at sector peak → cliff risk.
- **Vendor-financing as % of bookings >10%** → pseudo-revenue.
- **Inter-bank /counterparty exposure** in financials → 2008 lesson: counterparty CDS spreads widening on names that share assets ⇒ contagion.

### E.5 Valuation rules-of-thumb that fired empirically
- **EV/Sales > 30 at scale** ⇒ multiple compression within 24 months (pre-2020; see D.1 for caveat).
- **Sector forward P/E > 2× S&P forward P/E** ⇒ relative re-rating risk.
- **CAPE > 30 + earnings-growth deceleration** ⇒ index-level downside.

### E.6 Behavioral / sentiment markers
- IPO calendar: $-volume of new tech IPOs >2× prior peak (1999, 2007 LBO IPOs, 2018 unicorns) → late cycle.
- Insider selling: cluster selling >10x normal at >5 names within sector within 60 days.
- Sell-side analyst-rating distribution: <5% sells ⇒ extreme positioning (typical at 2000, 2007, 2018 tops).

### E.7 The "narrative reset" pattern
Across episodes, the kill-criterion that practitioners reliably identified ex post:

> When the sector narrative pivots from "growth" to "this name is just the highest-quality of a still-growing sector" AND simultaneously the highest-quality name guides growth down ≥1,000 bp QoQ, the sector regime has changed. The practitioner discipline pre-2020: don't argue with the highest-quality name's guidance.

Examples: Cisco's April 2001 guide-down; Schlumberger Q4 2014 capex commentary; Lehman's CFO call on collateral June 2008.
