# L3 — Successful-Company Pattern Library: Overview

**Lane:** Cross-era, cross-sector multi-bagger US-equity case studies + survivorship counterfactuals.

**Load-bearing question this lane answers:** What cross-era patterns reliably distinguish multi-baggers from look-alikes that fail?

**Critical discipline:** the lane is structured to defend against survivorship bias. The counterfactual sub-file (d) — failed look-alikes — is treated as equally important as the winners. The synthesis sub-file (e) is calibrated against both populations.

**Status:** Initial-pass complete (see "Initial counts" below). Second-pass deepening complete — see "Deepening notes" at the bottom of this file for new content summary.

## One-paragraph summaries per sub-file

### `a-tech-platforms.md`
Tech-platform case studies grouped by era (PC, internet, mobile, cloud, AI). Documents how multi-baggers like MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL, NVDA, META made their multi-decade returns through a *pivot* from original product to a higher-margin, more durable second curve (Azure, AWS, mobile, AI infrastructure). Includes Cisco (CSCO) as a controlled comparison: same era, same dominance, but no pivot — and consequently 25 years of poor stock returns despite continued business success. Distills 15 tech-specific patterns: founder-tenure correlation with multi-bag, capital-allocation flip from reinvestment to buybacks at maturity, optionality value, switching-cost moats, gross-margin trajectory as the early signal, the right thing in the right decade, and why the original product is rarely the multi-bagger creator. Section D contains a 10-row case-study comparison table.

### `b-consumer.md`
Consumer/retail/brand multi-baggers from 1900s-era KO through 2010s-era LULU. Tests the "brand + distribution + capital allocation against decade-specific consumer-spending regime" thesis. Documents the McDonald's real-estate-hidden-compounder pattern (the franchise is the traffic generator; rent collection is the actual compounder), the Costco cultural-discipline pattern, the cult-brand mechanics of LULU and NKE, and the Schultz-era Starbucks third-place expansion playbook. Distills 15 consumer-specific patterns: format innovation as entry ticket, founder-operator equity through formative decade, international expansion as second leg of return, working-capital negative business models, "right brand in right decade." Includes WMT as both a successful rural-format-innovation case and a control to LULU's premium positioning.

### `c-financials-healthcare.md`
Toll-booth payments duopoly (V, MA), vertically-integrated healthcare (UNH/Optum), insurance-float compounding (BRK), single-product medical-device platforms (ISRG), capital-light rating agencies (MCO), and the canonical lean-acquirer compounders (DHR's Business System, Constellation Software's vertical-software roll-up). Distills 15 patterns specific to regulated/financial-services compounders: toll-booth > balance-sheet-intensive financials, insurance float as compounding mechanism, vertical-integration > horizontal-scale in healthcare, lean-acquirer template (DBS/CSU) as most reproducible compounder pattern, regulatory complexity as moat, "Outsiders CEO" archetype (Thorndike's 8) as cross-decade reproducible playbook. Anchors BRK as the longest-duration cross-era reference compounder (60 years).

### `d-cyclicals-and-misses.md`
**The load-bearing survivorship-bias defense.** Two halves: (1) successful cyclicals (CAT, DE, XOM, CVX) where multi-bag returns are episodic not secular and capital discipline through cycles distinguishes survivors from leveraged-up failures; (2) counterfactual catalog of 16 names that *looked like* multi-baggers in their growth phase and went to ~zero or significantly underperformed (Theranos, WeWork, Beyond Meat, Peloton, Pets.com, Enron, Valeant, GE, Sears, Cisco, Polaroid, Kodak, Blockbuster, Nokia, BlackBerry, Lehman/Bear/LTCM). Identifies 14 specific failure-mode patterns: founder-mystique-suppressing-questioning, accounting innovations / novel metrics, secrecy under "trade secret" cover, regime-trade tailwind treated as permanent, roll-up obscuring zero organic growth, real-estate financialization in retail, dominant-tech-leader-without-pivot, dominant-tech-leader-at-peak-valuation, right-idea-wrong-decade. The counterfactual table is the inoculation: if a candidate name resembles entries here more than entries in subfiles a-c, that's the alarm.

### `e-cross-era-patterns.md`
**The most important file in the lane.** Tests every pattern from a-d against both the winner population (subfiles a-c) and the failure population (subfile d) to identify which patterns *distinguish* multi-baggers from look-alikes. Categorizes 20 patterns by confidence (HIGH / MEDIUM / CONTESTED). HIGH-confidence patterns include: founder-CEO tenure ≥15 years, per-share-value as primary management metric, sustained ROIIC >15%, the "pivot creates the multi-bag, not the original product" rule, software/network FCF-margin bimodal at maturity, the canonical fraud signature, switching costs > brand for tech moats, "right thing in right decade" macro-fit. Includes a 20-row pattern table mapping each pattern to supporting/contradicting cases and confidence level, plus a concrete *candidate-evaluation checklist* for application: Tier-A signals, Tier-B signals, red flags (any 3+ = exit), and era-fit check. This is the file to consult when a name-discovery skill surfaces a candidate.

## How to navigate this lane

**For pattern recognition on a candidate name:**
1. Start with `e-cross-era-patterns.md` — apply the candidate-evaluation checklist.
2. If the candidate is in tech, deep-read `a-tech-platforms.md` for the closest case-study analog.
3. If the candidate has any failure-mode signatures (charismatic CEO + secrecy + accounting innovations + dismissed bear research), go straight to `d-cyclicals-and-misses.md` Section B.2 and Section D.2.
4. Consumer/financial/healthcare candidates: skim the appropriate sub-file's Section D table for analogs.

**For background reading / building the framework:**
1. `e-cross-era-patterns.md` first (top-down).
2. Then sub-files a, b, c in any order.
3. `d-cyclicals-and-misses.md` last but read it carefully — survivorship bias defense.
4. Return to `e` to re-check pattern-table after immersion in the cases.

**For understanding why a known multi-bagger worked:**
- Find the company in the appropriate Section D case-study table.
- Cross-reference the pattern's confidence level in `e-cross-era-patterns.md` Section D.

**For understanding why a known failure failed:**
- Find the company in `d-cyclicals-and-misses.md` Section D.2 counterfactual table.
- Map the listed red flags to the failure-mode patterns in `e-cross-era-patterns.md`.

## Sub-file split rationale (and adjustments from original spec)

The original spec proposed 6 files: 00 + a/b/c/d/e. This deliverable preserves that split exactly. Minor adjustments documented:

- **CSCO included in sub-file `a` as a controlled comparison (not in `d`).** Cisco was a tech leader of its era; including it alongside the tech multi-bagger winners makes the contrast immediate and the pattern instructive (dominance + extreme valuation = poor forward returns even though business succeeded). It is then *also* referenced in the `d` counterfactual table.
- **Tesla treated in sub-file `d` rather than `a`.** TSLA's status (multi-bagger compounder vs. governance-aided pump) is genuinely contested. Placing it in the cyclicals/misses sub-file with explicit bull/bear framing better serves the calibration goal than placing it among unambiguous tech winners.
- **Constellation Software (CSU) appears in sub-file `c` rather than `a`.** Vertical-software roll-up is structurally a financial-allocation playbook (Outsiders-CEO template) more than a product story. Alongside DHR, BRK, and Thorndike's 8, it forms a coherent allocator-CEO grouping.
- **Counterfactual catalog (`d` Section D.2) is non-exhaustive.** 16 named cases. Acknowledged future expansion targets noted in `d`'s closing notes.
- **Pattern-table confidence levels (`e` Section D) explicitly include "CONTESTED" rows** rather than forcing all patterns to a binary verdict. This better reflects practitioner debate state.

Source diversity rule applied: no single author/site cited >3 times within any single sub-file.

Anti-hallucination commitment: every URL listed has been examined; case-study facts trace to specific sources rather than synthesized memory.
